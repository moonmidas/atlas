# HVAC Consolidation Rollup

**Category:** Home Services Rollups  
**Sub-Category:** HVAC/Mechanical  
**Entry Cost:** $500K-$2M (first acquisition)  
**Timeline:** 6-18 months to platform  
**Business Model:** Buy + improve + compound

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Buy HVAC companies ($1M-$5M revenue), apply operational excellence, build regional platform. Highly fragmented market ($120B) with top 10 players holding <5% share. Aging owners retiring without succession plans.

**Market Size:** $120B HVAC market, 100K+ companies  
**Target:** $2M-$8M revenue, 10-50 employees, 20+ year old companies

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile (Acquisition Targets)

**Seller Profile:** Owner-operator, 55-70 years old
**Revenue:** $2M-$8M annually
**EBITDA:** $200K-$800K (10-15% margin)
**Motivation:** Retirement, no succession, burned out

**Why They Sell:**
- Kids don't want the business
- Tired of 60-hour weeks
- No management team in place
- Health issues
- Want to diversify wealth

## 🔴 Top 15 Value Creation Opportunities

### 1. Pricing Optimization ⭐ HIGHEST IMPACT
**Current State:** HVAC companies underprice by 20-30%
**Why:** "That's what we've always charged"
**Opportunity:** Implement flat-rate pricing, margin analysis
**Value Creation:** 15-25% revenue increase without more volume
**Timeline:** Month 1-3

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you set your pricing?"
- "When was the last time you raised rates?"
- "What's your gross margin on service calls?"

---

### 2. Maintenance Agreement Program
**Current State:** <20% of customers on maintenance plans
**Why:** Never built systematic program
**Opportunity:** Membership model with recurring revenue
**Value Creation:** 30-50% increase in customer LTV
**Timeline:** Month 2-6

---

### 3. Shared Services (Back Office)
**Current State:** Each location has own bookkeeper, admin
**Why:** Owner-operators, no centralization
**Opportunity:** Centralized accounting, HR, dispatch
**Value Creation:** $100K-$300K annual savings per location
**Timeline:** Month 3-12

---

### 4. Technology Platform
**Current State:** Paper invoices, Excel dispatch, no CRM
**Why:** Owners not tech-savvy, "works fine"
**Opportunity:** ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro implementation
**Value Creation:** 20-30% efficiency gains
**Timeline:** Month 3-9

---

### 5. Marketing Centralization

**Value Creation:** 30-50% increase in leads

### 6. Technician Training Program

## 🎯 Deal Sourcing Strategy

### Channels
1. **Industry brokers** - 50% of deals
2. **Direct outreach** - 30% of deals
3. **Accountants/attorneys** - 15% of deals
4. **Associations** - 5% of deals

### What to Look For
- Owner 55+ years old
- No family succession plan
- Declining revenue (fixable issues)
- 10+ year history
- 10-50 employees
- Service area <30 mile radius

### Red Flags
- Owner wants to stay involved long-term
- Key man risk (owner is only salesperson)
- Customer concentration (>30% from one source)
- Declining reputation (Google reviews <4.0)
- Environmental liabilities (refrigerant handling issues)

## 💬 Seller Conversation

**Opening:**
> "I'm curious—have you thought about what happens to the business when you're ready to retire?"

**Pain Amplification:**
> "Most owners I talk to are working 60-hour weeks and feel stuck. They want to retire but don't have a succession plan. Is that your situation?"

**Value Proposition:**
> "We buy HVAC companies and keep the legacy going—your employees stay, your brand stays (or joins ours), and you get to retire with a check. Plus we handle the transition so your customers never know anything changed."

**Timeline:**
> "These conversations usually take 6-12 months from first talk to closing. No pressure—just want to see if there's a fit."

- [ ] 3 years financial statements (tax returns)
