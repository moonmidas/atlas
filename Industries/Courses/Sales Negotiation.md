# Sales Negotiation

**Category:** Courses  
**Sub-Category:** Cohort-Based Programs  
**Entry Cost:** $800-$2,500  
**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** Sales performance cohort with live call review, negotiation drills, and team training upsells

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Sales negotiation cohorts help professionals close more revenue with stronger conversations, sharper positioning, and better control in buying dynamics. The strongest promises are measurable: higher conversion, better close rates, improved deal size, or stronger negotiation outcomes.

This category is powerful because the ROI is immediate and easy to understand. Buyers often pay quickly when one conversation can repay the program many times over.

**Market Size:** Large sales enablement, training, and negotiation education market across B2B and services  
**Target:** Founders, account executives, consultants, closers, and agency owners  
**Examples:** B2B closing cohorts, negotiation labs, consultative selling bootcamps

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Buyers wanting manipulative tactics
- ❌ Reps unwilling to review their own calls
- ❌ Teams with no sales process at all and no implementation support
- ❌ Buyers who think price alone causes losses
- ❌ Prospects avoiding practice or role-play

**Best Prospects:**
- Revenue responsibility is direct and visible
- Calls are happening consistently
- Willingness to get feedback
- Offer already has some market demand
- High motivation to improve conversion or deal quality

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"I already know the basics of sales."**
> "That is often true. The issue is whether those basics are showing up under live pressure in your actual calls. That is what the cohort improves."

**"I just need more leads, not training."**
> "More leads can help, but if conversion is underperforming you may only be pouring more volume into a leaky process."

**"Negotiation is not really part of my role."**
> "Any pricing, timing, scope, or next-step discussion involves negotiation. Most people are doing it already, just without a framework."

**"I do not want to sound scripted."**
> "A good framework gives you structure, not robotic language. The goal is more natural confidence, not memorized lines."

**"The price feels expensive."**
> "If the program helps you save one deal, improve one close rate band, or stop unnecessary discounting, it usually pays for itself very quickly."

**"I am nervous about call review."**
> "That discomfort is normal, and it is often where the fastest growth lives. Reviewing real calls shortens the feedback loop dramatically."
