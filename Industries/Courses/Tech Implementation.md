# Tech Implementation

**Category:** Courses  
**Sub-Category:** Cohort-Based Programs  
**Entry Cost:** $500-$2,000  
**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** Implementation cohort for tools and systems with templates, setup support, and premium done-with-you upgrades

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Tech implementation cohorts help buyers install and adopt working systems such as Notion workspaces, automations, CRMs, websites, dashboards, or internal tools. The strongest promise is not education for its own sake. It is a deployed, usable system by the end of the cohort.

This category is compelling because many buyers have already bought software and still are not using it effectively. They pay for simplification, setup, and adoption, not just instruction.

**Market Size:** Large SaaS enablement, no-code implementation, and SMB systems training market  
**Target:** Small business owners, operators, consultants, creators, and team leads  
**Examples:** Notion setup cohorts, automation bootcamps, CRM implementation programs, dashboard build labs

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"Turn paid-for software and half-built systems into working infrastructure your team actually uses."

### Authority Builders
1. Tool implementation audit
2. System simplification checklist
3. Automation opportunity map
4. Before-and-after workspace teardowns

### Positioning Angles
- **Implementation Over Education:** Sell deployment, not awareness.
- **Tool ROI:** Emphasize getting value from software already purchased.
- **Adoption:** Include people and process, not just setup steps.

### Differentiators
- Practical done-with-you feel at cohort economics
- Strong blend of setup and adoption support
- Great fit for SMBs and operators without enterprise budgets
- Built around real workflows instead of generic demos

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Buyers wanting a fully custom consulting engagement
- ❌ Teams unwilling to standardize process first
- ❌ Businesses chasing tools without clarity on the use case
- ❌ Buyers who will not assign an owner
- ❌ Projects too large for a cohort implementation model

**Best Prospects:**
- Already paying for tools they underuse
- Clear workflow pain worth solving now
- Small teams willing to adopt shared systems
- Operators who want practical templates
- Buyers wanting a faster path than DIY and cheaper than consulting

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"I can just watch tutorials."**
> "Tutorials can teach features, but they rarely get your exact workflow implemented cleanly and adopted by your team."

**"We may need a consultant instead."**
> "Sometimes that is true for larger projects. But many teams are overbuying consulting for systems that can be implemented well with a structured cohort and light support."

**"My team will not use it anyway."**
> "That is why the offer includes adoption planning, ownership, and operating rituals. Implementation without behavior change is not enough."

**"I do not have time to build this now."**
> "That usually means the current system is already costing time every week. A focused build can remove recurring waste quickly."

**"The price feels high."**
> "Compared with unused software, manual labor, or one consulting engagement, the investment is usually modest for a system that actually goes live and gets used."

**"What if our needs are too specific?"**
> "A good cohort works best when built around a common implementation pattern with room for guided adaptation. We can qualify whether your use case fits before enrollment."
