## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"The career advice your parents gave you is costing you $20K/year. Here's what actually works in 2024."

### Authority Builders
1. Salary transparency database (crowdsourced)
2. Success stories (before/after promotions, raises)
3. Recruiter interviews (insider secrets)
4. Resume teardowns (anonymized real examples)

### Positioning Angles
- **Data-driven:** "Based on 10,000 salary submissions"
- **Insider:** "What recruiters won't tell you"
- **Practical:** "Scripts you can use today"

### Trust Signals
- Your own career success story
- Testimonials with photos/titles
- Transparent about what worked/didn't

---

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Get-rich-quick promises
- ❌ No real-world experience (never had a job)
- ❌ Generic advice ("just network more")
- ❌ Outdated advice (pre-2020 job market)
- ❌ MLM/crypto hidden in side hustle content

**Best Positioning:**
- Specific (one career stage or industry)
- Evidence-based (data over anecdotes)
- Actionable (templates, scripts, tools)

---

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"Career advice is all the same"**
> "Most advice is recycled from the 90s. I focus on [remote work/tech salaries/negotiation] with tactics that worked for [specific number] people last year."

**"I can't afford career coaching"**
> "Most of my content is free—the newsletter, the salary database, the community. Premium is for people who want [specific outcome] faster."

**"My situation is unique"**
> "Every career feels unique, but the patterns are universal. I've helped people in [industry 1], [industry 2], and [industry 3] with the same framework."

**"I've tried courses before and they didn't work"**
> "Most courses give you information, not transformation. My approach includes [accountability/community/1:1 support] because implementation is where people get stuck."

## 📞 Discovery Questions

**To Job Seekers:**
- "What's your biggest frustration with your current job search?"

- "Why do you want to leave your current field?"
- "What would your ideal workday look like?"
