# Inventory Supply Chain Automation

**Category:** Automation Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Inventory Control and Supply Chain Workflows  
**Entry Cost:** $4K-$12K  
**Timeline:** 2-6 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** $10K-$35K implementation projects plus $2K-$6K/month supply chain optimization retainers

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Inventory Supply Chain Automation agencies help businesses reduce the manual work around stock visibility, purchase planning, supplier communication, reordering, and exception management. The work often includes inventory sync, demand alerts, reorder thresholds, supplier scorecards, receiving workflows, and integrations between ERP, WMS, storefront, and purchasing systems.

This category is attractive because stockouts and overstock have direct financial consequences. A company does not need massive scale to feel the pain. Once multiple locations, warehouses, suppliers, or channels are involved, manual inventory coordination becomes expensive fast.

**Market Size:** Large market across wholesale, manufacturing, DTC, retail, distribution, and multi-location operations  
**Target:** Businesses with SKU complexity, supplier dependencies, or cross-channel inventory visibility issues  
**Examples:** ERP implementation boutiques, inventory planning agencies, WMS integration firms, procurement automation specialists

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Reorders happen too late ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Teams discover low-stock situations only when inventory is already critical.  
**Current Solution:** Spreadsheet checks and planner intuition.  
**Cost of Pain:** Stockouts, expedited freight, and lost sales.  
**Your Solution:** Reorder thresholds, lead-time-aware alerts, and purchasing triggers.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$18K foundation build.

**Discovery Questions:**
- How are reorder decisions made today?
- Which SKUs most often run low before the team reacts?

---

### 2. Inventory counts differ by system
**Pain:** ERP, WMS, retail, and ecommerce systems disagree on actual stock.  
**Current Solution:** Manual reconciliation and urgent adjustments.

**Your Solution:** Sync workflows, variance checks, and reconciliation alerts.

**Discovery Questions:**

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Very low-SKU businesses with minimal inventory complexity
- ❌ Buyers expecting advanced demand science beyond workflow scope
- ❌ Teams with no owner for purchasing or inventory discipline
- ❌ Prospects needing custom supply chain software development
- ❌ Businesses with highly unreliable base data and no willingness to improve it

**Best Prospects:**
- Operators dealing with multi-channel, multi-location, or supplier complexity
- Teams feeling the cost of both stockouts and overstock
- Businesses where planners are overwhelmed by repetitive manual monitoring

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"Inventory will always be part art, not just systems."**
> "That is true. The goal is not to remove judgment. It is to stop wasting judgment on repetitive monitoring and make the important decisions better informed."

**"We already have an ERP/WMS."**
> "That helps, but many teams still run planning and exceptions through spreadsheets because the workflow layer is incomplete."

**"Our data quality is not good enough yet."**
> "Then the right scope starts with the highest-value visibility and exception controls while improving the data habits that matter most."

**"This sounds expensive for operations plumbing."**
> "Stockouts, overstock, and rushed freight tend to be far more expensive than the project, especially when they recur."

**"We can just hire another planner."**
> "That may help temporarily, but adding headcount into a reactive process usually preserves the same bottlenecks."

**"Supply chain is too volatile to automate."**
> "Volatility makes alerts, thresholds, and exception workflows more valuable, not less."
