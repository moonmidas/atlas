# Data Reporting Automation

**Category:** Automation Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Business Intelligence and Operational Reporting  
**Entry Cost:** $4K-$12K  
**Timeline:** 2-6 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** $10K-$30K implementation projects plus $2K-$6K/month analytics support retainers

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Data Reporting Automation agencies help businesses replace spreadsheet-heavy reporting with reliable dashboards, pipelines, alerts, and executive visibility. The work often includes metric definitions, source consolidation, transformations, dashboard design, scheduled reporting, anomaly alerts, and data delivery into BI tools or operational systems.

This category is attractive because companies already know reporting is painful. Leaders wait too long for answers, analysts spend time producing reports instead of interpreting them, and teams lose trust when the same metric shows different values across tools.

**Market Size:** Large market across SaaS, e-commerce, agencies, finance, operations teams, and any business with multi-system data sprawl  
**Target:** Companies with recurring reporting needs, multiple source systems, and delayed or mistrusted analytics  
**Examples:** Power BI agencies, Looker and Tableau consultants, RevOps analytics firms, Airtable and dashboard automation shops

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Reports are still built manually in spreadsheets ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Analysts or operators export and stitch data together every week or month.  
**Current Solution:** Spreadsheet rollups and copy-paste reporting.  
**Cost of Pain:** Hours lost, delayed decisions, and high error risk.  
**Your Solution:** Automated pipelines, recurring dashboards, and scheduled deliveries.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$18K foundation build.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which reports still require manual exports every period?
- How many hours are spent producing recurring reporting today?

---

### 2. Metrics are defined differently across teams
**Pain:** Revenue, churn, pipeline, CAC, or margin mean different things in different reports.  
**Current Solution:** Each team uses its own logic.  
**Cost of Pain:** Confusion, argument, and slow decisions.  
**Your Solution:** Metric governance, documentation, and centralized reporting logic.  
**Pricing:** $4K-$10K.

**Discovery Questions:**

- Where do reports disagree because definitions are inconsistent?

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Teams with too little reporting need or too little data volume
- ❌ Buyers wanting advanced data science instead of practical reporting automation
- ❌ Companies with no internal owner for metrics or dashboard usage
- ❌ Prospects expecting perfect source data without cleanup work
- ❌ Projects requiring enterprise-grade data warehouse transformation far beyond scope

**Best Prospects:**
- Leadership teams frustrated by stale, manual, or conflicting reporting
- Ops-heavy businesses with repeated KPI reviews and spreadsheet dependence
- Organizations ready to standardize metric definitions across functions

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We already have dashboards."**
> "Many companies do. The real question is whether people trust them, use them, and get answers fast enough to act."

**"Our data is too messy for this."**
> "Messy data is often exactly why a reporting automation project is useful, as long as the scope starts with high-value metrics and realistic cleanup."

**"This sounds like a data team problem."**
> "Sometimes. But many businesses need a practical reporting layer long before they need a full internal analytics team."

**"We can hire an analyst instead."**
> "That hire often ends up producing the same manual reports unless the reporting system itself becomes more automated and trustworthy."

**"Leadership changes what they want every month."**
> "That is common. A good setup separates stable metric logic from flexible presentation so reporting can evolve without constant rebuilds."

**"We do not need fancy BI."**
> "Good. The value is not fancy dashboards. It is reliable answers, fewer manual reports, and faster decisions."
