## The Opportunity

Specialized software engineering roles represent the highest-demand, hardest-to-fill segment of tech recruiting.

**Market Size:** $12B annually  
**Target:** Tech companies building AI/ML, security, platform capabilities  
**Examples:** Hired, Triplebyte, A-List

## Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Generalist Recruiters Submit Unqualified Candidates (TOP PAIN)
**Pain:** Non-technical recruiters cannot distinguish between different engineering specializations
**Current Solution:** Engineering team reviews all resumes, reject 90% of submissions
**Cost of Pain:** 10-20 hours per week of engineering time on screening
**Your Solution:** Technical pre-screening, domain-specific evaluation
**Pricing:** $25K-$45K per placement

**Discovery Questions:**
- What percentage of recruiter candidates make it to final rounds?
- How much engineering time is spent on screening each week?
- What is the most common mismatch you see?

---

## Objection Handling

**"We use big recruiting platforms."**
Platforms give volume, not quality. For every qualified candidate they send, you review 50 unqualified. We send 3-5 pre-vetted candidates.

**"Specialized engineers are too expensive."**
They are expensive because they are scarce and valuable. A good ML Engineer can generate millions in revenue. What is the ROI you are looking for?
