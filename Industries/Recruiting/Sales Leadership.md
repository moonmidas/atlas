## 🎯 The Opportunity

Sales leadership hiring is mission-critical for revenue-generating companies, yet it's where companies make the most expensive mistakes. The wrong VP Sales or CRO can tank a quarter, destroy sales culture, and set growth back 12-18 months. The right hire can double revenue in the same timeframe.

Growth-stage B2B SaaS companies (Series A-C, $5M-$50M ARR) face a specific challenge: their founder-led sales process no longer scales, but they lack the experience to hire sales leaders who can build repeatable, scalable sales machines. They need leaders who have taken companies from founder-led sales to $50M+ ARR, built sales teams of 20-100+, and implemented the processes (forecasting, compensation, territory planning) that professional sales organizations require.

**Market Size:** $3.8B annually (sales leadership search)  
**Target:** B2B SaaS companies, tech-enabled services, enterprise software  
**Examples:** SaaStr (community), Peak Sales Recruiting, HireBetter

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Founder-Led Sales Cannot Scale ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** The founder has been the primary salesperson, but they cannot scale to close larger deals while also running the company. Revenue plateaus or grows linearly with founder capacity.
**Current Solution:** Hire junior sales reps and hope they figure it out, delay hiring sales leadership to save money, founder continues burning out on sales
**Cost of Pain:** Revenue plateau, founder burnout, missed market opportunities, inability to raise next round due to growth concerns
**Your Solution:** Sales leadership with scaling track record, founder transition support, sales process implementation
**Pricing:** $30K-$60K with transition consulting

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How much of your revenue still comes from founder-led deals?"
- "What's preventing you from stepping back from sales to focus on the business?"
- "How is your current sales team performing without experienced leadership?"

---

### 2. Wrong Sales Leader Destroys Culture
**Pain:** Sales leaders from large companies bring enterprise processes that crush startup culture, or leaders from wrong industries cannot adapt to the company's market dynamics.
**Current Solution:** Hire based on impressive company logos, hope they adapt, replace after 12-18 months when culture is damaged
**Cost of Pain:** Sales team turnover, damaged customer relationships, quarter(s) of missed targets, $200K-$400K in replacement costs
**Your Solution:** Culture-fit assessment, startup-stage experience verification, reference checking specifically for cultural adaptability
**Pricing:** $30K-$65K with culture guarantee

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How would you describe your ideal sales culture?"
- "Tell me about a sales leader who didn't work out—what went wrong?"
- "What concerns do you have about bringing in sales leadership from outside your industry?"

---

### 3. Cannot Differentiate Great Salespeople from Great Sales Leaders
**Pain:** Companies hire top individual contributors expecting them to lead, only to discover that closing deals doesn't equate to building teams, processes, and strategy.
**Current Solution:** Promote top performers, provide minimal leadership training, hope individual success translates to team success
**Cost of Pain:** Failed leadership, lost top performer in IC role, team underperformance, need to hire replacement leader
**Your Solution:** Leadership competency assessment, management track record verification, team-building experience evaluation
**Pricing:** $30K-$60K with leadership assessment

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you evaluate whether a great salesperson will be a great sales leader?"
- "What's your experience promoting top performers into management?"
- "What specific leadership behaviors do you want to see from sales leadership?"

---

### 4. Sales Process Non-Existent or Broken
**Pain:** Sales teams operate without standardized processes—no consistent qualification criteria, forecasting is guesswork, deals fall through cracks.
**Current Solution:** Ad-hoc processes that vary by rep, spreadsheet CRM, hope that talent overcomes process gaps
**Cost of Pain:** Unpredictable revenue, deals lost to competitors with better follow-through, inability to scale sales team
**Your Solution:** Sales process expertise as hiring criteria, CRM implementation support, sales methodology consulting
**Pricing:** $35K-$70K with process advisory

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Walk me through your current sales process. Is it documented?"
- "How do you currently forecast revenue?"
- "What sales processes do you want a new leader to implement?"

---

### 5. Compensation Plans Create Misalignment
**Pain:** Sales compensation plans are misaligned with company goals, creating perverse incentives (e.g., reps closing unprofitable deals, focusing on easy wins vs. strategic accounts).
**Current Solution:** Copy comp plans from other companies, adjust reactively when problems arise, underpay and lose talent
**Cost of Pain:** Unprofitable revenue, wrong customer mix, sales team turnover, internal conflict over fairness
**Your Solution:** Compensation strategy advisory, plan design support, sales economics optimization
**Pricing:** $30K-$60K with compensation consulting

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How satisfied are you with your current sales compensation plan?"
- "What behaviors is your comp plan incentivizing? Are those the right behaviors?"
- "What percentage of your sales team is hitting quota?"

**Current Solution:** Hire external recruiters for every role, accept that leaders aren't recruiters, pay high fees for every sales hire
**Cost of Pain:** $15K-$30K per sales hire in external fees, slow team building, dependency on external resources
**Your Solution:** Recruiting capability assessment, network evaluation, hiring track record verification
**Pricing:** $30K-$65K with recruiting assessment

**Discovery Questions:**

- "What experience do you want them to have with recruiting sales talent?"
- "How have previous sales leaders performed at team building?"

### 9. Sales Leaders Cannot Adapt to Company's Market
**Pain:** Sales leaders from different markets (enterprise vs. SMB, product-led vs. sales-led) cannot adapt their playbooks to the company's specific go-to-market model.
**Current Solution:** Assume sales skills transfer, provide minimal market training, accept that leaders need time to learn (time you don't have)
**Cost of Pain:** 6-12 months of poor performance while leader learns market, potential strategy pivots that confuse team
**Your Solution:** Go-to-market model experience verification, adaptability assessment, domain expertise evaluation
**Pricing:** $35K-$70K with market-fit assessment

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your go-to-market model? Product-led, sales-led, or hybrid?"
- "What market dynamics should a sales leader understand on day one?"
- "How comfortable are you with sales leaders learning your market on the job?"

**Your Solution:** Territory design expertise, quota-setting methodology, fairness framework development
**Pricing:** $30K-$65K with territory advisory

**Discovery Questions:**

- "What complaints do you hear about territory fairness?"
- "How do you set quotas, and what percentage of reps are hitting them?"

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"We place sales leaders who have scaled revenue from founder-led to $50M+—and know how to build the sales machine you need."

### Authority Builders
1. **Sales Leadership Compensation Report:** Quarterly data on CRO/VP Sales compensation by stage and vertical
2. **Sales Scaling Playbook:** Guide for building sales teams at different revenue stages
3. **Sales Process Audit Tool:** Assessment framework for evaluating sales organization readiness
4. **Sales Leader Success Framework:** Methodology for predicting sales leader success at growth-stage companies

### Positioning Angles
- **The Revenue Focus Angle:** "Sales leadership mistakes cost quarters. Our placements have an average tenure of 3.2 years versus industry average of 18 months."
- **The Stage Expertise Angle:** "Big company sales leaders often fail at startups. We only place leaders with $5M-$100M ARR scaling experience."
- **The Performance Angle:** "Our sales leaders average 140% of quota in year one. We don't just fill roles—we place performers."

### Differentiators
- Deep network of 3,000+ sales leaders with growth-stage scaling experience
- Proprietary assessment for sales leadership competency (not just past performance)
- Compensation strategy advisory included in every search
- Sales process implementation support for placed leaders
- 12-month performance guarantee

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Companies without product-market fit (sales cannot fix this)
- ❌ Unrealistic quota expectations or compensation constraints
- ❌ Founders unwilling to delegate sales authority
- ❌ Companies with recurring sales leadership turnover (systemic issues)
- ❌ Organizations that view sales as "necessary evil" rather than growth driver

**Best Prospects:**
- Series A-C B2B SaaS with $5M-$50M ARR
- Companies transitioning from founder-led to professional sales
- Organizations preparing for rapid scale (Series B/C, international expansion)
- Companies with clear ICP and some repeatable sales process
- CEOs who understand sales is strategic, not just tactical

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"Salespeople are easy to find."**
> "Salespeople, yes. Sales leaders who can build teams, implement processes, and scale revenue predictably are rare. The best ones aren't on job boards—they're happy in their current roles and only move for the right opportunity. That's our network."

**"We've been burned by bad sales leaders before."**
> "That's unfortunately common. Most sales leader failures aren't about capability—they're about fit. We assess not just sales skills but cultural fit, stage experience, and leadership style. Our 92% 2-year retention rate comes from getting fit right."

**"We need someone who can sell, not just manage."**
> "Early-stage, that makes sense. But as you scale, you need someone who can build the machine, not just run it. We'll help you clarify whether you need a player-coach (early) or a pure leader (growth). Many clients find that clarity is valuable before we even start sourcing."

**"Can you guarantee they'll hit their numbers?"**
> "No one can guarantee performance, but we can guarantee fit and capability. We assess whether candidates have hit similar numbers in similar situations. Our placements average 140% of plan in year one—not because we're lucky, but because we place leaders who've done it before."

**"Your fees seem high for sales roles."**
> "A bad sales leader costs you a quarter—sometimes a year—of revenue growth. Our fee is 1-2 months of their compensation but saves you from the $200K+ cost of a failed hire. What's the cost of missing your number this year?"
