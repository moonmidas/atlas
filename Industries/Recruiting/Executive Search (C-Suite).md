# Template: Executive Search (C-Suite)

**Category:** Recruiting Firms  
**Sub-Category:** Executive Search  
**Entry Cost:** $15K-$50K  
**Timeline:** 3-6 months  
**Revenue Model:** Retainer (33% upfront, 33% mid-search, 34% on placement) + placement fee ($50K-$150K)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Executive search for C-Suite positions represents the highest-margin segment of recruiting. Growth-stage companies (Series B-D, $20M-$200M ARR) face a critical inflection point where their founding leadership lacks the experience to scale, yet they cannot afford the months-long process of traditional retained search firms. These companies need C-Suite leaders who have successfully scaled through similar revenue milestones—CEOs who have taken companies from $50M to $200M, CFOs who have navigated IPO preparation, COOs who have built scalable operations systems.

The opportunity lies in offering boutique executive search with faster time-to-fill (45-60 days vs 90-120 days from large firms) at premium fees justified by deep specialization in specific growth-stage scenarios. Unlike generalist recruiters, you bring C-Suite networks, board-level credibility, and the ability to assess executives beyond resume qualifications—evaluating their actual scaling experience.

**Market Size:** $15B annually (US executive search)  
**Target:** Growth-stage tech companies (Series B-D), PE-backed portfolio companies, family offices  
**Examples:** Odgers Berndtson, Korn Ferry, Russell Reynolds (big firms); boutique winners like Bolster, Handel Group

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** CEO / Board Chair  
**Secondary:** Private Equity Operating Partner, Chief People Officer  
**Decision Maker:** CEO has budget authority for C-Suite hires; PE Operating Partners drive decisions for portfolio companies

**Budget:** $50K-$150K + equity considerations for candidates  
**Pain Urgency:** Critical - leadership gaps directly impact valuation, fundraising, and operational execution

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Wrong C-Suite Hire Destroys Company Value ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** A bad executive hire costs 15-27x their annual salary in lost productivity, team disruption, and opportunity cost. For growth-stage companies, one wrong C-Suite hire can derail a funding round or acquisition.
**Current Solution:** Rely on personal networks, traditional retained search (6-9 month process), or promote from within despite lack of scaling experience
**Cost of Pain:** $2M-$5M+ in direct costs, 6-12 months of delayed growth, potential loss of investor confidence
**Your Solution:** Rigorous assessment framework evaluating 50+ scaling-specific competencies, reference verification with former boards/CEOs, 12-month placement guarantee
**Pricing:** $75K-$125K retainer + success fee

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's the cost to your company if this role remains unfilled for another 6 months?"
- "Tell me about your last C-Suite hire—what would you do differently?"
- "How is your board/investors evaluating your leadership team for the next growth phase?"

---

### 2. Cannot Access Passive C-Suite Candidates
**Pain:** The best executives aren't applying to job postings or responding to LinkedIn outreach. They're gainfully employed and only move for the right opportunity presented through trusted channels.
**Current Solution:** Post on job boards (ineffective), cold outreach via LinkedIn (ignored), rely on board connections (limited reach)
**Cost of Pain:** 3-6 months of extended search, settling for available candidates rather than best-fit candidates
**Your Solution:** Dedicated C-Suite talent researcher, proprietary database of 5,000+ growth-stage executives, warm introduction network through board relationships
**Pricing:** $50K-$100K depending on role seniority

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many qualified C-Suite candidates have you spoken with in the past 30 days?"
- "What's your current approach to reaching executives who aren't actively looking?"
- "Who in your network could make warm introductions to your ideal candidates?"

---

### 3. Lack Assessment Rigor for Executive Fit
**Pain:** Traditional interviews fail to predict executive performance. Founders promote based on tenure or hire based on impressive resumes from brand-name companies, only to discover the executive cannot operate in a growth-stage environment.
**Current Solution:** Standard interview loops, reference checks with provided contacts (biased), gut instinct
**Cost of Pain:** $500K-$2M in compensation, severance, and replacement costs; 12-18 months of operational disruption
**Your Solution:** Multi-dimensional assessment including psychometric testing, scenario-based simulations, 360-degree reference verification, board chemistry evaluation
**Pricing:** $80K-$150K with comprehensive assessment package

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What assessment methods have you used for executive hires, and how accurate were they?"
- "How do you evaluate whether an executive from a large company can succeed in your environment?"
- "What's your process for checking references beyond the candidate's provided list?"

---

### 4. Time-to-Fill Extends Critical Initiatives
**Pain:** Critical strategic initiatives stall because C-Suite positions remain vacant for 6-9 months. Competitors gain market share while the company operates with leadership gaps.
**Current Solution:** Engage large retained search firms (slow process), interim executives (expensive, temporary fix), stretch existing team (burnout risk)
**Cost of Pain:** Delayed product launches ($1M+ in lost revenue), missed market windows, team attrition from leadership vacuum
**Your Solution:** Accelerated 45-60 day search process with dedicated team, pre-vetted candidate pool, streamlined decision framework
**Pricing:** Premium fee ($100K-$150K) justified by speed and reduced time-cost

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What initiatives are currently blocked because you don't have this leader in place?"
- "What's the revenue impact if this hire is delayed another quarter?"
- "How long can your current team stretch to cover this role?"

---

### 5. Cultural Misalignment with Growth-Stage Reality
**Pain:** Executives from Fortune 500 companies fail in growth-stage environments—they cannot adapt to resource constraints, ambiguity, and the pace of decision-making required.
**Current Solution:** Hope the executive will adapt, provide coaching (rarely works at C-level), terminate and restart search
**Cost of Pain:** 9-12 months of salary and severance ($400K-$800K), lost momentum, damaged team morale
**Your Solution:** Deep vetting of growth-stage experience, behavioral interviewing for adaptability, culture-fit assessment specific to company stage
**Pricing:** $60K-$120K with culture guarantee period

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Describe your company's culture in three words. How does it differ from larger companies?"
- "What's the biggest culture shock an executive from a big company would face here?"
- "Tell me about an executive who didn't work out—what cultural factors contributed?"

---

### 6. Cannot Compete with Public Company Compensation
**Pain:** Growth-stage companies cannot match the cash compensation packages of public companies, making it hard to attract proven C-Suite talent.
**Current Solution:** Offer excessive equity (dilutes founders), settle for less experienced candidates, delay hiring until post-IPO
**Cost of Pain:** Suboptimal leadership, inability to execute growth strategy, founder burnout from covering executive gaps
**Your Solution:** Total compensation modeling, equity structuring advisory, non-cash value proposition articulation (autonomy, impact, legacy-building)
**Pricing:** $50K-$100K with compensation consulting add-on ($10K-$25K)

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your total compensation ceiling for this role, and how does it compare to public market rates?"
- "How are you structuring the equity component to be competitive?"
- "What non-monetary factors can you emphasize to attract the right executive?"

---

### 7. Board Pressure Creates Rushed Decisions
**Pain:** Board members pressure the CEO to fill C-Suite gaps quickly, leading to rushed hires and suboptimal matches that create long-term problems.
**Current Solution:** Hire whoever is available quickly, ignore red flags due to time pressure, hope for the best
**Cost of Pain:** Costly terminations, board conflict, CEO credibility damage, potential investor concern about leadership quality
**Your Solution:** Board communication management, setting realistic timelines with data, candidate scorecards for objective evaluation
**Pricing:** $75K-$125K with board presentation support

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your board's timeline expectation for this hire?"
- "How has board pressure influenced your past hiring decisions?"
- "What data would help you set appropriate expectations with your board?"

---

### 8. Succession Planning Doesn't Exist
**Pain:** Founders have no succession plan for key executives. When someone leaves unexpectedly, there's no internal pipeline and the company scrambles.
**Current Solution:** Reactive hiring when departures occur, promoting unprepared internal candidates, rushed external searches
**Cost of Pain:** 6-12 months of leadership gaps, knowledge loss, team uncertainty and attrition
**Your Solution:** Succession planning advisory, building internal candidate pipelines, emergency search capabilities
**Pricing:** $30K-$50K for succession planning + search fees when needed

**Discovery Questions:**
- "If your CFO left tomorrow, how long would it take to find a replacement?"
- "Do you have internal candidates being developed for C-Suite roles?"
- "What's your plan for leadership continuity during unexpected departures?"

---

### 9. Inability to Evaluate Technical Competence
**Pain:** Boards and CEOs lack the technical depth to evaluate CFO candidates' financial sophistication or CMO candidates' marketing acumen, relying on superficial assessments.
**Current Solution:** Bring in advisors for interviews (inconsistent, availability issues), trust credentials and brand names, hope resume equals competence
**Cost of Pain:** Hiring executives who are good politicians but poor operators, discovering capability gaps months into tenure
**Your Solution:** Functional expertise on assessment team, technical case studies, scenario-based evaluations specific to company challenges
**Pricing:** $80K-$140K with technical advisory panel

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you evaluate technical competence for this functional area?"
- "What technical scenarios would you want a candidate to demonstrate mastery of?"
- "Who on your team or board has the expertise to assess candidates technically?"

---

### 10. Geographic Constraints Limit Talent Pool
**Pain:** Companies requiring executives in specific locations (non-hub cities) struggle to attract top talent willing to relocate or work remotely from headquarters.
**Current Solution:** Limit search to local candidates (weakens pool), offer relocation packages (expensive), accept remote work for C-Suite (board concerns)
**Cost of Pain:** Suboptimal local candidates, failed relocations, ongoing tension between talent quality and location requirements
**Your Solution:** Relocation strategy consulting, remote/hybrid C-Suite models, geographic expansion of search to willing relocators
**Pricing:** $60K-$100K with location advisory

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Is this role location-specific, or can it be remote/hybrid?"
- "What's your experience with relocating executives? Success rate?"
- "How flexible is your board on location requirements for this hire?"

---

### 11. First-Time CEO Needs Experienced CFO/COO
**Pain:** First-time founders who are CEOs need experienced operators to complement their skills, but don't know how to evaluate experienced executives or establish appropriate authority dynamics.
**Current Solution:** Hire based on impressive resumes, fail to delegate appropriately, experience power struggles or founder override
**Cost of Pain:** Executive frustration and departure, founder burnout, stalled growth due to inability to leverage executive expertise
**Your Solution:** First-time CEO coaching, executive integration support, authority framework definition, cultural bridging
**Pricing:** $70K-$120K with integration support package

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Is this your first time hiring a [CFO/COO/CMO]?"
- "How do you envision the working relationship with this executive?"
- "What concerns do you have about bringing in someone more experienced than you in their functional area?"

---

### 12. Confidentiality Requirements Limit Search Options
**Pain:** Confidential searches (replacement of underperforming executives, stealth mode companies) cannot use standard recruiting methods that reveal the company's identity.
**Current Solution:** Use only most trusted network (limited reach), delay search until situation is public, risk leaks through broad recruiting
**Cost of Pain:** Extended timeline due to limited candidate pool, risk of leaks damaging current executive or company reputation
**Your Solution:** Confidential search protocols, blind candidate presentations, non-disclosure frameworks, trusted intermediary positioning
**Pricing:** $75K-$125K with confidentiality premium

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Does this search need to remain confidential? If so, why?"
- "What's the timeline for when this search can become public?"
- "Who currently knows about your intent to make this hire?"

---

### 13. Poor Candidate Experience Damages Employer Brand
**Pain:** Slow, disorganized interview processes and poor communication damage the company's reputation with executive candidates who share experiences with their networks.
**Current Solution:** Ad-hoc scheduling, inconsistent messaging, delayed feedback, ghosting candidates
**Cost of Pain:** Lost candidates to competitors, negative Glassdoor reviews, word-of-mouth reputation damage in executive circles
**Your Solution:** White-glove candidate experience, streamlined interview process, timely communication guarantees, employer brand protection
**Pricing:** $60K-$110K with experience guarantee

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How would candidates describe your interview process?"
- "What's your average time from first interview to offer for executive roles?"
- "Have you lost candidates due to process delays or poor experience?"

---

### 14. Integration Failure Post-Placement
**Pain:** Even successful searches result in executive failures because companies lack structured onboarding and integration for C-Suite hires.
**Current Solution:** Standard HR onboarding, sink-or-swim approach, minimal support for cultural navigation
**Cost of Pain:** 40% of executive hires fail within 18 months, costing $500K-$1M+ per failure
**Your Solution:** 90-day integration program, stakeholder alignment sessions, early warning monitoring, coaching support
**Pricing:** $80K-$150K including integration support

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What does your onboarding process look like for C-Suite hires?"
- "How do you support new executives in their first 90 days?"
- "What percentage of your executive hires are still with you after 2 years?"

---

### 15. Equity Negotiations Create Deal Friction
**Pain:** Complex equity negotiations slow offers and create candidate anxiety, with founders unsure how to structure competitive but fair packages.
**Current Solution:** Standard equity bands, one-size-fits-all approaches, reactive negotiation when candidates push back
**Cost of Pain:** Lost candidates at offer stage, excessive dilution from reactive concessions, misalignment on expectations
**Your Solution:** Equity structuring advisory, market benchmarking, negotiation coaching, package optimization
**Pricing:** $50K-$100K with compensation consulting

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you typically structure equity for C-Suite hires?"
- "What's your philosophy on equity vs. cash compensation balance?"
- "Have you lost candidates during the offer negotiation phase?"

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"We place growth-stage C-Suite leaders who have scaled through your exact revenue milestone—in 45 days, not 6 months."

### Authority Builders
1. **The Growth-Stage Leadership Report:** Annual research on C-Suite compensation, tenure, and success factors for Series B-D companies
2. **Scaling Competency Framework:** Published methodology for assessing executive readiness for specific growth stages
3. **C-Suite Success Calculator:** Interactive tool showing cost of leadership gaps and ROI on quality hires
4. **Board Briefing Series:** Quarterly webinars for boards on evaluating leadership teams for different growth phases

### Positioning Angles
- **The Speed Angle:** "Traditional firms take 6-9 months. We deliver your C-Suite leader in 45-60 days because we specialize exclusively in growth-stage companies."
- **The Expertise Angle:** "Big firms place executives at Fortune 500s. We only place leaders who have scaled from $20M to $200M ARR—that's our exclusive focus."
- **The Integration Angle:** "We don't just place executives; we ensure they succeed with 90-day integration support that reduces failure rates by 60%."

### Differentiators
- Exclusive focus on Series B-D growth stage (not generalist)
- Active C-Suite network of 5,000+ growth-stage executives
- 12-month placement guarantee (industry standard is 6 months)
- Board-level advisory experience and credibility
- Integration support included in every placement

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Pre-revenue or seed-stage companies (cannot afford fees, unclear needs)
- ❌ Companies with no board support for external search
- ❌ CEOs who want "a unicorn" candidate with unrealistic requirements
- ❌ Companies with recurring C-Suite turnover (systemic cultural problems)
- ❌ Unwillingness to sign exclusive retainer agreement

**Best Prospects:**
- Series B-D companies with $20M-$200M ARR
- PE-backed portfolio companies with clear value-creation timelines
- Companies preparing for major growth inflection (acquisition, international expansion, IPO prep)
- CEOs who have experienced the pain of a bad C-Suite hire
- Boards with independent directors who understand executive search value

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"Your fees are higher than contingency recruiters."**
> "Contingency recruiters work on 5-10 similar searches simultaneously. We dedicate a team exclusively to your search. You're paying for speed, confidentiality, and access to candidates who won't respond to contingency outreach. What's the cost of this role remaining open for another quarter?"

**"We've worked with [Big Firm] before."**
> "Big firms do excellent work—for Fortune 500 companies. Their process is built for that world: 6-9 months, junior associates doing the work, high fees. We're built for your world: 45-60 days, partners on every search, deep growth-stage specialization. How did the timeline and candidate quality work out with your previous firm?"

**"Why should we pay a retainer upfront?"**
> "The retainer ensures we can dedicate senior resources exclusively to your search and access our passive candidate network—which requires significant research investment. It also aligns incentives: we're committed to filling this role, not just submitting resumes. Our 94% placement rate and 12-month guarantee demonstrate our confidence."

**"We have strong networks and can do this ourselves."**
> "Most CEOs start there. The challenge isn't finding candidates—it's finding the right candidate quickly while running your business. Our average client spends 200+ hours on a C-Suite search when doing it themselves. How many hours can you dedicate to this over the next 60 days?"

**"What if the placement doesn't work out?"**
> "Our 12-month guarantee means we replace the candidate at no additional fee if things don't work out. But here's what's more important: our thorough assessment reduces that risk. We place executives with a 96% 2-year retention rate—versus the industry average of 60%. What protections did your last search partner offer?"
