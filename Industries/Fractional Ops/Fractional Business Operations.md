## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional Business Operations services provide general operational leadership to companies that have outgrown ad-hoc operations but aren't ready for a full-time COO or operations leader. These businesses typically have $2M-$20M in revenue and are experiencing operational chaos from growth. They need someone to build processes, manage tools, and optimize operations—but can't justify $150K-$250K for a full-time executive.

A Fractional Business Operations leader works 2-4 days per week, serving as the operational utility player who can tackle whatever operational challenges arise.

**Market Size:** 600,000+ growth-stage companies needing operational support  
**Target:** CEOs and founders of service and product companies  
**Examples:** Operations consultancies, business efficiency firms, individual practitioners

## 💬 Objection Handling

**Do not we just need an office manager?**
> An office manager handles facilities, supplies, and day-to-day admin. Business Operations is strategic—building systems, optimizing processes, managing vendors, and preparing for scale. Many of my clients have both: an office manager for day-to-day and fractional Biz Ops for strategic improvements.

**How is this different from a COO?**
> A COO is a senior executive who leads all operations. Business Ops is more tactical and hands-on—doing the work to build systems and processes. Many companies start with fractional Biz Ops and grow into a COO role. I can also support an existing COO by handling specific operational initiatives.

**What specific things do you do?**
> I am a generalist operator who can handle diverse needs: process improvement, tool optimization, vendor management, project coordination, documentation, reporting, and more. I typically start with an assessment to identify your biggest operational pain points, then tackle them systematically. The specific work depends on your unique needs.

**How do we measure success?**
> Success metrics depend on what we focus on but typically include: process efficiency gains, cost reductions, time savings, error reduction, and employee satisfaction. We establish specific metrics for each initiative in month 1. Most clients see tangible improvements within 60 days.

**What if we need different skills than you have?**
> I bring generalist operational expertise that covers most common business operations needs. For specialized requirements—deep technical expertise, specific industry knowledge, or complex financial analysis—I coordinate with specialists in my network. Think of me as your operational quarterback who knows when to bring in specialists.
