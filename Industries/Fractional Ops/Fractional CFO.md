# Template: Fractional CFO

**Category:** Fractional Ops  
**Sub-Category:** Financial Leadership  
**Entry Cost:** $1,000-$3,000  
**Timeline:** 1-2 months to first client  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly retainer, 1-3 days/week engagement

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional CFO services provide strategic financial leadership to companies that have outgrown their bookkeeper/accountant but aren't ready for a full-time CFO. These businesses typically have $2M-$25M in revenue, are preparing for fundraising or acquisition, or are navigating complex financial decisions. They need forecasting, board reporting, fundraising support, and strategic financial guidance—but can't justify $250K-$400K for a full-time hire.

A Fractional CFO works 1-3 days per week, providing the strategic financial oversight needed while the company scales. Engagements often focus on specific milestones: Series A preparation, acquisition readiness, turnaround situations, or establishing financial infrastructure for growth.

**Market Size:** 500,000+ SMBs with $2M-$50M revenue  
**Target:** CEOs and boards of growth-stage companies ($3M-$30M revenue)  
**Examples:** Kruze Consulting, Pilot, Botkeeper, boutique fractional CFO firms

**Primary Buyer:** CEO/Founder (often technical or product-focused, uncomfortable with finance)  
**Secondary:** Board members, investors, PE firms, venture capitalists  
**Decision Maker:** CEO with board/investor influence

**Budget:** $8,000-$18,000/month  
**Pain Urgency:** High-Medium - urgent when fundraising, ongoing for growth management

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. No Financial Forecasting or Modeling ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** The company operates without a financial model. No 12-month forecast, no scenario planning, no understanding of cash runway. Making decisions without knowing the financial impact.
**Current Solution:** Rough mental math, spreadsheet cobbled together for investor asks, reactive cash management
**Cost of Pain:** $100K-$500K/year in poor capital allocation, missed opportunities, cash crises, failed fundraising
**Your Solution:** Build comprehensive financial model with scenario planning, 12-24 month rolling forecast, KPI dashboard
**Pricing:** $10,000-$16,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your cash runway right now? How confident are you in that number?"
- "When you hire someone or make a major purchase, how do you evaluate the financial impact?"
- "What would happen to your cash position if revenue dropped 20% next quarter?"

---

### 2. Fundraising Preparation Chaos
**Pain:** The company needs to raise capital but lacks investor-ready financials, has messy cap table history, and can't answer due diligence questions confidently.
**Current Solution:** Last-minute scramble, expensive clean-up projects, founder trying to learn finance on the fly
**Cost of Pain:** Failed fundraising rounds, down-rounds, excessive dilution, deals falling apart in due diligence
**Your Solution:** Fundraising readiness assessment, financial package preparation, investor deck support, due diligence preparation
**Pricing:** $15,000-$25,000/month (premium for fundraising urgency)

**Discovery Questions:**
- "When an investor asks for your financials, how long does it take you to pull them together?"
- "What's the most embarrassing financial question you've been asked in a pitch?"
- "How confident are you in your revenue projections for investors?"

---

### 3. Board Reporting Nightmares
**Pain:** Monthly/quarterly board prep consumes days of CEO time. Reports are inconsistent, metrics change, the narrative feels defensive. Board members ask questions the CEO can't answer.
**Current Solution:** CEO works nights compiling deck, inconsistent formatting, reactive responses to board questions
**Cost of Pain:** $50K-$150K/year in CEO time, board confidence erosion, relationship strain, poor governance
**Your Solution:** Board reporting package with consistent metrics, automated data pulls, narrative development, Q&A prep
**Pricing:** $8,000-$14,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many hours do you spend preparing board materials each quarter?"
- "What financial questions do your board members ask that you wish you had better answers for?"
- "How consistent are your KPIs month-to-month in definition and calculation?"

---

### 4. Cash Flow Management Crisis
**Pain:** The company can't accurately predict cash position, doesn't understand cash conversion cycle, and occasionally faces payroll anxiety. No 13-week cash flow forecast.
**Current Solution:** Check bank balance daily, hope for the best, occasional panic calls to accountant
**Cost of Pain:** $50K-$200K/year in poor cash management, missed opportunities, emergency borrowing, founder stress
**Your Solution:** Cash flow reporting system, 13-week rolling forecast, working capital optimization, cash management protocols
**Pricing:** $8,000-$12,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many weeks of runway do you have right now?"
- "When was the last time you were worried about making payroll?"
- "What would you invest in right now if you had confidence in your cash position?"

---

### 5. Unit Economics Confusion
**Pain:** The company doesn't understand true unit economics—CAC, LTV, gross margin by product, payback period. Making pricing and product decisions blindly.
**Current Solution:** Rough estimates, inconsistent calculations, ignoring unit economics because they're "not positive yet"
**Cost of Pain:** $100K-$400K/year in unprofitable growth, poor pricing decisions, misallocated resources
**Your Solution:** Unit economics model, cohort analysis, profitability analysis by product/customer segment, pricing optimization
**Pricing:** $10,000-$16,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your customer acquisition cost? Your lifetime value? How confident are you in those numbers?"
- "Which of your products/services are actually profitable? Which are subsidizing which?"
- "If you had to cut one product line, how would you decide which one?"

---

### 6. Compensation Planning Without Structure
**Pain:** Salary decisions are made inconsistently, equity grants are haphazard, no clear compensation bands or philosophy. Risk of pay inequity, retention issues, or overspending.
**Current Solution:** Gut feel, what candidate demands, occasional benchmarking via Glassdoor or recruiter
**Cost of Pain:** $75K-$200K/year in overpayment, retention issues, pay equity risk, poor hiring outcomes
**Your Solution:** Compensation philosophy development, salary banding, equity grant framework, total rewards analysis
**Pricing:** $8,000-$14,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you decide what to pay a new hire?"
- "Do you have written compensation bands for each role?"
- "How do you determine equity grants for new employees?"

---

### 7. Inadequate Financial Controls
**Pain:** No separation of duties, founders approving their own expenses, lack of purchase order process, vulnerability to fraud or errors.
**Current Solution:** Trust-based system, founder reviews everything, basic accounting controls
**Cost of Pain:** $25K-$100K/year in fraud risk, errors, audit findings, investor concerns
**Your Solution:** Internal controls assessment, approval workflows, financial policy development, segregation of duties design
**Pricing:** $8,000-$12,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Who can authorize payments and write checks in your company?"
- "How do you prevent duplicate payments or fraud?"
- "When was your last audit, and what were the findings?"

---

### 8. Inaccurate or Delayed Financial Close
**Pain:** Monthly close takes 15-30 days. Financials are consistently late, inaccurate, or require multiple revisions. Can't make timely decisions.
**Current Solution:** Bookkeeper/accountant working with incomplete data, founder providing missing info reactively
**Cost of Pain:** $50K-$150K/year in delayed decisions, inaccurate reporting, poor planning
**Your Solution:** Close process optimization, month-end checklist, reconciliation procedures, accounting system improvements
**Pricing:** $8,000-$12,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How quickly do you get monthly financials after month-end?"
- "How often do you have to revise financials after initial release?"
- "What's the bottleneck in your close process?"

### 9. Budgeting Without Accountability
**Pain:** Annual budget is created and ignored. Departments overspend without consequences. No variance analysis or accountability.
**Current Solution:** Budget created for investors/board, departments spend what they need, occasional "be careful" emails
**Cost of Pain:** $100K-$300K/year in budget overruns, uncontrolled spending, poor resource allocation
**Your Solution:** Budget process redesign, variance reporting, departmental accountability frameworks, reforecasting cadence
**Pricing:** $8,000-$14,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How closely does your actual spending track to budget?"
- "What happens when a department exceeds budget?"
- "When was the last time you did a true variance analysis?"

### 10. Tax Planning as Afterthought
**Pain:** Taxes are a surprise each year. No strategic tax planning, missing deductions, poor entity structure, quarterly estimates are guesswork.
**Current Solution:** Reactive tax filing, last-minute scramble, paying what accountant says without understanding
**Cost of Pain:** $25K-$100K/year in overpaid taxes, missed opportunities, penalties, inefficient structure
**Your Solution:** Tax planning strategy, quarterly estimate process, R&D tax credit analysis, entity structure review
**Pricing:** $8,000-$12,000/month (often coordinates with tax preparer)

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How much did you pay in taxes last year? Was that expected?"
- "Are you taking advantage of R&D tax credits or other incentives?"
- "Do you understand your quarterly tax estimates before you pay them?"

### Differentiators
- Deep experience with growth-stage company financing (not just big corporate)

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Pre-revenue companies (not enough financial complexity yet)
- ❌ Companies in financial distress without realistic turnaround plan (too risky)
- ❌ Founders who won't share financial information (can't help without transparency)
- ❌ Companies that just want someone to blame for existing problems
- ❌ Businesses with fundamental business model issues (finance can't fix a broken model)

**Best Prospects:**
- $3M-$30M revenue with clear growth trajectory
- Preparing for Series A, B, or C fundraising
- Considering acquisition or exit within 12-24 months
- Recent CFO departure needing interim coverage
- Board/investors pushing for financial discipline
- Complex business model requiring financial sophistication

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We have an accountant/bookkeeper. Isn't that enough?"**
> "Bookkeepers handle compliance and historical reporting—they tell you where you've been. A CFO helps you navigate where you're going. Most of my clients keep their bookkeeper and add strategic financial leadership. Think of it as the difference between having a pilot who files flight plans (bookkeeper) and a navigator who charts the course through storms (CFO). Both are necessary for a safe journey."

**"We can't afford CFO-level expertise right now."**
> "I hear that, and it's a common misconception. A full-time CFO at $250K+ is expensive. But fractional CFO services give you that expertise at 30-40% of the cost, and only the time you need. More importantly, what's the cost of NOT having this expertise? Failed fundraising, poor capital allocation, cash crises—these cost far more than a fractional CFO. Most of my clients find the CFO pays for itself through better capital efficiency and fundraising success."

**"How involved do you get versus just advising?"**
> "I do both. I roll up my sleeves and build your financial model, prepare your board deck, manage investor relationships, and oversee your finance function. I'm not just reviewing work—you'll see me in QuickBooks/Xero, leading forecasting sessions, and on calls with your bank. The goal is to leave you with working systems and a stronger finance team, not just recommendations."

**"What if we hire a full-time CFO in 6 months?"**
> "That's exactly the right trajectory—I expect most engagements to end with you hiring a full-time CFO. My job is to get you financially ready for that hire: clean books, working systems, documented processes, and a clear idea of what you need. Many clients keep me on in an advisory capacity after hiring their CFO, or I help with the search and transition. The goal is to make the fractional-to-full-time transition seamless."

**"We tried a part-time CFO before and it didn't work."**
> "That's frustrating. What didn't work about the previous arrangement? Often the issue is scope clarity—a part-time CFO who's just attending meetings isn't providing value. Or the engagement wasn't structured for impact. I typically work 2-3 specific days per week with clear deliverables, not just 'advisory' availability. Let's talk about what success would look like for you specifically, and I can design an engagement around that."

**"Do you work with our existing accounting firm?"**
> "Absolutely—I partner with your bookkeeper and CPA rather than replacing them. My role is strategic; their role is compliance and execution. I provide direction and oversight; they handle the day-to-day transactions and tax filings. This is typically more cost-effective than trying to do everything myself, and leverages the relationships you already have."

## 🚀 Validation Path

**Month 1:** 
- Define your CFO positioning and ideal client profile
- Create financial assessment framework and fundraising readiness scorecard
- Reach out to 50 CEOs and investors in your network
- Conduct 10-15 free financial assessments to refine messaging

**Month 2:**
- Convert 2-3 assessments into pilot engagements at $8K-$12K
- Build sample financial models and board decks as portfolio pieces
- Establish relationships with 3-5 VC/PE firms for referrals
- Document case studies from early clients

**Month 3:**
- Launch full retainer offering at target pricing
- Aim for 3-4 paying clients at $10K-$14K/month
- Publish authority content (fundraising guide, cash flow template)
- Join CFO communities and networks for visibility

**Month 4-6:**
- Scale to 5-7 concurrent clients
- Develop specialized offerings (fundraising prep, exit readiness)
- Build relationships with accounting firms for referrals
- Consider productized services (model templates, board deck reviews)

**Month 6-12:**
- Optimize for higher-value engagements ($15K-$18K)
- Develop interim CFO practice for urgent situations
- Build advisory relationships with portfolio companies
- Consider expanding team for overflow or specialized needs
