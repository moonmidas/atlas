**Category:** Fractional Ops

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional RevOps services align sales, marketing, and customer success operations to drive efficient revenue growth. These businesses typically have $5M-$50M in revenue and are experiencing friction between revenue teams, inconsistent metrics, and technology sprawl. They need someone to break down silos, unify metrics, and optimize the entire revenue engine—but can't justify $150K-$250K for a full-time hire.

A Fractional RevOps leader works 2-3 days per week, serving as the connective tissue between revenue teams. Unlike Sales Ops (focused only on sales), RevOps takes a holistic view of the entire customer journey from first touch to renewal.

**Market Size:** 300,000+ companies with multiple revenue teams  
**Target:** CROs, CEOs, and revenue leaders of $5M-$50M companies  
**Examples:** RevOps consultancies, revenue agencies, individual practitioners

## 💬 Objection Handling

**Is not this just Sales Ops with a fancy name?**
> Great question—Sales Ops focuses specifically on sales team effectiveness. RevOps takes a broader view across the entire customer journey: marketing, sales, and customer success. While Sales Ops optimizes the sales process, RevOps optimizes the entire revenue engine. Many companies start with Sales Ops and evolve to RevOps as they scale and feel interdepartmental friction.

**How do you get buy-in from all three teams?**
> You are right that buy-in is critical and often the hardest part. I start with alignment on shared goals—usually revenue and customer outcomes that all teams care about. Then I demonstrate quick wins that benefit everyone, like unified reporting that eliminates data disputes. Finally, I build relationships with each team's leadership to understand their specific pain points. The goal is shared success, not winners and losers.

**What if the teams do not want to align?**
> That is a red flag that usually signals deeper issues—comp culture, leadership problems, or fundamental business model concerns. RevOps can't fix those. I look for companies where the will to align exists but the how is missing. If teams are fundamentally opposed to working together, that requires cultural or leadership change first.

**How do we measure RevOps success?**
> Success metrics depend on your specific pain points but typically include: forecast accuracy improvement, interdepartmental SLA compliance, lead-to-customer conversion rates, customer lifetime value, and revenue per employee. We establish baselines in the first 30 days and track improvements monthly. Most clients see payback within 90 days through efficiency gains and improved conversion.

**We have tried to align teams before and failed.**
> That is common—alignment initiatives often fail because they are treated as one-time events rather than ongoing operations. They also fail when there is no single owner accountable for alignment. RevOps creates permanent accountability for alignment and builds the systems and metrics that sustain it. It is not a project with an end date—it is an ongoing function.
