# Template: Fractional Chief of Staff

**Category:** Fractional Ops  
**Sub-Category:** Strategic Execution  
**Entry Cost:** $500-$1,500  
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks to first client  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly retainer, 2-4 days/week engagement

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional Chief of Staff services provide strategic execution support to CEOs who need operational leverage but aren't ready for a full-time Chief of Staff or COO. These businesses typically have $2M-$20M in revenue, and the CEO is overwhelmed by the volume of decisions, communications, and priorities. They need someone to manage projects, filter communications, align priorities, and drive execution—but can't justify $150K-$250K for a full-time hire.

A Fractional Chief of Staff works 2-4 days per week, serving as the CEO's right hand and force multiplier. Unlike an executive assistant (who handles scheduling and logistics), the Chief of Staff handles strategic work: project management, communication filtering, priority alignment, and cross-functional coordination.

**Market Size:** 200,000+ companies with overwhelmed CEOs  
**Target:** CEOs of $2M-$20M revenue companies drowning in execution  
**Examples:** Chief of Staff Network, operations consultancies, individual practitioners

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"Isn't this just an expensive executive assistant?"**
> "Great question—the distinction is important. An executive assistant handles scheduling, travel logistics, and administrative tasks. A Chief of Staff handles strategic work: project management, communication filtering, priority alignment, and cross-functional coordination. I don't book your flights—I help you decide which meetings to take. I don't manage your calendar—I help you reclaim time for strategic work. Many CEOs have both; they serve different purposes."

**"Can't my COO do this?"**
> "If you have a COO, they absolutely should handle much of this. But many companies aren't ready for a COO yet, or need bridge support while preparing for that hire. A fractional Chief of Staff can provide immediate leverage and help prepare the organization for a COO. I've often transitioned to supporting a newly hired COO, or handed off to them as the company scales."

**"What if I become dependent on you?"**
> "The goal is actually the opposite—to reduce dependency on you as CEO. I document processes, build team capabilities, and create systems that outlast my engagement. Many clients find they can reduce my time as their organization gets stronger. The goal is to make you and your team more effective, not create a dependency."

**"How do we measure the ROI of a Chief of Staff?"**
> "The ROI is straightforward: CEO time reclaimed, projects completed, decisions made faster, and execution improved. In the first 30 days, we establish baseline metrics: your calendar composition, project completion rates, decision cycle time. I report monthly on improvements in these areas. Most clients see 15-20 hours/week returned to the CEO within 60 days—that's $12K-$16K in CEO opportunity cost alone."

**"Will my team accept direction from a fractional resource?"**
> "I work as an extension of you, not an external authority. When I coordinate or request information, it's clearly in support of your priorities. I build relationships quickly and focus on being helpful rather than directive. Most teams welcome the improved coordination and communication. Would it help for me to meet key team members before we start?"

**"What if I need different skills than you have?"**
> "I bring a specific set of skills around operations, coordination, and execution. For specialized needs—financial modeling, technical architecture, deep functional expertise—I coordinate with specialists or help you find them. Think of me as your generalist force multiplier who knows when to bring in specialists."
