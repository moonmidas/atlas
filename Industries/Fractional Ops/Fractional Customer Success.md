**Category:** Fractional Ops

**Revenue Model:** Monthly retainer, 2-3 days/week engagement

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional Customer Success services provide strategic CS leadership to SaaS and subscription companies that have outgrown reactive support but aren't ready for a full-time VP Customer Success. These businesses typically have $3M-$30M in ARR, 50-500 customers, and are experiencing churn issues, expansion challenges, or CS team scaling problems. They need playbooks, health scoring, team structure, and retention strategy—but can't justify $150K-$220K for a full-time executive.

A Fractional CS leader works 2-3 days per week, building the infrastructure for scalable customer success while driving immediate retention and expansion improvements.

**Market Size:** 200,000+ SaaS/subscription companies needing CS leadership  
**Target:** CEOs and CROs of B2B SaaS companies  
**Examples:** CS consulting firms, boutique agencies, individual practitioners

## 💬 Objection Handling

**Do not our CSMs already know what to do?**
> Individual CSMs often have great instincts, but relying on heroics doesn't scale. Without playbooks, health scoring, and systematic processes, success depends on having great CSMs—and keeping them. I document and systematize what your best CSMs do intuitively so all customers get great experiences, not just the lucky ones.

**How is this different from CS consulting?**
> Traditional CS consultants assess and recommend. I embed as a leader, build the actual systems, and help execute. You get working playbooks, implemented health scores, and trained teams—not just recommendations. I also serve as strategic CS leadership while you grow toward a full-time hire.

**What if we are not ready for structured CS?**
> If you have recurring revenue and customers renewing (or not), you are ready. The question is whether you manage retention proactively or reactively. Reactive CS means accepting whatever churn happens. Proactive CS means systematically improving retention and expansion. Most SaaS companies find CS ROI is 5-10x the investment through reduced churn alone.

**How do we measure CS success?**
> The primary metrics are retention and expansion: logo churn rate, revenue churn, net revenue retention (NRR), and expansion revenue. Secondary metrics include time-to-value, health scores, NPS, and CSM efficiency. We establish baselines in month 1 and track improvements monthly. Most clients see retention improvements within 90 days.

**What about our existing CS team?**
> I work with your existing team, making them more effective through playbooks, tools, and structure. Most CS teams welcome the clarity and support. My goal is to elevate the team's capabilities, not replace them. Many clients eventually promote from within to CS leadership after building the foundation together.
