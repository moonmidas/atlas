**Category:** Fractional Ops

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional Sales Ops services provide operational excellence to sales organizations that have outgrown ad-hoc processes but aren't ready for a full-time Sales Ops hire. These businesses typically have $3M-$30M in revenue, 5-30 sales reps, and are experiencing CRM chaos, forecasting failures, or enablement gaps. They need CRM optimization, forecasting rigor, compensation design, and sales process efficiency—but can't justify $120K-$180K for a full-time hire.

A Fractional Sales Ops professional works 2-4 days per week, building the systems and processes that help sales teams scale.

**Market Size:** 400,000+ companies with sales teams needing operational support  
**Target:** VP Sales, CROs, and CEOs of $3M-$30M revenue companies  
**Examples:** Sales Ops consulting firms, RevOps agencies, individual practitioners

## 💬 Objection Handling

**We have Salesforce/HubSpot already. Why do we need Sales Ops?**
> Great question—having CRM is necessary but not sufficient. Most companies have CRM but use 20% of its capability. Sales Ops optimizes your CRM configuration, ensures data quality, builds reporting, and drives adoption. Think of CRM as the engine and Sales Ops as the mechanic who keeps it tuned and the driver who knows how to use it effectively.

**Can not our sales managers handle this?**
> Sales managers should focus on coaching and developing reps, not building systems and running reports. When managers do Sales Ops work, both suffer—coaching gets neglected and systems get built poorly. Sales Ops frees managers to do what they do best: develop people and drive performance.

**How do we measure Sales Ops ROI?**
> The ROI is straightforward: forecast accuracy improvement, CRM adoption rates, ramp time reduction, rep productivity gains, and commission error reduction. In the first 90 days, we establish baseline metrics and track improvements. Most clients see payback within 60-90 days through improved forecast accuracy and rep efficiency alone.

**We tried Sales Ops before and it did not work.**
> That is frustrating. What did not work about the previous arrangement? Often the issue is scope clarity or authority—Sales Ops needs to be able to implement changes, not just recommend them. I embed with your team, build the systems, train users, and drive adoption. You will see working processes, not just recommendations.

**What is the difference between Sales Ops and RevOps?**
> Sales Ops focuses specifically on sales team effectiveness: CRM, forecasting, comp, enablement. RevOps is broader, covering sales, marketing, and customer success alignment. Many companies start with Sales Ops and expand to RevOps as they scale. I can operate in either model depending on your needs.
