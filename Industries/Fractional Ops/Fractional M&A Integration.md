## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional M&A Integration services provide strategic leadership for post-acquisition integration to companies that acquire businesses but lack internal integration expertise. These businesses are typically acquisitive companies, PE-backed platforms, or strategic buyers completing 1-4 deals per year. They need someone to plan and execute integration—managing systems, culture, operations, and value capture—but don't have enough deal volume to justify a full-time integration officer ($200K-$350K).

A Fractional M&A Integration leader works intensely during deal phases (diligence through 6-12 months post-close), managing the complex work of bringing two organizations together.

**Market Size:** 50,000+ acquisitive companies and PE firms  
**Target:** CEOs, COOs, and corporate development leaders  
**Examples:** Integration consultancies, boutique advisory firms, individual practitioners

## 💬 Objection Handling

**Do not investment bankers handle integration?**
> Investment bankers focus on deal execution—finding targets, negotiating terms, and closing. Integration starts after they are gone. The skills that make great deal-makers are different from the skills that make great integrators. You need both: bankers to close the deal and integration specialists to make it successful.

**Our corporate development team can handle this.**
> Corporate development teams are often stretched managing deal flow and cannot dedicate the intensive time integration requires. They also typically focus on financial and legal aspects rather than operational and cultural integration. I work alongside corp dev, handling the operational heavy lifting while they manage deal pipeline.

**How is this different from management consulting?**
> Traditional consulting firms provide analysis and recommendations. I embed as an operator, executing the integration plan, managing workstreams, and ensuring outcomes. You get integration delivered, not just integration planned. I am accountable for results, not just reports.

**What if the deal falls through after we start?**
> Integration planning typically starts during diligence, before definitive agreements. If the deal does not close, you have still gained valuable intelligence about the target and your own integration capabilities. My fees are structured to align with deal milestones, reducing risk if transactions do not complete.

**Can you handle multiple deals at once?**
> Deal timing varies, but I typically work on 1-2 integrations simultaneously at different stages. For clients with multiple concurrent deals, I can build a team or coordinate multiple fractional resources. Let us discuss your deal pipeline and timing to ensure proper coverage.
