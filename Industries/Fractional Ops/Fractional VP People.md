## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional VP People services provide strategic HR and people operations leadership to companies that have outgrown ad-hoc people management but aren't ready for a full-time VP People or CHRO. These businesses typically have 30-150 employees, are experiencing rapid hiring, and need infrastructure for performance management, culture, and compliance. They need someone to build people systems—but can't justify $180K-$280K for a full-time executive.

A Fractional VP People works 2-3 days per week, serving as the strategic people leader while building toward a full-time hire. Unlike traditional HR (focused on compliance and admin), this role focuses on organizational design, culture, talent strategy, and people operations infrastructure.

**Market Size:** 500,000+ growth-stage companies with people complexity  
**Target:** CEOs and founders of 30-150 person companies  
**Examples:** People Ops consultancies, HR advisory firms, boutique fractional CHROs

## 💬 Objection Handling

**Do not we just need an HR generalist?**
> HR generalists handle day-to-day employee issues, compliance, and administration. A VP People focuses on strategy: organizational design, culture, talent systems, and people operations infrastructure. Most of my clients have both—a generalist for day-to-day and fractional VP People for strategic direction. As you scale toward 100+ people, you will likely need both roles anyway.

**How is this different from an HR consultant?**
> Traditional HR consultants typically handle compliance, policy, or specific projects. I embed as a strategic leader, attend leadership meetings, and build ongoing people operations capability. It is the difference between having someone write a handbook (consultant) and having someone design your entire people strategy and help execute it (fractional VP People).

**What if we are not ready for structured HR?**
> If you are 30+ people and growing, you are ready—whether you realize it or not. The question is whether you address people operations proactively or reactively. Reactive HR means dealing with turnover crises, compliance issues, and cultural erosion. Proactive HR means building the foundation for sustainable growth. Most founders wish they had started earlier.

**How do we measure success?**
> Success metrics depend on your priorities but typically include: time-to-hire, time-to-productivity, voluntary turnover rate, engagement scores, and manager effectiveness ratings. We establish baselines in the first 30 days and track improvements monthly. Most clients see payback within 90 days through reduced turnover and faster hiring.

**Will managers accept direction from a fractional resource?**
> I work as a partner to managers, not an authority over them. My role is to make managers more successful by giving them tools, frameworks, and support. When managers see me helping them hire better, develop their teams, and handle difficult conversations, they become advocates. The goal is manager enablement, not manager oversight.
