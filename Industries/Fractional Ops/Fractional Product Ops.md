## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional Product Ops services provide operational excellence to product organizations that have outgrown ad-hoc processes but aren't ready for a full-time Product Ops hire. These businesses typically have $5M-$50M in revenue, multiple product teams, and are experiencing roadmapping chaos, data fragmentation, or process inefficiency. They need someone to build product infrastructure—but can't justify $130K-$200K for a full-time hire.

A Fractional Product Ops leader works 2-3 days per week, building the systems that help product teams ship better products faster.

**Market Size:** 250,000+ product companies needing product operations  
**Target:** Chief Product Officers, VP Product, and CEOs of tech companies  
**Examples:** Product Ops consultancies, boutique firms, individual practitioners

## 💬 Objection Handling

**Do not PMs just do this work themselves?**
> PMs often end up doing operational work—managing roadmaps, pulling data, coordinating launches—but that is not the best use of their time. Every hour a PM spends on operations is an hour not spent understanding customers and defining solutions. Product Ops frees PMs to do what they do best: product strategy and discovery.

**Is not this just project management?**
> Project management is one piece of Product Ops, but not the whole thing. Product Ops also includes analytics, tooling, process design, communication, and stakeholder management. Think of Project Managers as focused on execution of specific initiatives, while Product Ops optimizes how the entire product organization works.

**How do we measure Product Ops success?**
> Success metrics include: release frequency/stability, time spent on operational tasks vs. strategic work, data accessibility, stakeholder satisfaction, and product team velocity. We establish baselines in month 1 and track improvements. Most clients see PM productivity gains of 20-30% within 90 days.

**What if we only have one product team?**
> Product Ops typically becomes valuable when you have 3+ PMs or significant operational complexity. With one team, the CPO/VP usually handles operations. If you are planning to scale the team in the next 6-12 months, it can be valuable to build Product Ops infrastructure proactively.

**How does Product Ops differ from Program Management?**
> Program Management focuses on coordinating large cross-functional initiatives. Product Ops focuses on optimizing the product organization itself: processes, tools, data, and communication. Some companies have both; the roles are complementary. I can operate in either capacity depending on your needs.
