# Template: Fractional CMO

**Category:** Fractional Ops  
**Sub-Category:** Marketing Leadership  
**Entry Cost:** $1,000-$3,000  
**Timeline:** 1-2 months to first client  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly retainer, 2-3 days/week engagement

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Fractional CMO services provide strategic marketing leadership to companies that have outgrown ad-hoc marketing efforts but aren't ready for a full-time CMO. These businesses typically have $3M-$30M in revenue, are spending significantly on marketing without clear strategy, or are preparing for growth phases that require marketing sophistication. They need brand strategy, team leadership, performance optimization, and growth planning—but can't justify $200K-$350K for a full-time executive.

A Fractional CMO works 2-3 days per week, providing the strategic oversight and leadership needed while the company scales. Engagements focus on building marketing infrastructure, leading marketing teams, establishing performance frameworks, and driving growth initiatives.

**Market Size:** 400,000+ growth-stage companies with marketing complexity  
**Target:** CEOs and growth leaders of $3M-$30M revenue companies  
**Examples:** Chief Outsiders, Hawkeye Digital, RiseOpp, boutique fractional CMO firms

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. No Strategic Marketing Direction ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Marketing is a collection of tactics without strategy—random acts of marketing. No clear positioning, messaging, or growth framework. Teams execute without understanding why.
**Current Solution:** Reactive campaign creation, copying competitors, chasing shiny objects, agency-of-the-month
**Cost of Pain:** $200K-$500K/year in wasted spend, poor ROI, team confusion, missed growth opportunities
**Your Solution:** Marketing strategy development, positioning framework, messaging architecture, growth planning
**Pricing:** $12,000-$16,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What is your marketing strategy beyond 'do more marketing'?"
- "How do you decide which marketing channels or tactics to invest in?"
- "What would your marketing team say are the top 3 priorities—and would they agree with each other?"

---

### 2. Marketing Team Chaos
**Pain:** The marketing team lacks leadership, clear roles, or development path. High turnover, confusion about priorities, and individual contributors optimizing for their own metrics rather than business outcomes.
**Current Solution:** Senior IC trying to lead, CEO micromanaging, rotating freelancers/agencies
**Cost of Pain:** $150K-$400K/year in turnover costs, poor performance, misaligned efforts, low morale
**Your Solution:** Team structure design, role clarity, performance frameworks, 1:1 development cadence, hiring support
**Pricing:** $10,000-$16,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many people are on your marketing team, and what does each person own?"
- "When was the last time someone on your marketing team left? Why?"
- "How do you evaluate marketing team performance beyond activity metrics?"

### 3. Attribution and Measurement Confusion
**Pain:** No clear understanding of what's working. Marketing reports activity (impressions, clicks) not outcomes (revenue, CAC). Can't answer "what's our ROI on marketing spend?"
**Current Solution:** Vanity metrics in Google Analytics, last-click attribution, hoping for the best
**Cost of Pain:** $100K-$300K/year in wasted spend on ineffective channels, inability to optimize, poor budget allocation
**Your Solution:** Marketing analytics framework, attribution model, dashboard development, data infrastructure review
**Pricing:** $10,000-$14,000/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What percentage of your marketing budget can you confidently say is driving revenue?"
- "How do you currently attribute revenue to marketing efforts?"
- "What marketing metrics do you report to the board/CEO?"

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Pre-product-market fit companies (can't market a product no one wants)
- ❌ Founders who want marketing to "fix" a sales or product problem
- ❌ Companies expecting immediate results without strategic foundation
- ❌ Organizations that have burned through 3+ marketing leaders recently
- ❌ Businesses unwilling to invest in marketing infrastructure

**Best Prospects:**
- $3M-$30M revenue with product-market fit established
- Clear growth goals and funding (or path to funding)
- Existing marketing team that needs leadership
- Recent growth plateau requiring strategic reset
- Preparing for fundraising or acquisition
- Willingness to commit 6+ months to transformation

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We already have a marketing agency."**
> "Great—agencies can execute well when given clear direction. The question is: who's setting that direction? Most of my clients keep their agencies and add strategic leadership. Agencies need a CMO to define strategy, manage performance, and ensure alignment with business goals. Without strategic leadership, agencies often optimize for their own metrics rather than yours. I can work with your existing agencies to get more value from that relationship."

**"Can't our VP of Marketing handle this?"**
> "If you have a strong VP Marketing who's strategic but stretched, I can work alongside them as a mentor and strategic partner. If your VP is tactical or junior, they likely need the frameworks and leadership development I can provide. I've worked both ways—sometimes augmenting existing marketing leadership, sometimes filling a strategic gap while you develop internal talent. Let's look at what your current marketing leader needs to succeed."

**"Marketing is too important to be fractional."**
> "I agree marketing is critical—which is why having NO strategic marketing leadership is worse than having fractional leadership. Most growth-stage companies face a choice: no CMO, a junior marketer trying to act like one, or fractional expertise. Fractional gives you senior-level thinking and proven frameworks at a fraction of the cost. And the engagement often transitions to full-time as you grow—my job is to build toward that."

**"How do we measure the ROI of a fractional CMO?"**
> "Great question—marketing should be held accountable. In the first 90 days, we establish baseline metrics and specific improvement targets: CAC reduction, lead quality improvement, pipeline contribution, team retention. I report against these metrics monthly. Many clients see payback within 60-90 days through marketing efficiency gains alone—before growth acceleration kicks in. Would you like to see a sample 90-day plan with specific targets?"

**"We've tried marketing consultants before and got decks, not results."**
> "That's a frustrating and common experience. Most marketing consultants give you a strategy deck and leave. I embed with your team, lead your marketing meetings, manage your agencies, and drive execution. You'll see me working alongside your team—not just advising from the sidelines. The goal is working systems and a stronger team, not a binder on a shelf. Would references from current clients help?"

**"What if we need you full-time during a launch or crisis?"**
> "I build flexibility into my engagements. During critical periods—product launches, fundraising, major campaigns—I can increase my time commitment temporarily. Some clients also use my engagement to develop an internal successor who can step up during intensive periods. The fractional model is designed to flex with your needs, not be rigid."

## 🚀 Validation Path

**Month 1:** 
- Define your CMO positioning and ideal client profile
- Create marketing maturity assessment framework
- Reach out to 50 CEOs and CMOs in your network
- Conduct 10-15 free assessments to refine messaging

**Month 2:**
- Convert 2-3 assessments into pilot engagements at $8K-$12K
- Build sample marketing strategy frameworks as portfolio pieces
- Establish relationships with VC firms and executive recruiters
- Document case studies from early clients

**Month 3:**
- Launch full retainer offering at target pricing
- Aim for 3-4 paying clients at $10K-$14K/month
- Publish authority content (marketing metrics guide, assessment tool)
- Join CMO communities and marketing associations

**Month 4-6:**
- Scale to 5-8 concurrent clients
- Develop specialized offerings (fundraising prep, turnaround marketing)
- Build relationships with marketing agencies for partnerships
- Consider productized services (strategy sprints, team assessments)

**Month 6-12:**
- Optimize for higher-value engagements ($14K-$16K)
- Develop advisory relationships with portfolio companies
- Build network for overflow and specialized needs
- Consider content/course offerings for additional revenue
