# Insurance Agent CRM

**Category:** Micro SaaS  
**Sub-Category:** Insurance/Professional Services  
**Entry Cost:** $12K-$30K  
**Timeline:** 2-4 months to MVP  
**Revenue Model:** $49-$149/month per agent

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

CRM built specifically for insurance agents managing 500+ client relationships, policy renewals, and cross-sell opportunities. Generic CRMs (HubSpot, Salesforce) miss the nuances of insurance workflows.

**Market Size:** 1.2M licensed agents in US  
**Target:** Independent agents, small agencies (1-20 agents)

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** Independent agent or agency owner
**Secondary:** Office managers, producers
**Decision Maker:** Owner (agencies) or individual (independent)

**Budget:** $50-$150/month per agent
**Pain Urgency:** High (renewals happening daily, clients slipping through cracks)

---

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Policy Renewal Tracking ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Renewals happen daily, clients slip through cracks
**Current Solution:** Excel spreadsheets, calendar reminders, memory
**Cost of Pain:** Lost renewals ($500-$5K per policy), client churn
**Your Solution:** Automated renewal tracking with alerts
**Pricing:** $79-$129/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you track policy renewals currently?"
- "How many renewals do you miss per month?"
- "What's your retention rate?"

---

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"HubSpot wasn't built for insurance. Your renewals are slipping through the cracks because your CRM doesn't understand policies."

### Authority Builders
1. Insurance industry retention benchmarks
2. "The State of Insurance Agent Technology" report
3. Case study: "How [Agent] increased retention by 15%"
4. ROI calculator for missed renewals

### Positioning Angles
- Industry-specific: "Built by agents, for agents"
- Retention focus: "Keep more clients, automatically"
- Cross-sell: "Identify coverage gaps before competitors"

### Differentiators
- Policy-centric (not contact-centric)
- Carrier integrations
- Commission tracking
- E&O protection features

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Captive agents (State Farm, Allstate) - can't use outside tools
- ❌ Part-time agents (<10 hours/week)
- ❌ Agents with <200 clients (not enough pain)
- ❌ Tech-averse agents ("I like my rolodex")
- ❌ Agencies switching AMS (agency management systems) - too much change

**Best Prospects:**
- Independent P&C agents with 500+ clients
- Life/health agents with complex needs
- Growing agencies (hiring new producers)
- Agents 40-60 years old (established book, see the value)

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We already use HubSpot/Salesforce"**
> "Those are great general tools. How do you track policy renewals in HubSpot? [Wait] That's exactly why we built this—for the insurance-specific workflows generic CRMs can't handle."

**"We're part of a cluster/group"**
> "Many cluster members use us because the cluster tools don't handle [specific pain]. Can I show you how we complement what you have?"

**"I don't have time to switch"**
> "We migrate your data in 48 hours. Most agents are sending renewal alerts within a week. What if we handled the migration for you?"

**"Our agency management system does this"**
> "AMS systems are great for carriers, but terrible for client relationships. Most agents use us alongside their AMS for the client-facing features. Can I show you how they work together?"
