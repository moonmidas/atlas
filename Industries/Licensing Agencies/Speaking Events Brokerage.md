# Speaking Events Brokerage

**Category:** Licensing Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Speaker booking and event talent brokerage  
**Entry Cost:** $10K-$25K  
**Timeline:** 3-6 months  
**Revenue Model:** 20-30% commission on speaking fees, bureau retainers, event planning add-ons

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Speaking events brokerages connect speakers with conferences, corporate meetings, private events, and association programs. The agency creates value by matching event goals to speaker fit, managing pricing and logistics, and reducing the complexity event teams face when booking experts, entertainers, executives, or public personalities.

The sweet spot is not competing head-on with massive speaker bureaus for household names, but representing high-conviction niche experts, category operators, thought leaders, and professional speakers who close well with targeted event buyers. Repeat event calendars create dependable inventory and repeat demand.

**Market Size:** Large global event speaker market across corporate offsites, associations, trade shows, and leadership events  
**Target:** Professional speakers, founders, authors, executives, conference organizers, agencies, and corporate event teams  
**Examples:** Washington Speakers Bureau, Harry Walker, boutique speaker agencies

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** Event producer, conference programmer, HR/learning lead, or executive assistant  
**Secondary:** CEO, marketing, procurement, talent manager, speaker agent  
**Decision Maker:** Event director, chief people officer, founder, or executive sponsor

**Budget:** $5K-$100K per appearance depending on speaker profile and event scope  
**Pain Urgency:** High because event deadlines and agenda design create time pressure

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Event teams need a strong speaker fast ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Programming teams are often under pressure to fill keynotes or breakout sessions quickly.  
**Current Solution:** Search bureaus, LinkedIn, or previous event contacts.  
**Cost of Pain:** Weak agenda quality, late bookings, and stressed teams.  
**Your Solution:** Curated speaker options by topic, audience, and budget.  
**Pricing:** 20-30% of booking fee or $1K-$3K search fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which event roles are hardest to fill on time?
- How much time does speaker sourcing currently consume?

---

### 2. Speaker fit is often unclear beyond the bio
**Pain:** Great credentials do not guarantee audience resonance or delivery quality.  
**Current Solution:** Judge by website, clips, and recommendations.  
**Cost of Pain:** Underwhelming sessions and attendee dissatisfaction.  
**Your Solution:** Better qualification around delivery style, topic depth, and audience fit.  
**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What makes a speaker feel like a bad fit after booking?
- Which audience traits matter most when evaluating speakers?

---

### 3. Pricing is opaque and inconsistent
**Pain:** Buyers struggle to know what a fair speaker fee looks like.  
**Current Solution:** Ask around or accept quoted price.  
**Cost of Pain:** Overpayment or lost talent.  
**Your Solution:** Rate guidance and realistic booking ranges by event type.  
**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Where do budget conversations break down most often?
- What speaker tiers feel hardest to price confidently?

---

### 4. Event logistics are tedious and error-prone
**Pain:** Travel, AV, briefings, and timing details create operational load.  
**Current Solution:** Event staff coordinate manually.  
**Cost of Pain:** Last-minute problems and more staff time.  
**Your Solution:** Broker-managed logistics checklist and speaker coordination.  
**Pricing:** $500-$3K logistics fee or included in commission.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which logistics issues create the most stress?
- How often do speaker-specific requests disrupt the event plan?

---

### 5. Speakers lack outbound booking systems
**Pain:** Great speakers often depend on referrals and sporadic inbound interest.  
**Current Solution:** Post content and wait.  
**Cost of Pain:** Revenue volatility.  
**Your Solution:** Outbound calendar development to event organizers and bureaus.  
**Pricing:** Monthly speaker rep retainer plus commission.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What percentage of speaking business is inbound now?
- Which event categories do you want more access to?

---

### 6. Event buyers want customization without overpaying
**Pain:** Corporate teams expect tailored content, Q&A prep, and relevance.  
**Current Solution:** Buy a keynote and hope it fits.  
**Cost of Pain:** Weak engagement or expensive custom prep.  
**Your Solution:** Scope customization levels clearly and price them correctly.  
**Pricing:** $1K-$10K premium for workshops or tailored content.

**Discovery Questions:**
- How much customization does the event really need?
- What prep work creates the most value for your audience?

---

### 7. Event calendars create seasonal revenue gaps
**Pain:** Speakers and brokers can have heavy and light quarters.  
**Current Solution:** Accept the seasonality.  
**Cost of Pain:** Revenue instability.  
**Your Solution:** Mix associations, corporate, offsite, and virtual bookings across the year.  
**Pricing:** Included in strategy.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which months are strongest and weakest for bookings?
- What event segments could smooth seasonality?

---

### 8. Speakers are hard to compare objectively
**Pain:** Event teams see dozens of similar profiles with little clarity on actual performance.  
**Current Solution:** Watch clips and rely on brand recognition.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower programming confidence.  
**Your Solution:** Speaker positioning by audience type, transformation, and delivery style.  
**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What criteria do you use today besides topic and budget?
- Which delivery styles perform best at your events?

**Pain:** Small details can create outsized disputes.  
**Current Solution:** Redline each deal from scratch.  
**Cost of Pain:** Slower booking cycle and admin burden.

**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**

### 10. Mid-market events feel ignored by large bureaus
**Pain:** Smaller events still need quality talent but get less attention.  
**Current Solution:** Handle booking internally.  
**Cost of Pain:** More work and weaker talent access.  
**Your Solution:** Boutique service for mid-market event teams.  
**Pricing:** Included or flat search fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Where have larger bureaus been unhelpful?
- What service level do you wish you had during bookings?

**Pain:** Buyers and speakers disagree about virtual value versus in-person effort.  
**Current Solution:** Discount virtual heavily without logic.  
**Cost of Pain:** Margin loss or buyer resistance.  
**Your Solution:** Clear format-based pricing ladder.  
**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**

- Which hybrid expectations create friction?

---

### 12. Event themes change faster than speaker materials
**Pain:** Speakers keep pitching stale talks while buyers need timely relevance.  
**Current Solution:** Reuse old positioning.  
**Cost of Pain:** Fewer bookings and weak conversion.  
**Your Solution:** Update talk positioning and event-specific sales angles.  
**Pricing:** $1K-$4K messaging refresh.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which topics are getting traction right now?
- What parts of the speaker's positioning feel outdated?

---

### 13. Follow-on upsells are missed
**Pain:** Workshops, meet-and-greets, MC roles, and advisory add-ons are not packaged.  
**Current Solution:** Sell a keynote only.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower ACV.  
**Your Solution:** Multi-format speaking packages.  
**Pricing:** Included with add-on commission.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which add-on formats would buyers actually value?
- How often are events asking for more than a single talk?

---

### 14. Social proof is weakly presented
**Pain:** Testimonials, clips, and past-event outcomes are scattered.  
**Current Solution:** Share a deck or website link.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower buyer confidence.  
**Your Solution:** Booking-ready proof assets and speaker one-sheets.  
**Pricing:** $1K-$3K packaging fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What proof do buyers ask for most often?
- Which materials are missing from your booking process today?

---

### 15. Speakers need relationship-based repeat bookings
**Pain:** One-off events are fine, but recurring calendars drive stability.  
**Current Solution:** Restart sales every quarter.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower forecastability.  
**Your Solution:** Event account management and repeat booking strategy.  
**Pricing:** Retainer plus commission.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What percent of bookings come from repeat buyers?
- Which event accounts could become annual relationships?

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"We help event teams book speakers who actually fit the room, not just the bio."

### Authority Builders
1. Speaker booking scorecard by event type
2. Speaking fee benchmark by format and audience size
3. One-sheet and clip teardown for sellable speaker positioning
4. Corporate event briefing checklist

### Positioning Angles
- **Programming Quality:** Better fit creates better audience response.
- **Operational Ease:** Reduce the back-and-forth of booking and logistics.
- **Revenue Stability:** Build repeat event relationships for speakers and bureaus.

### Differentiators
- Stronger niche and mid-market event coverage than giant bureaus
- Better speaker-fit matching beyond fame and topic labels
- More packaging around workshops and add-on formats

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Speakers with weak stage delivery despite strong credentials
- ❌ Event buyers with vague goals and unrealistic budgets
- ❌ Clients who leave logistics until the last minute
- ❌ Speakers unwilling to customize at all for the audience
- ❌ Events with unstable budgets or unclear authority to book

**Best Prospects:**
- Repeat corporate and association buyers
- Speakers with strong testimonials but weak business development
- Events that need practical support, not just a speaker directory

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We can find speakers ourselves."**
> "You can find names yourself. The value is reducing mismatch, negotiation friction, and logistics overhead while improving the actual fit for the room."

**"Speaker commissions are too high."**
> "They are only high if the alternative is free. In reality the alternative is staff time, weak fit, and a lot of booking friction."

**"We only need one event booked."**
> "That is fine. One event is often the best way to prove fit before building a longer booking relationship."

**"Our budget is too small for a bureau."**
> "That is exactly where a boutique broker can help. Mid-market events often need guidance more than big-name access."

**"Virtual talks should be much cheaper."**
> "Sometimes yes, but value should still reflect prep, audience size, and impact, not just travel savings."

**"We already have a preferred speaker list."**
> "Great. We can strengthen it with fresh options and handle the booking process when internal teams are stretched."

## 📞 Discovery Questions

**To Event Buyers:**
- What outcome do you want the speaker to create for the audience?
- What has made previous speaker bookings disappoint?
- Which constraints matter most: budget, topic, timing, or format?

**To Speakers:**
- Which event categories are most attractive and why?
- How much of your current speaking work is repeat versus one-off?
- What parts of booking and delivery support would make the biggest difference?

**To Operations/Procurement:**
- Which booking terms and logistics details create the most friction?
- How early do you need contracts finalized to feel comfortable?
- What approvals or budget thresholds slow bookings down?
