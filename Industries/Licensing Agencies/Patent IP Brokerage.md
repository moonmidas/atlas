# Patent IP Brokerage

**Category:** Licensing Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Patent and IP monetization brokerage  
**Entry Cost:** $25K-$55K  
**Timeline:** 6-12 months  
**Revenue Model:** 20-35% success fee, retainers for packaging and outreach, deal-structuring fees

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Patent IP brokers connect patent owners with licensees, acquirers, investors, and operating companies that can commercialize or acquire protected technology. They create value by assessing commercial relevance, packaging portfolios, identifying likely buyers, and running negotiations that most inventors, labs, and small portfolio owners are not equipped to manage.

The strongest opportunities sit between under-monetized patents and strategic buyers that want freedom to operate, category differentiation, or defensible technology without building it from scratch. This is a slower, higher-ticket business than creator licensing, but one winning mandate can materially change annual revenue.

**Market Size:** Multi-billion-dollar global patent transaction and licensing market across manufacturing, software, medtech, energy, and industrial sectors  
**Target:** Independent inventors, SMEs, corporations with dormant portfolios, universities, manufacturers, and strategic acquirers  
**Examples:** Ocean Tomo, ICAP Patent Brokerage, boutique IP transaction advisors

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Patent owners do not know who will actually buy or license their IP ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Inventors and smaller portfolio owners lack market access to relevant acquirers and licensees.  
**Current Solution:** Cold outreach, patent marketplaces, or waiting for inbound interest.  
**Cost of Pain:** Years of zero monetization and sunk filing costs.  
**Your Solution:** Buyer mapping and targeted outreach by application, industry, and strategic fit.  
**Pricing:** 20-35% success fee plus optional $5K-$20K packaging retainer.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Who have you already approached, and what response did you get?
- Which industries or use cases are most commercially relevant for the portfolio?

---

### 2. Portfolios are technically strong but commercially unclear
**Pain:** Claims may be valid, but buyers cannot see the business case quickly.  
**Current Solution:** Share raw patent filings and attorney summaries.  
**Cost of Pain:** Buyer confusion and low engagement.  
**Your Solution:** Commercial packaging tied to markets, products, and buyer incentives.  
**Pricing:** $7K-$25K commercialization memo or included in retainer.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What product or market story does the portfolio currently tell?
- Where do buyers seem confused when first reviewing it?

---

### 3. Sellers overestimate value
**Pain:** Many owners anchor to invention effort, not market demand or claim scope.  
**Current Solution:** Hold out for unrealistic offers.  
**Cost of Pain:** Deals never start.  
**Your Solution:** Market-based valuation framing and deal range discipline.  
**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What value range are you expecting and why?
- What comparable transactions are shaping your expectations?

---

### 4. Buyers fear weak enforceability
**Pain:** A portfolio is worth less if claims are narrow, old, or easy to design around.  
**Current Solution:** Long internal legal review before engagement.  
**Cost of Pain:** Slower cycle times and lower bids.  
**Your Solution:** Pre-transaction diligence packaging and claim relevance analysis.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$30K diligence package.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which enforceability objections come up most often?
- How prepared are you with prior-art and claim-strength materials?

---

### 5. Patent holders lack deal structure options
**Pain:** Owners think only in terms of outright sale or single license.  
**Current Solution:** Use one default transaction model.  
**Cost of Pain:** Missed creative deal paths.  
**Your Solution:** Structure options including field-of-use, milestone, hybrid, and royalty deals.  
**Pricing:** Included in transaction fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Are you open to licensing, sale, joint venture, or milestone structures?
- What risk/reward tradeoff would make the portfolio attractive to you?

---

### 6. Dormant corporate portfolios leak value
**Pain:** Large companies sit on non-core patents they will never exploit.  
**Current Solution:** Pay maintenance fees and ignore them.  
**Cost of Pain:** Ongoing cost and zero return.  
**Your Solution:** Portfolio triage and monetization campaign for non-core assets.  
**Pricing:** Retainer plus success fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- How many patents are non-core but still maintained?
- What process exists today for monetization or divestiture?

---

### 7. Universities and labs have commercialization gaps
**Pain:** Strong science rarely becomes buyer-ready transaction material.  
**Current Solution:** Rely on overstretched tech transfer staff.  
**Cost of Pain:** Low licensing throughput.  
**Your Solution:** External buyer development and commercialization packaging.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$40K project fee plus success fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What stage of commercialization are most inventions stuck at?
- Which sectors have the most unrealized licensing potential?

---

### 8. Buyers do not want broad, unfiltered portfolios
**Pain:** Acquirers prefer curated assets tied to their strategic need.  
**Current Solution:** Share every patent in the portfolio.  
**Cost of Pain:** Low engagement and long diligence cycles.  
**Your Solution:** Narrow packages by application, geography, and use case.  
**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which subset of the portfolio is actually relevant to each buyer segment?
- How much pruning has been done before outreach?

**Pain:** Buyers want enough information to evaluate without legal friction.

**Cost of Pain:** Fewer first meetings.

**Pain:** Strategic buyers know they want innovation but lack an outbound acquisition engine.  
**Current Solution:** Wait for bankers, inbound, or conference networking.  
**Cost of Pain:** Missed strategic assets.  
**Your Solution:** Patent scouting and curated deal flow by technology theme.

**Discovery Questions:**

### 11. Litigation shadow distorts deal conversations
**Pain:** Some buyers assume any patent transaction is a pre-litigation threat.  
**Current Solution:** Avoid engagement or price defensively.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower close rates.  
**Your Solution:** Position deals around strategic value, freedom to operate, and commercialization.  
**Pricing:** Included.

**Discovery Questions:**

- What framing has made buyers more open historically?

### 12. Global filing complexity clouds value

**Current Solution:** Share raw docket lists.  
**Cost of Pain:** Buyer uncertainty and mispricing.  
**Your Solution:** Portfolio map by jurisdiction, expiry, and claim relevance.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which jurisdictions matter most commercially?
- How clearly can a buyer see term and territory value today?

### 13. Inventors do not have negotiation leverage alone

**Current Solution:** Go direct with minimal support.  
**Cost of Pain:** Weak terms and abandoned deals.

**Pricing:** Included in success fee.

### 14. Buyers need faster triage on inbound opportunities
**Pain:** Corp dev or legal teams cannot analyze every portfolio deeply.  
**Current Solution:** Ignore most inbound approaches.  
**Cost of Pain:** Good deals get missed.  
**Your Solution:** Buyer-ready teasers and short relevance memos.  
**Pricing:** Included or sell-side prep fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What format helps your team assess new IP fastest?
- What usually causes you to dismiss opportunities too early?

---

### 15. Patent owners do not build repeatable monetization systems
**Pain:** Monetization happens only when cash pressure spikes.  
**Current Solution:** Sporadic sale attempts.  
**Cost of Pain:** Portfolio value decays and opportunities expire.  
**Your Solution:** Annual review and monetization roadmap for strategic assets.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$25K portfolio strategy retainer.

**Discovery Questions:**

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Portfolios with unclear ownership or assignment history
- ❌ Sellers anchored to fantasy valuations
- ❌ Patents with weak commercial relevance and no buyer thesis
- ❌ Clients expecting instant transactions in slow-moving sectors
- ❌ Buyers looking only for distressed pricing with no strategic fit

**Best Prospects:**
- Corporations with non-core but still relevant patent assets
- Universities and inventors with strong patents but weak market access
- Strategic buyers with defined technology acquisition priorities

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We can handle this with our law firm."**
> "Your law firm protects legal quality. We increase commercial likelihood by packaging the asset for buyers and running the market process."

**"Patent deals take too long."**
> "They do take time, which is exactly why poor packaging and weak targeting are so costly. A disciplined process shortens the path to serious conversations."

**"We are not ready to sell outright."**
> "That is fine. Many strong outcomes use field-of-use, regional, milestone, or royalty-based structures instead of a full sale."

**"Our patents are too niche."**
> "Niche is not a problem if the buyer set is real and the commercial use case is specific. Broad but vague is usually worse."

**"Success fees are high."**
> "They are high relative to doing nothing, but not relative to idle IP, maintenance spend, and the value of a well-run buyer process."

**"We do not want to look litigious."**
> "A commercialization-led process does the opposite. It frames the transaction around strategic value, not threat."
