# Method & Process Licensing

**Category:** Licensing & IP  
**Sub-Category:** Method/Process Licensing  
**Entry Cost:** $25K-$100K  
**Timeline:** 3-12 months  
**Revenue Model:** $5K-$50K license fee + 3-8% revenue share

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Method and process licensing involves packaging proprietary business methodologies, frameworks, and operational systems into licensable intellectual property. This includes operating systems like EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System), Scaling Up, the Rockefeller Habits, as well as industry-specific methodologies for consulting, coaching, training, and professional services.

The model works by methodology creators licensing their systems to certified implementers, coaches, and consultants who use the IP to serve their own clients. Licensees pay upfront certification/training fees plus ongoing royalties based on their revenue from using the methodology.

**Market Size:** $25B business coaching and consulting market; $8B methodology licensing  
**Target:** Business consultants, executive coaches, training firms, industry specialists  
**Examples:** EOS Worldwide (5,000+ implementers), Scaling Up (2,000+ coaches), Sandler Training, ActionCOACH, The ONE Thing

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Inability to Scale Beyond Founder ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Successful consultants and coaches hit revenue ceiling ($500K-$2M) limited by their personal time and capacity; can't serve more clients without cloning themselves
**Current Solution:** Hire associates, raise prices, productize into courses/books, create group programs
**Cost of Pain:** $1M-$5M in unrealized annual revenue; founder burnout; business stagnation
**Your Solution:** Turn methodology into licensable IP with certified implementer network
**Pricing:** $5K-$50K certification fee + 5-10% revenue share

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What does your revenue look like now versus 2-3 years ago—are you still trading time for money?"
- "How many clients can you personally serve at one time? What's the hard ceiling?"
- "If you could serve 100x more clients without working 100x more hours, what would that mean for your business?"

---

### 2. Inconsistent Delivery Quality
**Pain:** As they scale with junior consultants or partners, methodology delivery becomes inconsistent, damaging reputation and results
**Current Solution:** Extensive training programs, detailed documentation, quality oversight, mystery shopping
**Cost of Pain:** 15-25% client churn due to poor implementation; reputational damage; legal liability exposure
**Your Solution:** Structured certification program with competency testing, ongoing assessment, and recertification requirements
**Pricing:** $2K-$5K per implementer annually for quality assurance program

---

### 3. IP Protection and Misappropriation
**Pain:** When training others in their methodology, IP gets copied, modified, or taught without authorization; difficult to enforce
**Current Solution:** Legal contracts, trademarks, trade secret protections, cease and desist enforcement
**Cost of Pain:** $100K-$500K in lost licensing revenue; brand dilution; competitive threats from former trainees
**Your Solution:** Comprehensive IP protection framework with licensing agreements, certification marks, and enforcement protocols
**Pricing:** $15K-$30K for IP protection setup + monitoring services

---

### 4. Certification Program Development
**Pain:** Creating a legitimate certification program requires curriculum development, assessment design, and ongoing management—expertise the founder lacks
**Current Solution:** Hire instructional designers, partner with universities, outsource to training companies
**Cost of Pain:** $75K-$250K to develop comprehensive program; 6-12 month delays; quality compromises
**Your Solution:** Turnkey certification program development with learning management, assessment tools, and credential management
**Pricing:** $30K-$75K program development + $1K/month platform

---

### 5. Finding and Vetting Licensees
**Pain:** Identifying qualified implementers who will represent the brand well and actually generate revenue is time-consuming and uncertain
**Current Solution:** Application processes, interviews, referral networks, trial periods
**Cost of Pain:** 30-50% of certified implementers never generate meaningful revenue; wasted training investment
**Your Solution:** Licensee screening platform with predictive success modeling, territory mapping, and pre-qualified candidate pool
**Pricing:** $3K-$8K per successful placement

---

### 6. Revenue Share Collection and Auditing
**Pain:** Tracking licensee revenue and collecting royalties is awkward, time-consuming, and often results in underpayment or disputes
**Current Solution:** Self-reporting with periodic audits, trust-based relationships, minimum guarantees
**Cost of Pain:** 10-20% underreporting; $25K-$75K annually in audit and collection costs; damaged relationships
**Your Solution:** Automated revenue tracking with CRM integration, transparent reporting, and blockchain audit trails
**Pricing:** 2-3% of collected royalties as administration fee

---

### 7. Territory Management Conflicts
**Pain:** Licensees compete for the same clients, encroach on each other's territories, or create channel conflict with the founder's direct business
**Current Solution:** Geographic territories, industry verticals, client size bands, non-compete agreements
**Cost of Pain:** $200K-$1M in lost revenue from conflicts; licensee turnover; legal disputes
**Your Solution:** AI-powered territory optimization with lead routing, conflict resolution protocols, and dynamic market allocation
**Pricing:** $500-$1,500 per licensee annually

---

### 8. Brand Coherence Across Network
**Pain:** As the implementer network grows, brand messaging, visual identity, and market positioning become inconsistent
**Current Solution:** Brand guidelines, approved marketing materials, periodic brand audits, style enforcement
**Cost of Pain:** Brand dilution; confused market; 20-30% inefficiency in marketing spend
**Your Solution:** Centralized brand management platform with templated materials, co-op advertising, and brand monitoring
**Pricing:** $2K-$5K setup + $300/month per licensee

---

### 9. Continuing Education and Updates
**Pain:** Methodologies evolve, but keeping all implementers current is logistically challenging and expensive
**Current Solution:** Annual conferences, online updates, recertification requirements, newsletter communications
**Cost of Pain:** $50K-$150K annually in update delivery; implementers using outdated methods; client confusion
**Your Solution:** Continuous learning platform with micro-updates, version control, and automated compliance tracking
**Pricing:** $1K-$3K per implementer annually

---

### 10. Marketing and Lead Generation for Licensees
**Pain:** Individual implementers struggle with marketing and business development; many fail due to lack of clients, not methodology issues
**Current Solution:** Shared marketing resources, referral networks, business development training, co-op advertising
**Cost of Pain:** 40-60% implementer attrition within 2 years; $100K-$300K investment lost on failed implementers
**Your Solution:** Integrated lead generation platform with shared marketing, inbound lead distribution, and sales enablement tools
**Pricing:** $500-$2K per month per implementer

---

### 11. Community Building and Network Effects
**Pain:** Licensees feel isolated; lack of peer connection reduces retention, idea sharing, and collective learning
**Current Solution:** Annual conferences, online forums, regional meetups, mastermind groups
**Cost of Pain:** 25-35% higher attrition; missed innovation opportunities; weaker competitive position
**Your Solution:** Comprehensive community platform with networking, case study sharing, peer mentoring, and collaborative problem-solving
**Pricing:** $200-$500 per implementer annually

---

### 12. Legal and Compliance Complexity
**Pain:** Multi-jurisdictional licensing creates employment law, tax, and regulatory complexity that most founders are unprepared for
**Current Solution:** Franchise attorneys, compliance consultants, restrictive agreements to avoid franchise classification
**Cost of Pain:** $75K-$200K in legal fees; risk of accidental franchise classification; operational restrictions
**Your Solution:** Compliance framework distinguishing licensing from franchising with jurisdiction-specific agreement templates
**Pricing:** $25K-$50K for legal framework + $5K annually per jurisdiction

---

### 13. Methodology Evolution and Version Control
**Pain:** Improving the methodology while maintaining consistency across hundreds of implementers is operationally complex
**Current Solution:** Annual updates, version numbering, grandfather clauses, mandatory retraining
**Cost of Pain:** Client confusion from version differences; $100K-$300K annually in update management; implementer resistance
**Your Solution:** Methodology version control system with backward compatibility, migration tools, and gradual rollout management
**Pricing:** $5K-$15K per major update + $200 per implementer

---

### 14. Competitive Differentiation
**Pain:** As more methodologies enter the market (EOS clones, Scaling Up alternatives), standing out becomes increasingly difficult
**Current Solution:** Brand building, thought leadership, case studies, unique positioning, vertical specialization
**Cost of Pain:** 20-30% price pressure; commoditization; increased CAC
**Your Solution:** Competitive intelligence platform, positioning optimization, and unique methodology IP development
**Pricing:** $10K-$25K positioning project + ongoing competitive monitoring

---

### 15. Exit and Succession Planning
**Pain:** Founders want to build sellable asset but licensing businesses are often too dependent on the founder's personal brand and relationships
**Current Solution:** Brand separation initiatives, management team development, earn-out structures
**Cost of Pain:** 30-50% valuation discount due to key person dependency; failed exits; trapped founders
**Your Solution:** Founder independence roadmap with brand transition, succession planning, and enterprise value maximization
**Pricing:** $50K-$150K consulting engagement

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Methodologies without proven results or case studies
- ❌ Founders unwilling to systematize their "secret sauce"
- ❌ Consultants with ego-driven resistance to delegating client work
- ❌ Markets with excessive competition or commoditized methodologies
- ❌ Founders expecting passive income without ongoing engagement

**Best Prospects:**
- Successful consultants with $500K+ revenue seeking scale
- Methodologies with 3+ years of proven client results
- Founders with teaching/training aptitude and patience
- Niches with clear pain points and willingness to pay
- Existing client base that could become licensee candidates

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"My methodology is too unique/complex to systematize"**
> "That's what every successful method creator thinks initially. But consider EOS—Gino Wickman spent 20 years developing something deeply personal, yet now 5,000+ implementers deliver it consistently. The key is identifying the non-negotiable core versus the customizable context. What parts of your method do you consider truly impossible to teach others?"

**"I don't want to lose control of quality"**
> "That's the right concern—quality is everything. Our certification framework includes competency testing, ongoing assessments, mystery shopping, and recertification requirements. You'll have more visibility and control than you do now with informal associates. How do you currently ensure quality when you're not personally delivering?"

**"Won't my best people just steal my IP and compete?"**
> "That's a risk in any knowledge business, but licensing actually reduces it. With proper IP protection, trademarked frameworks, and contractual agreements, you have more legal protection than informal training. Plus, our tracking systems make unauthorized use easier to detect. Have you had IP issues with people you've trained informally?"

**"I tried hiring associates and it didn't work"**
> "Associates and licensees are fundamentally different. Associates work FOR you; licensees work WITH you using your IP. The motivation, investment, and commitment are entirely different. Licensees pay you $25K-$50K upfront—they're invested in success. What went wrong with your associate model?"

**"The legal complexity sounds overwhelming"**
> "It can be, which is why we provide a complete compliance framework distinguishing licensing from franchising. We've helped 50+ method creators navigate this successfully. The key is structure and documentation—things we'll build with you. What's your biggest concern about the legal side?"

**"I make good money now—why go through all this complexity?"**
> "If you're satisfied with your current income and impact, you may not need this. But most founders we work with hit a ceiling: more clients means less sleep, higher prices means smaller market, and they have no sellable asset. If you ever want to exit or scale beyond yourself, licensing is the path. What's your 5-year vision for the business?"

## 📞 Discovery Questions

**To Methodology Creator / Founder:**
- "Walk me through your current business model—how do you make money today?"
- "What does a typical engagement look like, and how many can you handle simultaneously?"
- "Have you trained others in your methodology? What happened?"
- "What percentage of your revenue is tied to your personal delivery versus team/associates?"
- "If you stopped working tomorrow, how much revenue would continue?"
- "What's your vision for the business in 5 years—are you still delivering personally?"

**To Operations/Finance:**
- "How do you track and collect revenue from any licensed or referred work today?"
- "What systems do you use for training delivery and documentation?"
- "How much are you spending on associate management and quality control?"

**To Current Implementers/Associates:**
- "How did you learn the methodology? Was the training sufficient?"
- "What support do you get for finding and serving clients?"
- "What would make you more successful as an implementer?"
