# Health Wellness Coaching Membership

**Category:** Expert Memberships  
**Sub-Category:** Health Wellness Coaching  
**Entry Cost:** $2K-$8K  
**Timeline:** 1-2 months  
**Revenue Model:** $100-$300/month membership

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Create an ongoing coaching membership for a specific wellness outcome such as metabolic health, sustainable fat loss, strength habits, stress regulation, or nutrition accountability. The offer wins when it combines expert guidance with weekly accountability and realistic behavior change support.

This category is strongest when it stays narrow and outcome-driven. General wellness is crowded. Specific populations and problems convert better and retain longer.

**Market Size:** $1T+ wellness market with strong recurring coaching demand  
**Target:** Adults with clear health goals, busy professionals, parents, or niche condition groups  
**Examples:** nutrition memberships, habit-based fitness coaching, accountability coaching communities

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** Adult seeking health change without extreme programs  
**Secondary:** Spouse, employer wellness stipend, physician referral source  
**Decision Maker:** Individual buyer

**Budget:** $1.2K-$3.6K annually  
**Pain Urgency:** High because health pain is personal, emotional, and persistent

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Inconsistent Follow-Through ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** They know what to do but do not stick with it.  
**Current Solution:** Start diets, apps, and challenges repeatedly.  
**Cost of Pain:** Years of stalled progress and worsening health markers.  
**Your Solution:** Weekly accountability, habit tracking, and personalized check-ins.  
**Pricing:** $150-$300/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What health change do you keep restarting?"
- "Where does your consistency usually break?"

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"Stop restarting your health every Monday and build a system you can actually live with."

### Authority Builders
1. Simple health audit or scorecard
2. Habit reset guide
3. Client case studies with realistic timelines
4. Weekly Q and A or coaching clinic

### Positioning Angles
- **Sustainable over extreme:** Built for real life.
- **Specific outcome coaching:** Narrower and more credible.
- **Accountability as the product:** Knowledge is not the missing piece.

### Differentiators
- High-touch accountability
- Evidence-informed but practical
- Behavior change built around real constraints

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Buyers wanting medical treatment beyond coaching scope
- ❌ Prospects expecting drastic transformation with no habit change
- ❌ People chasing every new wellness trend
- ❌ Clients unwilling to track anything at all
- ❌ Serious conditions requiring clinical oversight only

**Best Prospects:**
- Clear goal with repeated failed attempts
- Willing to engage weekly
- Need consistency more than information
- Value personalized support

---

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"I already know what to do."**
> "That is often true. The real gap is doing it consistently in your actual life, especially when stress and schedule hit."

**"I can use an app for much less."**
> "Apps can remind. They usually cannot adapt, notice your patterns, or help you recover when life knocks you off track."

**"I do not want another restrictive program."**
> "Good. The model works best when it helps you build durable habits, not when it imposes a short extreme sprint."

**"I never stick with these things."**
> "That is exactly the problem the membership is built to solve. The offer is not more information. It is structure, accountability, and adjustment."

**"This feels expensive."**
> "Compared with another PDF, yes. Compared with years of false starts, low energy, and avoidable health decline, it is modest."

**"What if I fall behind?"**
> "Falling behind is part of real behavior change. The system should make recovery easy instead of making you feel like you failed."
