## 🎯 The Opportunity

Construction is a $1.8 trillion industry where timing is everything. Suppliers, contractors, equipment vendors, and manufacturers need to know about projects 6-18 months before construction starts—when decisions about materials, equipment, and subcontractors are being made. Yet most rely on word-of-mouth, delayed Dodge reports, or discovering projects when it's too late to influence specifications.

The opportunity is to aggregate construction project data from permit filings, planning departments, architectural firms, and proprietary sources—then deliver early-stage pipeline intelligence. Target building material suppliers, construction equipment vendors, MEP contractors, and specialty subcontractors who need advance visibility to influence specifications and secure work.

**Market Size:** $4B+ construction data market, growing 10% annually  
**Target:** Building material suppliers, equipment vendors, subcontractors, manufacturers  
**Examples:** Dodge Data & Analytics, ConstructConnect, BuildingConnected, Blue Book, eSUB

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Construction Projects Are Discovered Too Late ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** By the time most construction projects appear on public bidding sites, key decisions about materials, equipment, and subcontractors are already made. The 6-18 month pre-construction planning phase is when influence matters most.
**Current Solution:** Dodge Reports (delayed, expensive), plan rooms (late stage), relationships with architects/GCs, waiting for RFPs
**Cost of Pain:** Missing 70-80% of relevant projects; inability to influence specifications; competing only on price at bid time
**Your Solution:** Early-stage project tracker with permit-to-construction timeline and specification influence windows
**Pricing:** $500-$800/month for project pipeline

**Discovery Questions:**
- "When do you typically find out about new construction projects in your area?"
- "How many months before construction starts would be ideal for you to know about a project?"
- "What percentage of projects do you think you miss entirely with your current process?"

---

### 2. Key Decision-Maker Identification Is Difficult
**Pain:** Even when projects are known, identifying the right people to contact—architects specifying products, owners making decisions, GCs awarding subs—is challenging and time-consuming.
**Current Solution:** Plan room contact lists (limited), networking, calling general contractors, LinkedIn searching
**Cost of Pain:** Calling on wrong people; delayed access to decision-makers; lost specification opportunities
**Your Solution:** Project contact database with role identification and decision-making authority scoring
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for contact intelligence

---

### 3. Project Specifications Are Unknown Until Too Late
**Pain:** Understanding what materials, equipment, or systems are specified on a project requires waiting for bid documents—but by then, specs are locked and hard to change.
**Current Solution:** Plan rooms at bid phase, waiting for contractor inquiries, architect relationships (limited coverage)
**Cost of Pain:** Inability to influence specifications; competing as commodity against specified products; price-only competition
**Your Solution:** Early specification tracking with material/equipment prediction and substitution opportunity identification
**Pricing:** $600-$900/month for specification intelligence

---

### 4. Competitor Project Involvement Is Opaque
**Pain:** Knowing which competitors are bidding, specifying, or have relationships on specific projects helps prioritize opportunities and develop counter-strategies. This intelligence is rarely available.
**Current Solution:** Rumor, plan room observation, contractor disclosure (limited), post-bid learning
**Cost of Pain:** Competing blindly; missed relationship opportunities; poor bid strategy
**Your Solution:** Competitive project tracking with bidder identification and win probability analysis
**Pricing:** $500-$800/month for competitive intelligence

### 5. Project Value and Scope Estimation Is Inaccurate
**Pain:** Determining if a project is worth pursuing requires understanding budget, scope, and scale—but early-stage projects lack this detail and current estimation methods are unreliable.
**Current Solution:** Square footage estimates, permit value (incomplete), contractor disclosure, guessing
**Cost of Pain:** Pursuing undersized projects; missing large opportunities; poor resource allocation
**Your Solution:** AI-powered project valuation with scope estimation and priority scoring
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for project analytics

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We already use Dodge Reports—why would we need this?"**
> "Dodge is the industry standard and provides excellent data. The challenge is timing—Dodge projects often appear 2-4 months before construction, when many specifications are already locked. We capture projects at the planning and permitting phase, 6-12 months earlier when you can still influence specs. Many of our customers actually use both—us for early pipeline development and Dodge for detailed bid documents. At 1/3 the cost of a full Dodge subscription, we're complementary rather than competitive."

**"How do you find projects earlier than Dodge?"**
> "We monitor different signals: planning department submissions, architectural firm project announcements, environmental impact filings, zoning applications, and early-stage permits. These events happen 6-18 months before construction starts, well ahead of when projects appear in bid-focused databases. We're not always first—sometimes Dodge beats us—but on average we identify 40% more projects and do so 4-6 months earlier. We also track projects that never reach bidding but are negotiated directly with GCs."

**"What if the project cancels or delays?"**
> "Project attrition is real—about 20-30% of early-stage projects never break ground or delay significantly. That's why we built status monitoring into our platform. We track permit expirations, planning extensions, news about financing delays, and architectural firm updates. When a project stalls, we alert you immediately so you can reallocate effort. This actually saves you time compared to chasing dead leads you found elsewhere. Our customers tell us the early intelligence is worth the 20-30% attrition rate."

**"How accurate is your project valuation?"**
> "Early-stage valuation is inherently uncertain—we're estimating based on square footage, project type, location, and permit values. We provide ranges rather than point estimates and clearly mark confidence levels. For a 50,000 sq ft office building, we might estimate $8M-$12M construction value with medium confidence. As projects progress and more data becomes available, we refine estimates. The value isn't perfect precision—it's directional prioritization so you know this is a $10M project worth pursuing vs. a $500K project that isn't."

**"Our business is relationship-based—will data really help?"**
> "Relationships are absolutely the foundation of construction sales. Data makes relationships more productive. Instead of calling your GC contacts asking 'What projects are you bidding?', you call saying 'I see you're bidding the Metro Medical Center project—how can we support your mechanical scope?' The relationship gets you the meeting; data makes the meeting productive. One customer increased their specification success rate by 35% by combining their existing relationships with our early project intelligence."
