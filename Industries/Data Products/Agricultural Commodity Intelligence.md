# Agricultural Commodity Intelligence

**Category:** Data Products  
**Sub-Category:** Agricultural Analytics  
**Entry Cost:** $15,000-$30,000  
**Timeline:** 2-3 months to first revenue  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly subscription with commodity coverage tiers

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Agriculture is a $1 trillion industry where margins are thin and volatility is high. Farmers, grain elevators, commodity traders, and agribusinesses make critical decisions about planting, pricing, hedging, and logistics without adequate data on local prices, weather impacts, and supply forecasts. Current solutions are either expensive professional platforms (Bloomberg, Reuters) or simplistic tools lacking local market intelligence.

The opportunity is to aggregate commodity prices, weather data, supply forecasts, and local market signals—then deliver actionable intelligence for farmers marketing grain, elevators managing basis, and traders making positions. Target mid-size farms, regional elevators, and commodity advisors who need professional-grade data at accessible prices.

**Market Size:** $3B+ agricultural data market, growing 11% annually  
**Target:** Mid-size farms, grain elevators, commodity trading advisors, agribusinesses  
**Examples:** DTN, Barchart, FarmLogs (BASF), Farmers Business Network, Gro Intelligence

---

**Primary Buyer:** Farm Owner/Operator, Grain Merchandiser, Commodity Trading Advisor  
**Secondary:** Agronomists, risk managers, elevator managers, input suppliers  
**Decision Maker:** Farm owner, Elevator manager, Principal at trading firm

**Budget:** $200-$600/month

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Local Basis Pricing Is Opaque ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Farmers and elevators need to understand local basis (difference between local cash price and futures)—the key to marketing decisions—but local bids change constantly and historical data is scarce.
**Current Solution:** Calling multiple elevators, local ag radio, delayed USDA reports, spreadsheets with limited history
**Cost of Pain:** Selling grain 10-20 cents/bushel below optimal price; missed basis improvement opportunities; $5K-$20K revenue loss per 10,000 bushels
**Your Solution:** Real-time local basis tracking with historical analysis and optimal timing recommendations
**Pricing:** $300-$500/month for basis intelligence

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you currently track basis levels at different buyers in your area?"
- "What's the range you've seen basis move in a given marketing year?"
- "How much do you think suboptimal timing costs you per year in grain marketing?"

---

### 2. Weather Impact Modeling Is Inaccessible to Most
**Pain:** Weather is the single biggest factor in crop yields, but sophisticated weather analytics require expertise and expensive data that most farmers and elevators don't have.
**Current Solution:** Weather apps (general), crop reports (delayed), agronomist advice, gut instinct
**Cost of Pain:** Poor planting/harvest timing; inadequate crop insurance coverage; yield surprises costing $10K-$100K+
**Your Solution:** Crop-specific weather analytics with yield impact modeling and risk forecasting
**Pricing:** $250-$450/month for weather intelligence

---

### 3. Supply and Demand Forecasts Are Delayed
**Pain:** USDA reports provide supply/demand data but with significant delays and revisions. By the time data is published, markets have moved. Real-time supply forecasting is valuable but expensive.
**Current Solution:** USDA WASDE reports (monthly), private forecasters (expensive), analyst reports (delayed)
**Cost of Pain:** Trading and marketing decisions on stale data; missed market moves; poor hedging timing
**Your Solution:** Real-time supply modeling using satellite imagery, weather data, and export tracking with forecast updates
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for supply forecasting

---

### 4. Export Demand Tracking Is Fragmented
**Pain:** Understanding export demand—where grain is going, at what pace, and what's competing—is critical for price forecasting but data is scattered across sources.
**Current Solution:** USDA export sales reports (weekly), shipping news, analyst commentary, cargo tracking (manual)
**Cost of Pain:** Missing demand shifts; poor export timing; inadequate supply allocation
**Your Solution:** Unified export tracker with sales pace, destination analysis, and competitor monitoring
**Pricing:** $350-$600/month for export intelligence

---

### 5. Elevator Bid Aggregation Is Manual
**Pain:** Farmers selling grain need to compare bids across multiple elevators, processors, and end users—but collecting and comparing real-time bids is tedious.
**Current Solution:** Calling multiple buyers, elevator websites (rarely updated), local networks, accepting first reasonable bid
**Cost of Pain:** Selling at 5-15 cents/bushel below best available price; $2,500-$7,500 loss per 50,000 bushels
**Your Solution:** Aggregated bid board with real-time prices, basis comparison, and optimal buyer matching
**Pricing:** $200-$400/month for bid aggregation

---

### 6. Grain Storage Decision Optimization Is Complex
**Pain:** Deciding whether to store grain and for how long requires modeling carry, basis improvement, interest costs, and quality risk—calculations most farmers don't do systematically.
**Current Solution:** Rules of thumb, elevator storage programs, marketing advisors (expensive), guessing
**Cost of Pain:** Selling too early and missing storage returns; storing too long and losing money; $3K-$15K annual suboptimization
**Your Solution:** Storage optimizer with carry analysis, basis forecasting, and break-even calculations
**Pricing:** $250-$450/month for storage analytics

---

### 7. Cash Price Forecasting Lacks Local Granularity
**Pain:** Futures price forecasts are widely available, but cash price forecasting requires basis prediction—highly local and harder to model.
**Current Solution:** Futures forecasts plus basis guesswork, advisor opinions, historical averages
**Cost of Pain:** Poor marketing timing; missed price peaks; panic selling at lows
**Your Solution:** Local cash price forecasting with basis prediction and confidence intervals
**Pricing:** $350-$600/month for price forecasting

---

### 8. Input Price Tracking Is Disconnected from Output
**Pain:** Farmers make input purchasing decisions (seed, fertilizer, chemicals) without connecting them to expected output prices—making true margin calculation impossible.
**Current Solution:** Input supplier quotes, separate from grain marketing, no integrated margin view
**Cost of Pain:** Poor input timing; compressed margins; inability to lock in profitable margins
**Your Solution:** Integrated margin tracker connecting input costs to output pricing with profit opportunity alerts
**Pricing:** $300-$500/month for margin management

---

### 9. Crop Condition Monitoring Is Subjective
**Pain:** Understanding how crops are developing relative to normal—critical for yield forecasting and marketing—requires objective data that most lack.
**Current Solution:** Field walking, crop tours (expensive), USDA condition reports (subjective), neighbor observations
**Cost of Pain:** Yield forecast errors leading to marketing mistakes; poor crop insurance decisions
**Your Solution:** Satellite-based crop condition monitoring with vegetation indices and yield modeling
**Pricing:** $250-$450/month for crop monitoring

---

### 10. Freight and Logistics Optimization Is Overlooked
**Pain:** Getting grain from farm to market involves complex freight decisions—elevator vs. direct, truck vs. rail, timing—that significantly impact net price.
**Current Solution:** Elevator recommendations, freight broker quotes (spotty), historical patterns, accepting default option
**Cost of Pain:** 5-15 cent/bushel freight premium; missed backhaul opportunities; inefficient logistics
**Your Solution:** Freight optimizer with route comparison, backhaul matching, and delivery timing recommendations
**Pricing:** $300-$500/month for logistics intelligence

---

### 11. Options and Hedging Strategy Is Complex
**Pain:** Managing price risk through options, futures, and hedging requires expertise most farmers lack. Mistakes are expensive and confidence is low.
**Current Solution:** Broker recommendations (biased), expensive advisors, avoiding hedging entirely, simple cash sales
**Cost of Pain:** Unhedged price risk costing $10K-$50K in bad years; poorly structured hedges that don't protect as intended
**Your Solution:** Decision-support tools for hedging with scenario analysis and strategy recommendations
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for risk management tools

---

### 12. Quality Premium and Discount Tracking
**Pain:** Understanding quality factors (protein, test weight, moisture) and their impact on price premiums/discounts varies by buyer and changes with supply conditions.
**Current Solution:** Buyer specifications (varies), local knowledge, occasional testing, accepting standard grade
**Cost of Pain:** Missing premium opportunities; unexpected discounts; poor variety selection for next season
**Your Solution:** Quality premium tracker with buyer comparison and optimal delivery timing
**Pricing:** $200-$350/month for quality intelligence

---

### 13. Acreage and Planting Intention Surveys Are Limited
**Pain:** Understanding what farmers are planting—critical for supply forecasting—is based on USDA surveys with limited scope and delays.
**Current Solution:** USDA Prospective Plantings (annual), anecdotal reports, crop tours, guessing
**Cost of Pain:** Supply forecast errors; poor marketing timing; missed rotational pattern shifts
**Your Solution:** Real-time acreage tracking using satellite detection, equipment sales data, and input orders
**Pricing:** $350-$600/month for planting intelligence

---

### 14. International Supply and Competition Monitoring
**Pain:** Global grain markets are interconnected, but monitoring international production (Brazil, Argentina, Ukraine, Russia) requires expertise and data access most don't have.
**Current Solution:** USDA world production reports (delayed), analyst reports (expensive), news monitoring
**Cost of Pain:** Missing global supply shifts; inadequate export competition analysis; poor price forecasting
**Your Solution:** Global supply tracker with major producer monitoring and export competition analysis
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for global intelligence

---

### 15. Historical Performance and Benchmarking Is Lost
**Pain:** Farmers don't systematically track their marketing performance vs. benchmarks—did they beat the average, time the market well, optimize basis?
**Current Solution:** Memory, spreadsheets (if maintained), no systematic comparison to benchmarks
**Cost of Pain:** Repeated mistakes; inability to improve marketing; overconfidence or excessive regret
**Your Solution:** Marketing performance tracker with benchmark comparison and improvement recommendations
**Pricing:** $200-$350/month for performance analytics

- ❌ Speculators without underlying commodity exposure
- ❌ Users requiring 100% accurate forecasts (impossible)

**Best Prospects:**
- Commercial grain farms with 2,000+ acres

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"I get all this from my elevator/grain buyer."**
> "Your elevator provides valuable service, but remember—they're the buyer. Their incentives are to buy your grain at prices favorable to them. We have no position in the market—we just provide data. Many of our customers work with their elevator AND use our data to verify they're getting competitive bids. One farmer discovered his regular elevator was consistently 8-12 cents below the best bid in the area—costing him $4,000+ annually. Use your relationships, but verify with data."

**"How is this different from DTN or Barchart?"**
> "DTN and Barchart are excellent for futures quotes and news—we reference them ourselves. Where we differentiate is local intelligence: basis tracking at specific elevators, cash bid aggregation in your county, and grain marketing decision tools. Think of them as the broad market data; we're the local marketing intelligence. Most serious marketers use both—DTN for futures and news, us for cash marketing execution."

**"I don't have time to analyze all this data."**
> "You're right—farmers are busy during planting and harvest. That's why we built alert-driven workflows instead of data dumps. We send you only what matters: basis opportunities at your local buyers, storage break-even notifications, optimal marketing windows. Most customers spend 10 minutes per day in our app, mostly checking alerts. We also offer text/SMS alerts for urgent opportunities. The goal is actionable intelligence, not more data to process."

**"Can you guarantee I'll make more money?"**
> "No—we provide information, not guarantees. What we can say is that farmers who systematically track basis, compare bids, and use price forecasting typically capture 10-20 cents more per bushel than those who don't. On 50,000 bushels, that's $5K-$10K. At $400/month ($4,800/year), you need to capture just 5-10 cents more per bushel to break even. Most customers see significantly better results than that, but your results depend on how you use the information."

**"This seems expensive compared to free weather apps."**
> "Free weather apps tell you if it's going to rain tomorrow. We tell you how that rain affects your specific crop development, yield potential, and local basis. We integrate weather with crop modeling, soil conditions, and satellite imagery to predict impact. One customer avoided a $15K loss by using our yield forecasts to adjust their crop insurance coverage before the deadline. That's 3 years of subscription cost saved in one decision."
