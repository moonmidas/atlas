# Job Market Analytics

**Category:** Data Products  
**Sub-Category:** HR Technology / Labor Market Intelligence  
**Entry Cost:** $20,000-$40,000  
**Timeline:** 3-4 months to first revenue  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly subscription with tiered access levels

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Recruiters, HR leaders, and workforce planners operate in an information vacuum. They need to understand salary benchmarks, hiring velocity, skill demand shifts, and labor market trends to make competitive offers, plan headcount, and advise business leaders. Yet most rely on stale salary surveys, anecdotal evidence, or expensive consulting reports that arrive quarterly.

The opportunity is to aggregate real-time job market data from job boards, professional networks, company career pages, and public filings—then package it into actionable intelligence for recruiters setting compensation, HR planning workforce strategy, and staffing firms identifying hot markets. Unlike traditional salary surveys that lag by 6-12 months, this data is current and granular.

**Market Size:** $24B+ HR analytics market, growing 14% annually  
**Target:** Mid-market recruiters, HR Business Partners, workforce planning teams, staffing firms  
**Examples:** Lightcast (formerly Emsi Burning Glass), TalentNeuron (Gartner), Compdata, Payscale

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Salary Benchmarks Are Stale and Inaccurate ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Traditional salary surveys are published annually or semi-annually, reflecting data that's 6-12 months old. In a market where tech salaries moved 20%+ in 2021-2022, stale data leads to uncompetitive offers.
**Current Solution:** Salary surveys (Radford, Mercer), Payscale reports, Glassdoor (self-reported, unreliable), recruiter intuition
**Cost of Pain:** Lost candidates due to low offers (costing $50K+ per failed search); overpayment inflating payroll 10-20%
**Your Solution:** Real-time salary benchmarks updated weekly from actual job postings and offer data
**Pricing:** $400-$800/month for core benchmarking

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How current is the salary data you're using when making offers right now?"
- "Walk me through the last time you lost a candidate to a competitor's higher offer—did you see it coming?"
- "What would it be worth to know the market moved 10% before your next compensation review?"

---

### 2. Time-to-Fill Forecasting Is Pure Guesswork
**Pain:** Recruiters can't predict how long roles will take to fill, leading to poor capacity planning and missed hiring targets. They rely on historical averages that don't account for current market conditions.
**Current Solution:** Historical averages, recruiter estimates, gut instinct
**Cost of Pain:** Missed hiring targets delaying product launches; emergency agency fees at 20-30% of salary; recruiter burnout from unrealistic expectations
**Your Solution:** AI-powered time-to-fill forecasting based on current market demand, supply, and seasonality
**Pricing:** $300-$600/month for forecasting module

---

### 3. Skill Demand Shifts Go Unnoticed Until Too Late
**Pain:** The skills companies need evolve constantly (AI, cloud, cybersecurity), but HR and recruiters lack early warning when demand spikes for emerging skills. They're always playing catch-up.
**Current Solution:** Industry reports, conference presentations, LinkedIn trend articles, hiring manager complaints
**Cost of Pain:** Skill gaps delaying projects; paying premium for scarce talent; training investments in declining skills
**Your Solution:** Emerging skills tracker with demand velocity, supply scarcity, and salary impact analysis
**Pricing:** $500-$900/month for skills intelligence

---

### 4. Competitor Hiring Activity Is Opaque
**Pain:** Understanding which competitors are hiring, for what roles, in what locations, and at what scale provides strategic intelligence. Most companies have zero visibility into competitor workforce strategy.
**Current Solution:** Monitoring competitor career pages, LinkedIn job alerts, industry gossip
**Cost of Pain:** Surprise competitive moves (new product lines, expansions); inability to preemptively retain talent; lost competitive intelligence
**Your Solution:** Competitor hiring tracker with role analysis, location mapping, and hiring velocity
**Pricing:** $600-$1,000/month for competitive intelligence

---

### 5. Location Strategy Lacks Labor Market Data
**Pain:** Companies opening offices or going remote need to understand labor supply, salary costs, and competition by metro area. They rely on outdated cost-of-living data rather than actual talent market dynamics.
**Current Solution:** Cost-of-living calculators, real estate data, limited salary surveys by geography
**Cost of Pain:** Poor location decisions costing $1M+ in unrealized savings or talent shortages; overpayment in low-cost markets
**Your Solution:** Labor market heat maps showing talent supply, demand, salary ranges, and competition by geography
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for location intelligence

---

### 6. Diversity Hiring Progress Is Hard to Measure
**Pain:** Companies set DEI goals but lack data on diversity in the talent pipeline, competitor diversity hiring rates, or market availability of diverse talent by role and geography.
**Current Solution:** Self-reported diversity data, EEOC filings (delayed), consultant diversity studies ($25K+)
**Cost of Pain:** Unrealistic diversity goals; misallocation of DEI budget; inability to benchmark progress
**Your Solution:** Diversity pipeline analytics with market availability benchmarks and competitor comparison
**Pricing:** $500-$900/month for DEI analytics module

---

### 7. Layoff and Hiring Freeze Intelligence Is Critical
**Pain:** Knowing when competitors or clients are freezing hiring or conducting layoffs creates opportunity to hire top talent and understand market health. This intelligence arrives too late through news.
**Current Solution:** News monitoring, LinkedIn posts from affected employees, recruiter networks
**Cost of Pain:** Missed window to recruit laid-off talent; continued aggressive hiring into softening market
**Your Solution:** Real-time layoff and hiring freeze tracking with affected role and talent identification
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month included in core platform

---

### 8. Remote Work Policy Benchmarking Is Nonexistent
**Pain:** Companies establishing remote/hybrid policies lack data on what competitors offer. They guess at competitive positioning and often get it wrong.
**Current Solution:** Anecdotal evidence, occasional industry surveys, Glassdoor reviews
**Cost of Pain:** Talent loss to more flexible competitors; overpermissive policies reducing collaboration; underpermissive policies causing attrition
**Your Solution:** Remote work policy tracker by company size, industry, and role type with trend analysis
**Pricing:** $300-$500/month for policy benchmarking

---

### 9. Recruiter Capacity Planning Is Reactive
**Pain:** Talent acquisition leaders can't predict recruiter workload based on market conditions. They staff for average loads and scramble during spikes or idle during slowdowns.
**Current Solution:** Historical requisition volumes, recruiter-to-req ratios, budget cycles
**Cost of Pain:** Recruiter burnout during spikes; wasted payroll during slowdowns; inconsistent candidate experience
**Your Solution:** Market-driven capacity planning with demand forecasting and recruiter load balancing
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for planning tools

---

### 10. Compensation Equity Audits Are Expensive and Infrequent
**Pain:** Companies need to audit pay equity regularly but rely on expensive consultants ($50K-$200K per study) and conduct them annually at best.
**Current Solution:** Consultant-led pay equity studies, internal HR analysis (limited data), EEOC reporting
**Cost of Pain:** Legal risk from undetected pay disparities; annual consultant costs; reactive rather than proactive equity management
**Your Solution:** Continuous pay equity monitoring with market benchmarks and internal comparison
**Pricing:** $600-$1,000/month for equity analytics

---

### 11. Industry Talent Flows Are Invisible
**Pain:** Understanding where talent is moving—from which companies, to which companies, and why—provides competitive intelligence. Most companies have no visibility into these patterns.
**Current Solution:** LinkedIn Premium (limited), exit interview data, industry networking
**Cost of Pain:** Inability to identify top sources of talent; missing early warning of talent drains; poor employer branding strategy
**Your Solution:** Talent flow analytics showing movement between companies, industries, and geographies
**Pricing:** $700-$1,200/month for talent flow module

---

### 12. Campus Recruiting Targets Are Arbitrary
**Pain:** Companies target universities for campus recruiting based on reputation rather than data on graduate supply, competition, and conversion rates by school.
**Current Solution:** University rankings, anecdotal success, relationships with career centers
**Cost of Pain:** Wasted campus recruiting spend ($50K-$200K per year); missed opportunities at underrated schools; poor diversity outcomes
**Your Solution:** Campus recruiting intelligence with graduate supply, competition levels, and ROI by university
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for university analytics

---

### 13. Contract vs. Full-Time Mix Optimization Lacks Data
**Pain:** Companies deciding between contractors and full-time employees lack data on contractor rates, availability, and typical engagement durations for specific skills.
**Current Solution:** Staffing agency quotes, occasional rate surveys, intuition
**Cost of Pain:** Overpayment for contractors; missed cost savings from strategic contractor use; compliance risk from misclassification
**Your Solution:** Contractor rate benchmarking with availability trends and engagement analytics
**Pricing:** $400-$700/month for contingent workforce intelligence

---

### 14. Hard-to-Fill Role Identification Is Reactive
**Pain:** Recruiters and hiring managers discover roles are hard to fill only after posting them and seeing low applicant flow. No proactive identification of talent scarcity.
**Current Solution:** Posting and seeing what happens, recruiter intuition, occasional market research
**Cost of Pain:** Delayed projects from slow hiring; excessive agency fees for emergency fills; poor candidate experience from rushed process
**Your Solution:** Pre-posting difficulty scoring based on market supply/demand and historical data
**Pricing:** $300-$500/month included in core platform

---

### 15. Executive Search Benchmarking Is Opaque
**Pain:** Companies conducting executive searches lack data on typical search durations, compensation ranges, and candidate availability for C-level and VP roles.
**Current Solution:** Executive search firm advice (biased), board member intuition, limited industry reports
**Cost of Pain:** Extended search timelines delaying strategic initiatives; overpayment for executive talent; failed searches costing $500K+
**Your Solution:** Executive search intelligence with market availability, compensation benchmarks, and timeline forecasting
**Pricing:** $800-$1,500/month for executive module

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Small companies with <50 employees (not enough hiring volume)
- ❌ Companies using only standard, non-competitive salary bands
- ❌ HR teams without analytics sophistication

- ❌ Staffing firms focused on high-volume, low-margin temp work

- Tech companies hiring 50+ roles per year in competitive markets

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We already subscribe to Radford/Mercer salary surveys—why do we need this?"**
> "Traditional surveys are valuable for validation, but they're inherently backward-looking. The data in your current survey was collected 6-12 months ago, and by the time it's published, the market has moved. We had a customer in 2022 using 2021 survey data to make offers—they were 25% below market for software engineers and lost three top candidates before they realized. We complement surveys with real-time signals from active job postings, offer acceptance rates, and competitor movements. Most customers keep their survey subscription but use us for tactical decisions."

**"How is your salary data different from free sources like Glassdoor?"**
> "Glassdoor relies on self-reported, unverified data—anyone can post anything. We've found Glassdoor numbers are often 15-30% off from actual offer data. We aggregate from multiple sources: active job postings with stated ranges, offer acceptance data from our customer base, and public filings. We also provide confidence intervals and sample sizes so you know when data is robust vs. directional. Plus, we go beyond salary to show equity ranges, signing bonuses, and remote work policies."

**"This seems like a lot of data—how do we actually use it?"**
> "You're right—raw labor market data is overwhelming. That's why we built role-specific playbooks. For recruiters, we show exactly what offer packages are winning right now in your market. For HR leaders, we provide quarterly briefings on market movements affecting your workforce. For executives, we deliver competitive intelligence on who's hiring from you. Most customers spend 30 minutes per week in our platform and get actionable insights. We also offer quarterly strategy sessions with our analysts."

**"Can we get this data ourselves by scraping job boards?"**
> "Technically, yes—you could build scrapers and analyze the data. Several of our customers tried that first. The challenge is scale: we monitor 10M+ job postings daily across 50,000+ sources. Building and maintaining that infrastructure costs $500K+ annually in engineering and data science. Then you need to normalize job titles, deduplicate postings, and validate salary data. Most teams give up after 6 months. Our value is doing that heavy lifting and packaging it into insights you can act on immediately."

**"We're concerned about data privacy and compliance."**
> "Totally understand—labor data is sensitive. We don't collect or display individual salary information. All our data is aggregated and anonymized, similar to how credit bureaus report market data. We comply with GDPR, CCPA, and employment law regulations. Our salary ranges are statistically derived from market data, not individual records. We can also provide data processing agreements and SOC 2 compliance documentation. Would you like to review our security whitepaper?"
