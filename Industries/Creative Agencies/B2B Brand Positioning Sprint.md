# B2B Brand Positioning Sprint

**Category:** Creative Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Brand Strategy  
**Entry Cost:** $5K-$10K  
**Timeline:** 4 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** One-time project ($25K-$45K)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

B2B companies struggle to differentiate in crowded markets. They have features but lack a compelling narrative. This 4-week intensive delivers complete brand positioning: messaging framework, competitive differentiation, and a compelling narrative that resonates with their ideal customers.

The positioning sprint eliminates the endless "we're not sure how to talk about ourselves" cycle. Companies get clarity fast, with deliverables they can implement immediately across sales, marketing, and product.

**Market Size:** $12B brand strategy market (B2B segment growing 15% annually)  
**Target:** B2B SaaS, professional services, and tech companies with $5M-$100M ARR  
**Examples:** Pentagram's B2B practice, Wolf & Heron, Focus Lab

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** VP of Marketing / CMO  
**Secondary:** CEO, Head of Sales  
**Decision Maker:** CEO (often requires executive buy-in on positioning)

**Budget:** $25K-$50K for strategic projects  
**Pain Urgency:** High - directly impacts sales effectiveness and competitive positioning

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Messaging Inconsistency Across Teams ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Sales says one thing, marketing says another, product has its own language. Prospects get confused and deals stall.
**Current Solution:** Quarterly messaging workshops, brand guidelines PDFs nobody reads, internal Slack debates
**Cost of Pain:** 20-30% of deals lost to "confusion" ($500K-$2M in pipeline annually)
**Your Solution:** Single source of truth messaging framework with training for all customer-facing teams
**Pricing:** $25K-$45K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many different ways does your team describe what you do?"
- "When a prospect asks 'what makes you different,' how consistent are the answers?"
- "How often do deals stall because prospects 'need to think about it' or seem confused?"

---

### 2. Sounding Identical to Competitors
**Pain:** Website, pitch decks, and sales calls use the same buzzwords as everyone else. Prospects can't distinguish between options.
**Current Solution:** Competitor analysis spreadsheets, occasional positioning exercises
**Cost of Pain:** Price pressure reduces margins by 15-25%, longer sales cycles (30-60 days added)
**Your Solution:** Differentiation framework based on true competitive advantages, not buzzwords
**Pricing:** $30K-$45K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "If I covered your logo and your top competitor's logo, could prospects tell the difference?"
- "When you lose deals, what's the reason 80% of the time?"
- "How often do prospects ask you to 'send pricing' because they see you as interchangeable?"

---

### 3. No Clear Ideal Customer Definition
**Pain:** Trying to be everything to everyone, resulting in generic messaging that resonates with no one
**Current Solution:** Occasional ICP exercises, HubSpot persona templates
**Cost of Pain:** 40-50% of marketing budget wasted on wrong audiences ($200K-$500K annually)
**Your Solution:** Refined ICP with psychographic insights, buying triggers, and disqualification criteria
**Pricing:** Included in sprint

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Who is your absolute best customer, and what makes them different from your worst?"
- "What percentage of your pipeline is actually qualified?"
- "When you say 'no' to a prospect, what criteria do you use?"

---

### 4. Founder/CEO Still Doing All the Selling
**Pain:** Company growth constrained by founder's calendar. Sales team can't articulate value without founder on every call.
**Current Solution:** Shadowing founder on calls, hoping to "pick it up"
**Cost of Pain:** Founder time worth $500+/hour spent on repetitive sales calls, 2-3x longer sales cycle
**Your Solution:** Messaging framework that codifies founder's instinctive positioning
**Pricing:** $35K-$45K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many sales calls does [Founder] do per week?"
- "What's the difference in close rate when [Founder] is on the call vs. when they're not?"
- "What happens to the business if [Founder] can't do sales for a month?"

---

### 5. Pivoting Without Repositioning
**Pain:** Product evolved but messaging still reflects old positioning. Market confused about current value proposition.
**Current Solution:** Band-aid website updates, inconsistent explanations
**Cost of Pain:** 50%+ of inbound leads are wrong fit, sales spends 30% of time disqualifying
**Your Solution:** Complete repositioning that aligns messaging with current capabilities and market opportunity
**Pricing:** $30K-$40K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How has your product changed in the last 18 months?"
- "Does your website accurately reflect what you actually do today?"
- "What percentage of inbound leads are actually qualified for your current offering?"

---

### 6. Entering New Market Segment
**Pain:** Current positioning optimized for original market, doesn't translate to new vertical or segment
**Current Solution:** Generic "we serve everyone" messaging, segment-specific landing pages with same core message
**Cost of Pain:** 6-12 months to gain traction in new market, $300K+ in failed marketing spend
**Your Solution:** Market-specific positioning with segment entry strategy
**Pricing:** $35K-$45K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your expansion strategy for [new market]?"
- "How are you currently positioning for this segment vs. your core market?"
- "What do prospects in [new market] say when they first learn about you?"

---

### 7. Post-Acquisition Brand Confusion
**Pain:** Multiple products, multiple brands, no unified narrative. Customers and employees confused about company identity.
**Current Solution:** "Family of brands" approach, separate websites, inconsistent messaging
**Cost of Pain:** Cross-sell opportunities missed (20-30% revenue potential), customer confusion increases churn
**Your Solution:** Unified brand architecture with clear product positioning hierarchy
**Pricing:** $40K-$45K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you currently explain the relationship between [Product A] and [Product B]?"
- "What's the biggest confusion customers have about your brand structure?"
- "How much revenue are you missing from customers who only buy one product?"

---

### 8. Undifferentiated Category Position
**Pain:** Competing in category defined by market leader. Always compared to them, never the first choice.
**Current Solution:** Feature comparison tables, "we're like X but better" messaging
**Cost of Pain:** Perpetual #2 position, 30-40% lower pricing power
**Your Solution:** Category design or strategic repositioning outside competitor's frame
**Pricing:** $35K-$45K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What category do you compete in, and who defined it?"
- "How often are you compared to [market leader] in sales conversations?"
- "What would it take to stop being 'the alternative to [competitor]'?"

---

**Current Solution:** Endless strategy meetings, powerpoint decks, no decisions
**Cost of Pain:** 6-12 months of strategic drift, opportunity cost of $1M+ in delayed growth
**Your Solution:** Facilitated positioning workshop with decision framework and documentation
**Pricing:** $30K-$40K

**Discovery Questions:**

- "What strategic decisions have been pending for more than 3 months?"
- "How aligned is your leadership team on where the company is headed?"

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"Stop sounding like everyone else. Get a brand position that wins deals in 4 weeks."

### Authority Builders
1. **The Positioning Audit:** Free 30-minute assessment of current messaging effectiveness
2. **B2B Positioning Report:** Annual analysis of category positioning trends
3. **Messaging Scorecard:** Self-assessment tool for evaluating current positioning
4. **Positioning Playbook Webinar:** Monthly workshop on positioning fundamentals

### Positioning Angles
- **The Sprint Approach:** "Most agencies take 3-6 months. We deliver in 4 weeks because we've perfected the process."
- **The B2B Specialist:** "Consumer agencies don't understand enterprise sales cycles. We do."
- **The Implementation Focus:** "You get deliverables you can use immediately, not a strategy deck that sits in a folder."

### Differentiators
- Fixed 4-week timeline with guaranteed deliverables
- B2B specialization (no consumer work)
- Includes team training and enablement materials
- Positioning tied to revenue outcomes, not just brand aesthetics
- Proprietary positioning framework battle-tested with 50+ B2B companies

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Companies without product-market fit (positioning won't fix product issues)
- ❌ Pre-revenue startups (need customers first to understand positioning)
- ❌ Companies where CEO won't participate (positioning requires leadership buy-in)
- ❌ Organizations in active acquisition talks (positioning changes during M&A create confusion)
- ❌ Companies seeking brand without strategy ("we just want a new logo")

**Best Prospects:**
- B2B SaaS with $5M-$100M ARR
- Recent product evolution or pivot
- Entering new market or vertical
- Post-Series A/B with growth pressure
- CEO/CMO aligned on need for clarity

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We can do this internally."**
> "Most companies try that first. The challenge is you're too close to the product—you can't see it with fresh eyes. We've done this 50+ times and have patterns you won't see internally. Plus, internal projects get deprioritized. We deliver in 4 weeks guaranteed. Would it help to see what we delivered for [similar company]?"

**"This seems expensive for 'messaging.'"**
> "I get that. Let's look at the cost of your current positioning. You're losing [X]% of deals to confusion or competition. If we improve your win rate by even 10%, that's [$X] in additional revenue. The sprint pays for itself with one additional deal. What's your average contract value?"

**"How is this different from working with [big brand agency]?"**
> "Great question. Big agencies are generalists—they do B2C, B2B, everything. We only do B2B positioning. Their process takes 3-6 months; ours takes 4 weeks. They charge $100K-$250K; we're $25K-$45K. And we include implementation support, not just strategy. Which of those differences matters most to you?"

**"What if we don't like the positioning?"**
> "We have checkpoints throughout the sprint. Week 1 is research and discovery—if we're off track, we catch it early. Week 2 is framework development with your feedback. By week 3, you've seen and approved the direction. We've never had a client reject the final positioning because they're part of building it. How involved is your team in decisions like this?"

**"We're not sure we're ready to commit to this."**
> "Totally understand. Most companies sit with positioning ambiguity for months or years. The cost is real deals lost every week. Let's start with a Positioning Audit—30 minutes, no cost. We'll assess your current messaging and give you specific recommendations. Even if we don't work together, you'll have clarity on what needs fixing. Does next Tuesday or Wednesday work for a brief call?"

**"We tried a positioning exercise before and it didn't work."**
> "That's actually common. Most positioning exercises fail because they lack three things: external perspective, tight timeline with accountability, and focus on implementation. Which part didn't work in your previous exercise? [Listen] Here's how we address that specifically..."

## 📞 Discovery Questions

**To VP Marketing/CMO:**
- "Walk me through your current sales process. Where do prospects get confused?"
- "If you could change one thing about how the market perceives you, what would it be?"
- "What's the delta between your close rate when the founder sells vs. the sales team?"
- "How do you currently measure positioning effectiveness?"

**To CEO:**
- "Describe your company in one sentence. Now ask your VP Sales to do the same. How aligned are you?"
- "What keeps you awake at night about competitive positioning?"
- "If we doubled your win rate, what would that mean for the business?"
- "What's the cost of another 6 months with unclear positioning?"
