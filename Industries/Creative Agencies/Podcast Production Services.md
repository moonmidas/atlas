# Podcast Production Services

**Category:** Creative Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Audio Content  
**Entry Cost:** $8K-$15K  
**Timeline:** Ongoing monthly  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly subscription ($3K-$8K/month per show)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Podcasts are powerful thought leadership and audience-building tools, but production is time-consuming and technical. Most B2B podcasts fail after 10 episodes because hosts burn out on editing, show notes, and promotion. This end-to-end production service handles everything: editing, show notes, clips, promotion, and strategy—keeping shows consistent and hosts focused on content.

Unlike basic editing services, this provides full podcast operations including strategy, content optimization, and growth support.

**Market Size:** $25B podcasting industry (B2B production services growing 30% annually)  
**Target:** B2B companies and executives with podcasts or podcast ambitions  
**Examples:** Sweet Fish Media, B2B Growth, The Podcast Consultant, Lower Street

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Editing Takes Too Long ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** 1-hour episode takes 4-6 hours to edit. Hosts burning out. Quality inconsistent.
**Current Solution:** Doing it themselves, occasional freelance help, accepting rough cuts
**Cost of Pain:** 10-20 hours/week on production, podcast becomes burden, inconsistent release
**Your Solution:** Professional editing with 48-72 hour turnaround
**Pricing:** $3K-$8K/month per show

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How long does editing take you currently?"
- "How often do episodes get delayed because of production?"
- "What part of podcast production do you dread most?"

---

### 2. Show Notes Don't Get Done

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Podcasts without clear audience or goal
- ❌ Hosts who won't commit to consistent recording
- ❌ Expecting overnight audience growth (takes 6-12 months)
- ❌ No marketing support for podcast promotion
- ❌ Pre-revenue companies without content strategy

**Best Prospects:**
- Established podcast (10+ episodes) or committed launch
- Weekly or bi-weekly release commitment
- Clear audience and business goal
- Marketing support for promotion
- VP Marketing with podcast budget

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We can edit ourselves."**
> "You can—and many do. But editing takes 4-6 hours per episode. At your hourly value, that's $1,000+ per episode in opportunity cost. Plus, professional editing sounds better and takes less time. What else could you do with 20 hours per month?"

**"What if we don't like the editing?"**
> "You get review and revision on every episode. We learn your preferences over time—what to cut, how to handle pauses, music choices. Most clients find we get better at matching their style with each episode. How particular are you about editing style?"

**"How do we grow the audience?"**
> "Production quality is table stakes—growth requires strategy. We create clips for social, optimize for SEO, suggest cross-promotions, and provide growth tactics. Consistency + quality + promotion = growth. But it takes 6-12 months. Are you committed for the long term?"

**"This is expensive for a podcast."**
> "Compared to hiring a producer ($60K-$80K salary), we're cost-effective. Plus, consider the cost of your time doing production—or the cost of an inconsistent show that dies after 10 episodes. What's your current investment in the podcast?"

**"What about video podcasts?"**
> "We do full video production—recording support, multi-camera editing, YouTube optimization, clip creation. Video podcasts get 3-5x engagement but require more production. What video presence do you want?"

**"How do sponsors work?"**
> "We can help with sponsor outreach, rate setting, fulfillment, and management. We take 15-20% of revenue for sponsor management. Most shows need 5K+ downloads per episode to attract sponsors. What's your current download count?"
