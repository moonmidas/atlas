# UX/UI Design Sprints

**Category:** Creative Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Product Design  
**Entry Cost:** $5K-$12K  
**Timeline:** 2 weeks per sprint  
**Revenue Model:** Per-sprint project ($12K-$20K per sprint)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Product teams need design work but can't hire full-time designers fast enough. Traditional agency models are too slow and expensive for iterative product development. This embedded design sprint service provides dedicated designers in 2-week bursts: rapid prototyping, user testing, and design deliverables—keeping product development moving without hiring delays.

Unlike staff augmentation that provides bodies, this provides structured sprints with defined outcomes—design velocity without the management overhead.

**Market Size:** $45B UX/UI design market (sprint segment growing 25% annually)  
**Target:** Product-led growth companies, Series A-C startups, and product teams with design backlogs  
**Examples:** Design Sprint Agency, AJ&Smart, Google Ventures, Figma's DesignOps

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Design Bottlenecks Blocking Releases ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Engineering ready but designs not done. Product releases delayed. Engineering sitting idle.
**Current Solution:** Hiring slow, pushing through without design, accepting delays
**Cost of Pain:** Engineering costs $150-300/hr sitting idle, 2-4 week delays per feature
**Your Solution:** Rapid design sprints unblocking engineering immediately
**Pricing:** $12K-$20K per sprint

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your current design backlog?"
- "How often do releases get delayed waiting for design?"
- "What happens to engineering when they're waiting for design?"

### 2. Can't Hire Designers Fast Enough

**Current Solution:** Contractors, freelancers, pushing existing team harder

**Your Solution:** Immediate design capacity without hiring process

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We should hire full-time designers."**
> "You should—and when you find the right people, you should. But hiring takes 3-6 months. What happens to your roadmap in the meantime? We bridge the gap: immediate capacity while you hire. Many clients use us for 6-12 months then transition to internal team. What's your hiring timeline?"

**"How is this different from contractors?"**
> "Contractors provide hours; we provide outcomes. Our sprint has structure: kickoff, design, review, iterate, deliver. We guarantee deliverables in 2 weeks. Contractors often extend, need more direction, and deliverables vary. What challenges have you had with contractors?"

**"What if we need changes after the sprint?"**
> "Sprints include iteration cycles—you see work in progress and provide feedback. After sprint, you can: 1) implement what we delivered, 2) book another sprint for additional work, or 3) transition to your team. What post-sprint support do you anticipate needing?"

**"This is expensive for 2 weeks."**
> "Compared to a $150K designer salary plus benefits, we're cost-effective for immediate needs. Plus, consider the cost of delayed releases—engineering sitting idle costs more than our sprint. What does a 2-week delay cost your engineering team?"

**"What if we don't like the designs?"**
> "You're involved throughout. Daily check-ins, mid-sprint review, final presentation. We iterate based on your feedback within the sprint. Our process has checkpoints to ensure alignment. What does your ideal design process look like?"

**"How do we work together?"**
> "We're embedded for 2 weeks: daily standups, Slack channel, shared Figma files. You treat us like team members, not vendors. We join your meetings, understand your context, deliver within your workflow. How do you prefer to work with external designers?"
