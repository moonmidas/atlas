# Rebrand Sprint

**Category:** Creative Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Brand Identity  
**Entry Cost:** $8K-$15K  
**Timeline:** 6 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** One-time project ($35K-$75K)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Companies outgrow their brands but full-service agency rebrands take 6-12 months and cost $200K-$500K. This 6-week sprint delivers strategic rebranding: positioning, visual identity, brand guidelines, and launch support—at a fraction of the time and cost.

Unlike traditional agencies that stretch projects for billable hours, this sprint methodology focuses on decisions over exploration—delivering a rebrand that works without the agency bloat.

**Market Size:** $45B branding industry (sprint segment growing 25% annually)  
**Target:** Growth-stage companies ($5M-$100M) needing brand evolution, not enterprise overhauls  
**Examples:** Collins, Wolf & Heron, Focus Lab, Metalab

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** CEO / CMO  
**Secondary:** VP Marketing, Creative Director  
**Decision Maker:** CEO (rebrand is strategic company decision)

**Budget:** $35K-$100K for rebranding  
**Pain Urgency:** Medium-High - brand misalignment becomes more painful as company grows

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Brand Doesn't Reflect Company Today ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Logo, colors, messaging created for 10-person startup. Now 100+ people. Brand looks amateur.
**Current Solution:** Living with it, minor updates, hoping nobody notices
**Cost of Pain:** Perception misalignment hurts sales, recruiting, and partnerships. Brand becomes liability.
**Your Solution:** Strategic rebrand aligned with current company stage and ambitions
**Pricing:** $35K-$75K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "When was your current brand created, and what stage was the company at?"
- "What feedback do you get about your brand from customers, recruits, investors?"
- "How do you feel when you hand someone your business card?"

---

### 2. Competitive Blending
**Pain:** Brand looks like everyone else in the space. No differentiation. Generic and forgettable.
**Current Solution:** Copying category leaders, following trends, minor differentiation attempts
**Cost of Pain:** Lost deals to more distinctive competitors, price pressure, weak brand recall
**Your Solution:** Strategic differentiation with unique visual and verbal identity
**Pricing:** $40K-$75K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How does your brand compare to your top 3 competitors?"
- "If you removed your logo, would customers recognize your brand?"
- "What makes your brand unmistakably yours?"

---

### 3. Brand Fragmentation
**Pain:** Multiple products, acquisitions, or teams with different brands. No cohesion. Looks like different companies.
**Current Solution:** Sub-brands, ignoring the problem, occasional attempts at unification
**Cost of Pain:** Customer confusion, missed cross-sell, brand equity dilution
**Your Solution:** Unified brand architecture with clear hierarchy and relationships
**Pricing:** $50K-$75K (complex)

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many different brand identities exist in your company?"
- "How do customers understand the relationship between your offerings?"
- "What confusion exists about your brand structure?"

---

### 4. Outdated Visual Identity
**Pain:** Design looks dated (gradients, skeuomorphism, overused fonts). Signals company behind the times.
**Current Solution:** Occasional refreshes, living with it, hoping for the best
**Cost of Pain:** Perception of being behind competitors, dated = untrustworthy for tech companies
**Your Solution:** Contemporary visual identity with timeless principles (not just trend-chasing)
**Pricing:** $35K-$55K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "When you look at your brand materials, what era do they feel from?"
- "What design trends have you outgrown?"
- "How do customers describe your brand visually?"

---

### 5. No Brand Consistency
**Pain:** Every department does their own thing. Sales decks, website, product UI all look different.
**Current Solution:** Brand guidelines (ignored), ad-hoc approvals, accepting inconsistency
**Cost of Pain:** Brand dilution, amateur appearance, wasted design effort
**Your Solution:** Comprehensive brand system with clear guidelines and templates
**Pricing:** Included in sprint

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How consistent is your brand across touchpoints?"
- "Who can use brand elements, and how do they get them?"
- "What brand inconsistencies frustrate you most?"

---

### 6. Post-Acquisition Brand Confusion
**Pain:** Acquired company or merged entities with conflicting brands. No clear path forward.
**Current Solution:** Operating multiple brands, gradual transitions, hoping confusion clears
**Cost of Pain:** Customer confusion, internal tension, brand investment spread thin
**Your Solution:** Strategic brand integration with migration planning
**Pricing:** $50K-$75K

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What brands exist post-acquisition/merger?"
- "How are you currently handling brand confusion?"
- "What's the timeline for brand integration?"

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We need a full-service agency for a rebrand."**
> "Traditional agencies are great for complex enterprise rebrands with 50 stakeholders. For growth-stage companies, that process is overkill—you're paying for bureaucracy you don't need. Our sprint approach delivers the same strategic rigor in 6 weeks because we focus on decisions, not exploration. What aspects of the traditional agency process do you actually need?"

**"What if we don't like the brand direction?"**
> "We have checkpoints throughout: Week 1 (strategy alignment), Week 2 (creative direction), Week 3 (design concepts). You see and approve before we proceed. We present 2-3 distinct directions, not endless variations. And we've done this 50+ times—our process works. What concerns you most about the creative process?"

**"How do we know the rebrand will work?"**
> "We test everything. Brand concepts get stakeholder feedback. Visual identity gets customer input. Messaging gets market validation. Plus, we measure success—brand awareness, perception, and business metrics. What would 'work' look like for your rebrand?"

**"This is expensive for 6 weeks."**
> "It's expensive compared to Fiverr, yes. But compared to a $200K agency rebrand that takes 8 months, we're 70% less expensive and 7x faster. Plus, you're getting a complete brand system, not just a logo. What's your budget for rebranding, and what do you expect for that investment?"

**"What about implementing the rebrand?"**
> "Great question. The sprint delivers all the assets and guidelines you need. Implementation can be done by your team, your web agency, or we can support with additional services. Most clients implement over 3-6 months post-sprint. What's your implementation capacity?"

**"We can't afford disruption right now."**
> "I understand—rebrands feel risky. But here's the thing: gradual brand drift is happening whether you address it or not. Every day with a misaligned brand costs you in perception and lost opportunities. Our phased launch approach minimizes disruption. When would be the 'right' time, and what's the cost of waiting?"
