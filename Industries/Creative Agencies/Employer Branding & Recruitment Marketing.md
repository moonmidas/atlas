# Employer Branding & Recruitment Marketing

**Category:** Creative Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Employer Brand & Talent  
**Entry Cost:** $8K-$15K  
**Timeline:** 8 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** One-time project ($30K-$60K) + Monthly retainer ($8K/month)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Companies compete fiercely for talent but most have employer brands that are generic, outdated, or nonexistent. Their careers pages are HR documents, not marketing assets. This 8-week intensive builds a comprehensive employer brand: Employee Value Proposition (EVP), careers page transformation, recruitment content, and ongoing marketing to attract and retain top talent.

Unlike traditional employer brand consultants who deliver strategy decks, this service delivers implementable assets that immediately improve recruitment outcomes and reduce cost-per-hire.

**Market Size:** $18B employer branding market (growing 14% annually)  
**Target:** Growth-stage companies (100-1,000 employees) hiring 50+ people annually  
**Examples:** Employer Branding Agency, Ph.Creative, Symphony Talent, Universum

---

### 1. Can't Attract Top Talent ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Competing with well-known brands for same talent pool. Company unknown or unappealing to candidates.
**Current Solution:** Increasing compensation, using recruiters, posting on more job boards
**Cost of Pain:** 3-6 month average time-to-fill for key roles, $50K-$150K per vacancy in lost productivity, settling for B-players
**Your Solution:** Differentiated employer brand that attracts candidates organically
**Pricing:** $30K-$60K one-time + $8K/month

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your average time-to-fill for engineering/sales/key roles?"

- "What do candidates say when they decline your offers?"

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Companies with toxic cultures (branding won't fix reality)
- ❌ Leadership not aligned on culture/values
- ❌ No hiring plan or growth trajectory
- ❌ Expecting overnight pipeline changes (takes 3-6 months to show results)
- ❌ HR-only decision without marketing alignment

**Best Prospects:**
- 100-1,000 employees
- Hiring 50+ people annually
- Recent funding or growth phase
- Competitive talent market (tech, sales, specialized roles)
- Leadership aligned on culture investment

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We can do this internally."**
> "You can—and many try. But here's the challenge: HR teams are stretched thin with recruiting, compliance, and employee issues. Employer brand needs dedicated focus and specialized skills (research, design, content) that are hard to hire. We deliver in 8 weeks what takes internal teams 12-18 months. When do you need to see recruiting results?"

**"How do we measure ROI?"**
> "Great question—we measure everything. Before: cost-per-hire, time-to-fill, application-to-interview ratio, Glassdoor rating. After: improvements in each metric. Most clients see 30-50% reduction in cost-per-hire and 20-30% faster time-to-fill within 6 months. What metrics matter most to your leadership?"

**"We don't have a big employer brand budget."**
> "I understand. Let's look at what you're already spending. [Calculate their recruiting costs] You're likely spending $500K+ on recruiting annually. A $30K-$60K investment in employer brand typically reduces those costs by 20-30%—saving $100K-$150K in year one. It pays for itself. What's your total annual recruiting spend?"

**"What if our culture isn't that special?"**
> "Every company has something unique—even if it's not obvious. Our research process uncovers what employees actually value. Sometimes it's the mission, sometimes it's the people, sometimes it's growth opportunities. We find the truth and help you tell it authentically. What do you think employees would say if we interviewed them?"

**"How is this different from [big HR consultancy]?"**
> "Traditional HR consultancies deliver strategy decks and leave implementation to you. We deliver the careers page, the content, the recruiter tools—everything you need to execute immediately. Plus, we specialize in growth-stage companies, not enterprise. We understand the speed and resource constraints you're working with. How much implementation support do you need?"

**"What if Glassdoor reviews are really bad?"**
> "Bad reviews are data, not destiny. We help you understand what's driving them and address root causes honestly. Sometimes that means operational changes, sometimes it means better storytelling about the reality. The worst thing is ignoring them. What do your recent reviews tell you about employee experience?"
