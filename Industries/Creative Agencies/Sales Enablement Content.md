# Sales Enablement Content

**Category:** Creative Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Sales Content  
**Entry Cost:** $5K-$10K  
**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks  
**Revenue Model:** One-time project ($20K-$40K) + Monthly retainer ($5K/month)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Sales teams are flying blind—no battle cards, outdated one-pagers, and email templates that sound robotic. Marketing creates content for awareness but ignores the sales conversation. This service builds a complete sales content system: battle cards, competitive intelligence, email sequences, and ongoing content updates that help sales close more deals faster.

Unlike generic content creation, this focuses specifically on the sales conversation—what reps need at each stage to advance and close opportunities.

**Market Size:** $12B sales enablement market (content segment growing 20% annually)  
**Target:** B2B SaaS and services companies with 10+ sales reps  
**Examples:** Highspot, Seismic, Showpad, Guru

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** VP of Sales / Sales Enablement Lead  
**Secondary:** CRO, VP Marketing  
**Decision Maker:** VP Sales or CRO

**Budget:** $20K-$60K for content systems + $5K-$10K/month for ongoing  
**Pain Urgency:** High - directly impacts sales productivity and win rates

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Reps Don't Know How to Handle Objections ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Every rep answers objections differently. Some responses hurt deals. No consistent approach.
**Current Solution:** Occasional training, Slack questions, hoping reps figure it out
**Cost of Pain:** 20-30% of deals lost to mishandled objections, inconsistent customer experience
**Your Solution:** Battle-tested objection handling guides with approved responses
**Pricing:** $20K-$30K (included in full system)

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What objections do your reps hear most frequently?"
- "How consistent are rep responses to the same objection?"
- "What objection has cost you the most deals recently?"

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"Marketing already creates sales materials."**
> "Most do. But here's the challenge: marketing content is designed for awareness and education. Sales content needs to handle objections, address specific situations, and advance deals. It's a different discipline. What percentage of marketing content do your reps actually use?"

**"We have a sales enablement platform."**
> "Great! Highspot, Seismic, Showpad—those are content management tools, not content creation. They organize what you have. We create what you need. In fact, we can optimize content specifically for your platform. What gaps do you still have in your enablement content?"

**"How do we know this will work?"**
> "We test everything with reps before finalizing. Battle cards get competitive scenario validation. Email templates get response rate testing. One-pagers get sales feedback. Plus, we measure adoption—if reps don't use it, we fix it. What would success look like for your enablement content?"

**"Sales should just use what we give them."**
> "In an ideal world, yes. But reps are creating their own materials anyway—usually 5-10 hours per week. The question is: do you want them using brand-approved, effective content, or whatever they scramble together? We make the approved content so good they want to use it. How much time do your reps spend creating their own materials?"

**"This is expensive for 'content.'"**
> "Let's look at the cost of the status quo. [Calculate rep time + lost deals] Most clients find that poor enablement costs $500K+ annually in rep productivity and lost deals. The system pays for itself with 2-3 additional closed deals. What's your average deal size?"

**"What if reps still don't use the content?"**
> "Adoption is critical—we don't succeed unless reps use the content. That's why we involve reps in creation, test everything, and build what they ask for. We also provide usage analytics and recommendations. If adoption is low, we diagnose why and fix it. How do you currently track content usage?"
