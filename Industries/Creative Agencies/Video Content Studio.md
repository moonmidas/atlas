# Video Content Studio

**Category:** Creative Agencies  
**Sub-Category:** Video Production  
**Entry Cost:** $15K-$30K  
**Timeline:** Ongoing monthly  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly subscription ($10K-$25K/month)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Video is the most engaging content format but B2B companies struggle to produce it consistently. Traditional video production is expensive, slow, and project-based. This subscription service provides monthly video production: strategy, filming, editing, and optimization—turning video from a sporadic luxury into a core marketing engine.

Unlike one-off video production companies, this model offers predictable costs, faster turnaround, and strategic guidance on what videos to create for maximum business impact.

**Market Size:** $60B video production market (B2B subscription segment growing 30% annually)  
**Target:** B2B SaaS, professional services, and tech companies investing in content marketing  
**Examples:** Wistia, Vidyard, Sandwich Video, Demo Duck

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We can hire a videographer in-house."**
> "You can—and for some companies that makes sense. But a full-time videographer costs $80K-$120K plus benefits, equipment, and management time. And one person can only do so much. Our team gives you multiple specialists—strategists, shooters, editors—for less than the cost of one hire. Plus, what happens when they're sick, on vacation, or leave? How are you currently handling video production?"

**"Video is too expensive for our budget."**
> "Let's look at what you're spending. [Calculate current video costs or opportunity cost of not doing video] Most companies underestimate video ROI. A single customer testimonial video used in sales can accelerate deals by 20-30%. One product demo can reduce support tickets and speed onboarding. When you look at video as a revenue tool, not a marketing expense, the math changes. What's the value of one additional customer?"

**"What if we don't like the videos?"**
> "You're involved throughout. We start with strategy alignment—what videos, why, and key messages. You approve scripts and storyboards before we shoot. After filming, you get rough cuts for feedback. We revise until you're satisfied. Plus, we learn your preferences over time—the videos get better each month. What does your ideal video approval process look like?"

**"How do we measure video ROI?"**
> "We track everything: views, engagement, click-through rates, and most importantly—business impact. We can tie video to pipeline (which videos influence deals), retention (onboarding video impact), and support (deflection from help docs). Before we start, we define what success looks like for your business. What metrics matter most to you?"

**"We tried video before and it didn't work."**
> "I hear that a lot. Usually, one of three things happened: no strategy (random videos), poor distribution (post and pray), or unrealistic expectations (one video to rule them all). We fix all three. What specifically didn't work about your previous video efforts? [Listen] Here's how we'd approach that differently..."

**"Our team is camera shy."**
> "That's normal—we work with camera-shy teams all the time. Our interview process makes people comfortable. We use conversational formats, not scripted performances. Remote recording options reduce pressure. And we can use voiceover, animation, and customer footage to minimize on-camera needs. How camera-shy are we talking?"

**Month 3:** Launch with 2 beta clients at $8K-$10K/month. Deliver first month. Gather feedback.
