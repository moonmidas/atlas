# Dental Practice OS

**Category:** Vertical SaaS  
**Sub-Category:** Healthcare/Dental  
**Entry Cost:** $100K-$250K  
**Timeline:** 6-9 months to market  
**Revenue Model:** Per practice monthly subscription

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Dental practices operate at the intersection of healthcare and small business, facing unique challenges: clinical care requirements, insurance complexity, patient retention pressures, and regulatory compliance. Most practices rely on legacy Practice Management Software (PMS) like Dentrix, Eaglesoft, or Open Dental that hasn't significantly evolved in 20 years. These systems are expensive, difficult to use, lack modern patient communication tools, and don't integrate well with the 10+ other systems practices need.

An all-in-one operating system designed specifically for dental practices—with modern scheduling, automated patient communication, integrated billing, recall management, and analytics—can capture significant market share by offering better user experience, lower total cost, and improved patient outcomes. The dental market is stable (everyone needs teeth), practices have predictable budgets, and decision-makers are reachable.

**Market Size:** 190K dental practices in US, $2.5B+ TAM at target pricing  
**Target:** Independent dental practices (1-5 dentists), group practices (DSO-ready)  
**Examples:** Weave ($1B+ valuation), NexHealth, Modento, Curve Dental, Denticon

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. No-Show and Cancellation Crisis ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** 10-15% no-show rate, last-minute cancellations, schedule gaps kill productivity, hygienists idle
**Current Solution:** Phone call reminders, postcard mailers, basic email reminders
**Cost of Pain:** Each no-show = $200-$400 lost production; 10% no-show rate on $100K/month practice = $10K lost monthly
**Your Solution:** Multi-channel automated reminders (SMS, email, voice), two-way confirmation, waitlist backfill, deposit collection
**Pricing:** $99/month recall/reminder module

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your current no-show rate?"
- "How many appointment slots go unfilled each week?"
- "What happens when a patient cancels at the last minute?"

---

### 2. Recall and Reactivation Failure
**Pain:** Patients fall through cracks, 6-month recalls missed, past-due hygiene patients not contacted, dormant patient database
**Current Solution:** Sporadic postcard campaigns, staff calling when remembered, hoping patients book
**Cost of Pain:** Average practice has $500K-$1M in unscheduled treatment; dormant patient database worth $300K+ in potential revenue
**Your Solution:** Automated recall campaigns by due date, reactivation sequences for dormant patients, unscheduled treatment follow-up
**Pricing:** Included in patient communication suite

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do you currently track patients due for recall?"
- "What percentage of your patient base hasn't been seen in 18+ months?"
- "How many unscheduled hygiene appointments do you have right now?"

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Start-up practices (<2 years, cash flow issues)
- ❌ Practices with <$500K annual production (can't afford tools)
- ❌ Corporate DSOs with custom IT requirements (long sales cycle)
- ❌ Dentists who say "I just want to do dentistry" (avoid business decisions)
- ❌ Practices with >50% Medicaid (low margins, different workflows)

**Best Prospects:**
- Established practices (3+ years) with stable production
- Currently using 3+ separate patient communication tools
- No-show rate >10%
- Multi-location practices feeling growth pain
- Practices adding associates or hygienists

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We're happy with our current setup."**
> "Many practices are, until they see what's possible. Can I ask—what's your current no-show rate, and how much revenue do you think that costs monthly? Most practices we talk to are losing $8K-$15K monthly to no-shows and cancellations. Even a 30% improvement pays for our platform 3x over."

**"We already have [Weave/NexHealth/modento]."**
> "Those are good products. The difference is we're built specifically for dental—not general healthcare. That means recall logic based on prophy intervals, treatment plan follow-up designed for dental case acceptance, and integrations that understand dental procedures. How much customization have you had to do to make your current platform work for dentistry?"

**"Switching systems is too disruptive."**
> "That's exactly why we designed our platform to integrate with your existing PMS rather than replace it. You keep Dentrix for charting and billing; we enhance patient communication and scheduling. Setup takes 2-3 days, not 2-3 months. And we migrate your patient data so nothing is lost. What specific concerns do you have about implementation?"

**"Our patients don't like technology."**
> "Surprisingly, we hear the opposite from practices—especially since COVID. 70% of patients prefer to text rather than call, and online scheduling is the #1 requested feature. But if you have specific patient demographics that prefer phone calls, our system accommodates that too. You choose the communication mix. Can I show you how other practices in [similar area] have balanced this?"

**"The timing isn't right."**
> "I understand timing is everything. Can I ask what specifically needs to happen for the timing to be right? Often practices think they need to wait for a slower period, but implementation actually works best during busy times because we can immediately show impact. Plus, every month you wait is another month of no-shows and lost recall revenue. What would need to change for this to be a priority?"

**"Your pricing is higher than competitors."**
> "Let's look at total cost. Most practices pay $400 for communication, $300 for scheduling, $150 for reviews, plus separate reminder services. That's over $1,000 for tools that don't integrate well. We're $599 for everything—integrated, dental-specific, and we guarantee 20% no-show reduction or your money back. On a $1M practice, a 20% no-show reduction adds $24K annually to your bottom line."
