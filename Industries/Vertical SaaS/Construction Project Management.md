# Construction Project Management

**Category:** Vertical SaaS  
**Sub-Category:** Construction/Contracting  
**Entry Cost:** $150K-$350K  
**Timeline:** 6-9 months to market  
**Revenue Model:** Per project or per company monthly subscription

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Mid-sized construction contractors ($5M-$50M annual revenue) face a unique operational challenge: they are too large for simple spreadsheets and too small for expensive enterprise systems like Procore or Autodesk. These contractors manage 10-50 concurrent projects with hundreds of moving parts: subcontractors, materials, change orders, schedules, and field-to-office communication. Most rely on a chaotic mix of Excel, email, phone calls, and paper, resulting in cost overruns, schedule delays, and profit erosion.

An all-in-one project management platform built specifically for the mid-market contractor—with field data collection, document management, budget tracking, and subcontractor coordination—can capture significant market share by providing enterprise-level functionality at a mid-market price point.

**Market Size:** 700K+ contractors in US, $3B+ TAM at target pricing  
**Target:** Commercial GCs, residential builders, specialty contractors ($5M-$50M revenue)  
**Examples:** Procore ($8B+ valuation), Autodesk Construction Cloud, Fieldwire, PlanGrid, CoConstruct

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Field-to-Office Communication Gap ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Field crews don't report issues promptly, office learns about problems too late, change orders buried, daily logs inconsistent
**Current Solution:** Phone calls, text messages, end-of-week reports, site visits
**Cost of Pain:** Problems discovered 3-7 days late = $5K-$50K in remediation; change orders lost = 10-20% profit erosion
**Your Solution:** Mobile daily logs, instant issue reporting, photo documentation, real-time sync with office
**Pricing:** $399/month base + $99/month per project

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How do field crews currently report daily progress and issues?"
- "How many days typically pass before the office knows about a field problem?"
- "What percentage of change orders get missed or documented late?"

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Contractors <$5M revenue (price sensitive, simple needs)
- ❌ Contractors >$100M (enterprise sales cycle, different needs)
- ❌ Companies not currently using any PM software (change-resistant)
- ❌ Family businesses with no professional management
- ❌ Contractors with chronic payment/solvency issues

**Best Prospects:**
- Growing contractors adding project managers (pain of scale)
- Currently using 3+ separate systems
- Project overruns >10% regularly
- Owner focused on professionalizing operations
- Commercial GC or residential builder

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We are happy with Procore/Autodesk."**
> "Those are excellent platforms—for $100M+ contractors. Most of our customers came from Procore frustrated by the complexity, per-project pricing, and feature overload. They were paying $1,500-$3,000 monthly for features they never used. We offer what mid-market contractors actually need at half the price. What features do you use in your current platform?"

**"Our crews will not use technology in the field."**
> "We hear that at every implementation, and 90% adoption is typical within 30 days. Our field app is simpler than texting—three taps to submit a daily report. Plus, crews love having their photos and issues documented instantly instead of forgotten. We provide on-site training and 24/7 field support. What specific concerns do you have about field adoption?"

**"We do not have time to implement new software mid-season."**
> "We specialize in mid-season implementations because that is when the pain is highest. We can have your first project live in 48 hours without disrupting current operations. Data migration happens after hours. Most contractors see value within the first week. What is your biggest concern about timing?"

**"Your pricing seems high for our size."**
> "Let us compare: Procore charges $400-$600 per project monthly. On 10 concurrent projects, that is $4,000-$6,000. We are $799 for unlimited projects. Even if you only save one change order per quarter or catch one budget overrun early, the system pays for itself. Most customers see 3-5% margin improvement—that is $150K+ on $5M revenue."

**"We have tried software before and it did not stick."**
> "That is common—most construction software is designed by engineers, not contractors. We assign a dedicated implementation specialist who knows construction, not just software. We train your team on your actual projects, not generic examples. And we do not consider implementation complete until your team is using it daily. What went wrong with your previous software attempt?"
