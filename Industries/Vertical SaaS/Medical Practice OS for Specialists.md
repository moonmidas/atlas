# Medical Practice OS for Specialists

**Category:** Vertical SaaS  
**Sub-Category:** Healthcare/Medical  
**Entry Cost:** $200K-$500K  
**Timeline:** 6-12 months to market  
**Revenue Model:** Per provider monthly subscription

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Specialty medical practices (orthopedics, dermatology, ophthalmology, ENT, etc.) face unique workflow challenges that generic EHR systems fail to address adequately. Unlike primary care, specialists deal with procedure-heavy workflows, complex imaging, device integration, surgical scheduling, and specific billing requirements. Most practices use generic EHRs (Epic, Cerner, athenahealth) that require extensive customization or workarounds, creating inefficiencies and physician frustration.

An all-in-one operating system built specifically for specialty workflows—with procedure templates, imaging integration, surgical coordination, and specialty-specific billing—can capture significant market share by providing purpose-built functionality that generic EHRs cannot match. The market has high willingness to pay due to physician salaries and procedure revenue.

**Market Size:** 50K+ specialty practices in US, $3B+ TAM at target pricing  
**Target:** Single-specialty practices (5-25 providers), specialty groups planning expansion  
**Examples:** Modernizing Medicine (modmed), Nextech, SRS Health, AdvancedMD, DrChrono

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Practices with <3 providers (price sensitive)
- ❌ Practices already on Epic/Cerner (high switching cost)
- ❌ Hospital-owned practices (IT decides)
- ❌ Practices not currently using an EHR (change-resistant)
- ❌ Academic medical centers (different sales cycle)

**Best Prospects:**
- Growing specialty groups (5-20 providers)
- Currently using generic EHR with heavy customization
- Physician frustration with current documentation
- Planning to add providers or locations
- Focus on procedures and surgery

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We are happy with athenahealth/Nextech/our current EHR."**
> "Those are solid general platforms. Most of our customers came from them frustrated by generic workflows that don't fit specialty needs—endless customization, workarounds for procedures, poor imaging integration. They were paying $1,200-$1,800 per provider for partial solutions. We offer purpose-built specialty workflows at competitive pricing. What is your biggest EHR frustration?"

**"Switching EHRs is too disruptive for a medical practice."**
> "It is definitely a major decision—which is why we do phased implementations. We start with one or two providers, migrate data over 60 days, and train during the transition. Most practices are fully operational in 90 days with minimal disruption. We also provide 24/7 go-live support. What concerns you most about the transition?"

**"Our physicians are resistant to change."**
> "Physician resistance is usually due to bad past experiences. Our specialty-specific templates actually reduce documentation time 30-40% compared to generic EHRs. Most physicians love the system within a week because it fits their workflow instead of fighting it. We also do one-on-one physician training, not classroom sessions. What EHR complaints do your physicians have currently?"

**"Your pricing seems high compared to general EHRs."**
> "Let us compare total cost: EHR ($400) + billing service ($400) + imaging ($150) + surgery scheduling ($100) = $1,050 for disconnected tools plus customization costs. We are $999 for everything integrated with specialty workflows. Plus, reducing documentation time 30% saves $50K+ annually per physician. What is your current total technology spend per provider?"

**"We need to see ROI before making a decision."**
> "Absolutely. We typically help practices achieve: 30% reduction in documentation time, 15% increase in collections through better coding, 20% reduction in claim denials, and 50% faster prior auth processing. On a 10-provider practice, that is $500K+ annual impact. We can do a detailed ROI analysis with your current metrics. What metrics matter most to your practice?"
