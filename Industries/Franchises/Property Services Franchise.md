# Property Services Franchise

**Category:** Franchises  
**Sub-Category:** Painting/Flooring/Exterior/Home Improvement Services  
**Entry Cost:** $75K-$200K  
**Timeline:** 3-6 months to launch  
**Revenue Model:** $30K-$50K franchise fee + 5-7% royalty

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Property services franchises package local demand for improvement, maintenance, and specialty trade coordination into a repeatable service model. Winning operators benefit from proven estimating, production management, lead generation, and subcontractor systems rather than building a contractor brand from zero.

**Market Size:** $500B+ home improvement market with large local services niches  
**Target:** Homeowners, property managers, HOAs, light commercial owners  
**Examples:** Five Star Painting, Floor Coverings International, House Doctors, Mosquito Joe

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** Operations-minded owner with sales discipline  
**Secondary:** Former contractors, project managers, corporate professionals, family operators  
**Decision Maker:** Franchisee or ownership group

**Budget:** $75K-$200K investment  
**Pain Urgency:** High because buyers want project execution systems and a repeatable local lead engine

---

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Lead generation consistency ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Local service demand is fragmented and hard to predict.  
**Current Solution:** Buy leads, run seasonal ads, ask for referrals.  
**Cost of Pain:** Empty crews and $5K-$20K monthly revenue swings.  
**Your Solution:** Local SEO, referral loops, outbound partnerships, and scripted estimates.  
**Pricing:** 1-2% marketing fund plus local spend.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many estimate requests do you need each month to hit goal revenue?"
- "What channels would you trust to produce repeatable local demand?"

---

### 2. Estimating accuracy
**Pain:** Bad estimates destroy margin and trust.  
**Current Solution:** Price from experience or competitor comparisons.  
**Cost of Pain:** 10-20% gross margin loss and change-order friction.  
**Your Solution:** Estimating tools, scope templates, and pricing guardrails.  
**Pricing:** Included in operations support.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What does one underbid project cost you?"
- "How will you standardize pricing across jobs and teams?"

---

### 3. Labor or subcontractor quality
**Pain:** Service quality depends on crews the owner may not control tightly.  
**Current Solution:** Use whoever is available locally.  
**Cost of Pain:** Rework, warranty issues, and bad reviews.  
**Your Solution:** Vendor scorecards, onboarding standards, and QA routines.  
**Pricing:** Included in support.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How will you qualify crews before they represent your brand?"
- "What is one callback worth in labor and reputation?"

---

### 4. Project scheduling and communication
**Pain:** Home service projects fall apart when scheduling changes are not communicated.  
**Current Solution:** Use texts, spreadsheets, and manual follow-up.  
**Cost of Pain:** Delays, no-shows, and admin overload.  
**Your Solution:** Job management software and customer communication workflows.  
**Pricing:** $150-$350/month technology fee.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How will you keep customers updated when schedules shift?"
- "What happens when three projects hit delays on the same day?"

---

### 5. Seasonality and backlog planning
**Pain:** Demand often spikes and drops by season or storm patterns.  
**Current Solution:** React after pipeline slows.  
**Cost of Pain:** Revenue volatility and poor labor utilization.  
**Your Solution:** Marketing calendars, backlog targets, and off-season offers.  
**Pricing:** Included in coaching.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How much backlog do you want booked at any time?"
- "What off-season services could stabilize revenue?"

---

### 6. Material pricing and procurement
**Pain:** Material price swings compress margin quickly.  
**Current Solution:** Buy from local suppliers one project at a time.  
**Cost of Pain:** 3-8 margin points and delayed jobs.  
**Your Solution:** Preferred vendors, pricing agreements, and approved SKU lists.  
**Pricing:** Included in network benefits.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Which materials hit your margins hardest when prices move?"
- "How much leverage would preferred pricing create?"

---

### 7. Review generation and trust
**Pain:** Property owners choose based on reviews and visual proof.  
**Current Solution:** Ask for reviews inconsistently.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower close rates and more price shopping.  
**Your Solution:** Review automations, before/after proof, and reputation workflows.  
**Pricing:** Included in tech stack.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many strong reviews do you need to dominate your market?"
- "How will you collect before-and-after proof on every job?"

---

### 8. Sales follow-up discipline
**Pain:** Many estimates never get properly followed up.  
**Current Solution:** Owner calls when time allows.  
**Cost of Pain:** 10-30% of quotes die without a real close attempt.  
**Your Solution:** CRM cadences, quote follow-up sequences, and financing scripts.  
**Pricing:** Included in support.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What percent of quotes are you closing today?"
- "How will you follow up on undecided customers?"

---

### 9. Financing offers for homeowners
**Pain:** Many property services are easier to close with payment options.  
**Current Solution:** Offer cash, check, or card only.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower ticket size and lower close rate.  
**Your Solution:** Financing partners and scripts for monthly-payment framing.  
**Pricing:** Included via vendor relationships.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How often do customers delay projects because of out-of-pocket cost?"
- "What higher-ticket jobs would financing help you close?"

---

### 10. Insurance and claims management
**Pain:** Damage claims or jobsite incidents can erase profit fast.  
**Current Solution:** Generic insurance and reactive problem-solving.  
**Cost of Pain:** Deductibles, lawsuits, and reputation damage.  
**Your Solution:** Approved coverage packages, claim processes, and risk SOPs.  
**Pricing:** Included in vendor support.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What claims scenario worries you most?"
- "How prepared are you to handle a damage complaint professionally?"

---

### 11. Territory development
**Pain:** Owners spread too thin instead of dominating a defined trade area.  
**Current Solution:** Take work anywhere it appears.  
**Cost of Pain:** High drive time and diluted marketing.  
**Your Solution:** Territory planning, density targets, and route discipline.  
**Pricing:** Included in franchise agreement.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What geographic density do you need for healthy operations?"
- "How far is too far for profitable work?"

---

### 12. KPI visibility
**Pain:** Owners cannot clearly see close rate, gross margin, or crew utilization.  
**Current Solution:** Use QuickBooks and memory.  
**Cost of Pain:** Slow corrections and weak profitability.  
**Your Solution:** Dashboards and peer benchmarks.  
**Pricing:** Included in software.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Which metric would tell you first that the business is drifting?"
- "How will you compare performance without network data?"

---

### 13. Add-on service expansion
**Pain:** Many operators leave easy adjacent revenue untapped.  
**Current Solution:** Sell only the core service.  
**Cost of Pain:** $50K-$200K missed annual revenue.  
**Your Solution:** Cross-sell playbooks and bundled offers.  
**Pricing:** Optional add-on kits $500-$3K.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Which add-on services fit naturally with your core offer?"
- "How important is average ticket growth in year one?"

---

### 14. Owner dependence
**Pain:** Many local service brands rely on the founder for every sale and issue.  
**Current Solution:** Owner wears every hat.  
**Cost of Pain:** Burnout and limited scale.  
**Your Solution:** Role design, admin support systems, and management cadences.  
**Pricing:** Included in coaching.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What tasks must the owner stop doing before growth becomes possible?"
- "How much of your week do you want in sales versus operations?"

---

### 15. Resale value
**Pain:** Independent service businesses are harder to sell when systems are weak.  
**Current Solution:** Hope a local buyer appears later.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower valuation and longer exit timelines.  
**Your Solution:** Branded systems, reporting, and transfer support.  
**Pricing:** Included in network value.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Do you want a cash-flowing job or a transferable local asset?"
- "What exit multiple would make this investment compelling?"

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"Own a local property services business with the estimate, production, and lead-gen systems already proven."

### Authority Builders
1. Estimate accuracy checklist
2. Local-service close-rate benchmark report
3. Before/after marketing guide
4. Crew QA scorecard
5. Territory density planner

### Positioning Angles
- **Trust at Home:** Consumers buy reliability before they buy the service.
- **Operational Discipline:** Margin is won in estimating and production, not just sales.
- **Local Market Density:** Tight territory focus beats scattered jobs.

### Differentiators
- Repeatable estimating and QA systems
- Better local lead generation and follow-up discipline
- Material and vendor leverage
- Clear add-on revenue expansion paths

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Buyers who want passive ownership from day one
- ❌ Under-capitalized operators with no sales runway
- ❌ Owners unwilling to manage crews closely
- ❌ Operators who treat estimating casually
- ❌ Buyers who chase too many services too early

**Best Prospects:**
- Strong sales and operations instincts
- Comfort with customer-facing project management
- Willingness to follow process and inspect quality
- Interest in building a dense local route

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"I could just start my own painting or flooring company."**
> "You can, but the expensive part is not filing an LLC. It is learning estimating, staffing, reviews, marketing, and production the hard way while protecting margin."

**"This seems too operationally messy."**
> "It is operationally heavy, which is exactly why systems matter. The right model reduces improvisation and makes daily execution more manageable."

**"Customers in my market only care about price."**
> "Price matters, but homeowners invite brands they trust into valuable assets. Reviews, process, and financing options often matter more than being the cheapest."

**"Finding good crews is too hard."**
> "That challenge never disappears. The advantage is having better screening, QA, and expectations than local independents."

**"I do not want to deal with angry homeowners."**
> "Then this is probably the wrong category. The best operators win because they communicate clearly and solve problems faster than competitors."
