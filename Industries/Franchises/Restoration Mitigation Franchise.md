# Restoration Mitigation Franchise

**Category:** Franchises  
**Sub-Category:** Property Restoration/Emergency Services  
**Entry Cost:** $100K-$300K  
**Timeline:** 3-6 months to launch  
**Revenue Model:** $50K-$100K franchise fee + 6-10% royalty

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Restoration franchises provide emergency water damage mitigation, fire/smoke cleanup, mold remediation, and reconstruction services. This recession-resistant, insurance-driven industry benefits from predictable demand and high average tickets.

**Market Size:** $210B restoration industry, growing 5% annually  
**Target:** Homeowners, commercial property managers, insurance companies  
**Examples:** Servpro, ServiceMaster Restore, Rainbow Restoration, 911 Restoration

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile (Franchisee)

**Primary Buyer:** Entrepreneur seeking B2B/B2C hybrid model  
**Secondary:** Contractors, insurance professionals, corporate refugees  
**Demographics:** 35-55 years old, $250K+ net worth, $100K+ liquid capital

**Budget:** $100K-$300K investment  
**Pain Urgency:** High (stable demand, insurance-backed revenue, scalability)

---

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points (Franchisee Perspective)

### 1. Insurance Relationships ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** 80% of restoration work comes through insurance referrals—without carrier relationships, you starve
**Current Solution:** Cold call adjusters, hope for referrals, work directly with homeowners
**Cost of Pain:** Slow growth, price competition, feast-or-famine workflow
**Your Solution:** Preferred vendor programs, national insurance partnerships, direct claim assignments
**Pricing:** $50K-$100K franchise fee

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Do you have existing relationships with insurance adjusters or carriers?"
- "How will you get on preferred vendor lists without brand recognition?"
- "What percentage of restoration companies fail due to lack of insurance referrals?"

---

### 2. Emergency Response Systems
**Pain:** Water damage requires 24/7 emergency response—missed calls = lost jobs
**Current Solution:** Forward to cell phone, hope you answer, delayed response
**Cost of Pain:** Lost jobs to competitors, reputation damage, revenue loss
**Your Solution:** 24/7 call center, dispatch protocols, emergency response procedures, after-hours coverage
**Pricing:** Included in operations

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Who answers your phone at 2am when a pipe bursts?"
- "What happens if you miss an emergency call from an insurance adjuster?"
- "How quickly do you need to respond to win water damage jobs?"

---

### 3. Technical Training & Certification
**Pain:** Restoration requires IICRC certifications and specialized knowledge
**Current Solution:** Pay for training independently, limited ongoing education
**Cost of Pain:** Inadequate services, liability issues, lost certifications
**Your Solution:** IICRC certification programs, ongoing technical training, equipment operation, safety protocols
**Pricing:** Included in training

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Are you familiar with IICRC certification requirements?"
- "What happens if technicians aren't properly trained on mold remediation?"
- "How much does comprehensive restoration training cost independently?"

---

### 4. Equipment & Technology
**Pain:** Professional restoration requires expensive specialized equipment
**Current Solution:** Purchase retail, limited financing options, outdated technology
**Cost of Pain:** High startup costs, inefficient equipment, competitive disadvantage
**Your Solution:** Equipment packages, financing programs, technology updates, volume discounts
**Pricing:** Included in vendor programs

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Have you priced industrial drying equipment, air scrubbers, and moisture meters?"
- "What happens when equipment breaks during a large loss job?"
- "How much could group purchasing save on $150K+ in equipment?"

---

**Pricing:** Included in operations

**Discovery Questions:**

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"The franchise fee and royalty are expensive for a contractor business."**
> "Restoration isn't typical contracting—it's insurance-driven with specialized requirements. The franchise fee buys you preferred vendor status with major carriers, which takes years to develop independently. Our systems include Xactimate training, 24/7 call handling, and adjuster relationship protocols that win jobs. Most importantly, restoration jobs average $5K-$50K—with the right insurance relationships, revenue adds up fast."

**"Why pay higher royalties (6-10%) than other franchises?"**
> "Restoration royalties are higher because the support is more intensive—24/7 emergency dispatch, insurance company management, estimating support, and technical training. But consider the economics: with average tickets of $5K-$50K and insurance backing payments, the royalty is a small percentage of high-value jobs. Our franchisees typically achieve $1M+ revenue faster than independents due to carrier relationships."

**"I have contractor experience—why do I need a franchise?"**
> "Contracting skills are valuable, but restoration is different—it's insurance-driven, not bid-driven. Success requires adjuster relationships, Xactimate expertise, 24/7 availability, and specialized knowledge (mold, fire, water). Many skilled contractors struggle in restoration because they lack the insurance side. Our systems bridge that gap and provide the carrier relationships you need."

**"How do I get insurance companies to refer work to me?"**
> "That's the #1 challenge and exactly why our franchise model works. We have national preferred vendor agreements with major carriers, local adjuster outreach programs, and brand recognition that gets you in the door. Independents spend years trying to build these relationships—we provide them from day one. What's your plan to get on carrier preferred vendor lists without brand backing?"

**"What about the long hours and emergency calls?"**
> "Restoration requires flexibility, but our systems help—you get 24/7 call center support so you're not answering every call personally. As you grow, you build an on-call team. The trade-off is recession-resistant, insurance-backed revenue with high tickets. Many owners find the schedule manageable with proper systems and staffing. Are you willing to be flexible for predictable, high-value work?"

**"Isn't restoration just storm chasing?"**
> "Weather events create spikes, but 60-70% of restoration work is everyday emergencies—pipe bursts, appliance leaks, small fires. Our systems generate consistent baseline work through insurance relationships, not just storm chasing. Plus, we're positioned to scale up when storms hit. The diversity creates stable revenue."
