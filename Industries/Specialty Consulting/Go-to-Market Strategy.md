## 🎯 The Opportunity

Post-PMF startups face a critical inflection point: they have product-market fit but lack a systematic path to customers. The "if we build it, they will come" mentality fails, and founders burn precious runway chasing scattered opportunities instead of building repeatable growth engines. This is the highest-leverage moment for an outside advisor—when the product works but the go-to-market (GTM) machine doesn't.

The opportunity serves Series A-C startups ($2M-$20M ARR) and established companies launching new products. These organizations have budget urgency (runway matters) and can measure results quickly (months, not years).

**Market Size:** $8B+ GTM consulting market, expanding with SaaS explosion  
**Target:** Post-PMF B2B startups and new product launches in established companies  
**Examples:** Stage 2 Capital, Toptal GTM strategists, independent advisors like Jason Lemkin's network commanding $300-$500/hour

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** CEO/Founder (startups), VP of Product or General Manager (established cos)  
**Secondary:** VP of Sales (if hired), Head of Marketing, Board members pushing for growth  
**Decision Maker:** CEO at startups; Division President/GM at larger companies

**Budget:** $25K-$60K for strategy; $10K-$20K/month for ongoing advisory  
**Pain Urgency:** High—burn rate pressure, board expectations, competitive threats create immediate need

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. No Clear ICP Definition ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** "Everyone could use our product" thinking leads to scattered sales efforts. Marketing speaks to no one. Sales chases unqualified leads. Resources diluted across too many segments.

**Current Solution:** Basic demographic filters, sales team "gut feel," copying competitors' positioning

**Cost of Pain:** 40-60% of sales time wasted on wrong prospects; CAC 3-5x higher than focused competitors; marketing budget spread thin with poor ROI; 12-18 months of runway burned without repeatable growth

**Your Solution:** Deep ICP research using closed-won/closed-lost analysis, ideal customer scoring model, segmentation framework, ICP-driven messaging

**Pricing:** $30K-$45K for ICP strategy + $8K/month for 6 months advisory

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Describe your perfect customer—the one you wish you could clone 100 times."
- "What percentage of your leads actually close, and what's the common profile of those that do?"
- "When you look at your best customers, what do they have in common beyond just being in the same industry?"
- "Have you ever turned down a deal because the customer wasn't the right fit?"

---

### 2. No Repeatable Sales Motion
**Pain:** Every deal is different. No standard process, no playbook, no predictability. What worked for the first 10 customers doesn't scale.

**Current Solution:** Founder doing all selling, scattered notes in CRM, "we'll figure it out as we go"

**Cost of Pain:** Founder trapped in sales role (can't focus on product/strategy); can't hire sales team effectively (no process to teach); month-to-month revenue unpredictability; fundraising becomes difficult without growth metrics

**Your Solution:** Sales methodology selection (MEDDIC, SPIN, etc.), stage definitions and exit criteria, playbook creation, sales hire readiness assessment

**Pricing:** $25K-$50K for sales motion design

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Walk me through your last 5 deals—were they all sold the same way or completely different?"
- "If you had to hire a salesperson tomorrow, what would you teach them about how to sell?"
- "What's your current sales cycle length, and how much does it vary deal to deal?"

---

### 3. Marketing-Sales Misalignment
**Pain:** Marketing generates leads sales ignores. Sales complains about lead quality. Marketing says sales doesn't follow up. Finger-pointing instead of revenue.

**Current Solution:** Weekly "alignment meetings" that devolve into blame sessions, SLAs that aren't enforced, separate goals and comp plans

**Cost of Pain:** 50-70% of marketing leads never contacted; sales team ignores 80% of MQLs; marketing budget wasted on wrong audience; turnover in both departments from frustration

**Your Solution:** Unified funnel metrics, lead scoring and qualification criteria, service level agreements with teeth, integrated comp plan design

**Pricing:** $20K-$40K for alignment framework + quarterly business reviews

---

### 4. Channel Strategy Confusion
**Pain:** Unclear whether to sell direct, through partners, PLG, or enterprise sales. Trying to do everything, succeeding at nothing.

**Current Solution:** Following what worked for the founder's network, copying competitor channels, opportunistic partnerships without strategy

**Cost of Pain:** Resources spread across ineffective channels; channel conflicts when direct and partner compete; missed opportunities in best-fit channel

**Your Solution:** Channel-fit assessment, channel strategy and prioritization, partner program design, hybrid GTM architecture

**Pricing:** $25K-$45K for channel strategy

---

### 5. Pricing Model Doesn't Match GTM
**Pain:** Self-serve product with enterprise pricing (or vice versa). Freemium with no conversion path. Pricing friction kills deals.

**Current Solution:** Pricing set by gut feel, copying competitors, "we'll optimize later"

**Cost of Pain:** Wrong buyers enter funnel; sales cycles balloon; CAC payback periods unsustainable; growth stalled despite product-market fit

**Your Solution:** Pricing-GTM alignment analysis, packaging for each channel, friction reduction, trial-to-paid optimization

**Pricing:** $20K-$35K for pricing-GTM integration

---

### 6. No Clear Competitive Positioning
**Pain:** Prospects ask "how are you different from [competitor]?" and get vague answers. Sales team lacks consistent differentiation story.

**Current Solution:** Feature comparison grids, "we're better" claims without proof, positioning that changes with each sales call

**Cost of Pain:** Lost deals to inferior products with better positioning; commoditization leading to price pressure; inability to command premium pricing

**Your Solution:** Competitive landscape analysis, differentiation framework, battle cards and objection handling, category positioning strategy

**Pricing:** $25K-$40K for positioning strategy

---

### 7. No Metrics or Growth Framework
**Pain:** Flying blind on what's working. Can't answer basic questions about CAC, LTV, payback period. Board decks lack credible growth projections.

**Current Solution:** Spreadsheets updated manually before board meetings, vanity metrics (website traffic), guessing on what's driving growth

**Cost of Pain:** Can't make data-driven decisions; waste budget on ineffective channels; fundraising fails due to weak metrics; strategic pivots based on hunches not data

**Your Solution:** North Star metric definition, growth accounting framework, dashboard design, board-ready metrics package

**Pricing:** $20K-$35K for metrics framework

---

### 8. Founder Dependency in Sales
**Pain:** CEO/founder is the only one who can sell. Growth capped by founder's time. Can't scale beyond personal network.

**Current Solution:** Founder works 80-hour weeks, keeps promising to "hire sales," occasionally interviews sales candidates without clear hiring criteria

**Cost of Pain:** Founder burnout; growth plateaus; investors question scalability; company stuck at $1-3M ARR for years

**Your Solution:** Founder-led sales transition plan, first sales hire profile and interview process, sales enablement for non-founders, 90-day ramp program

**Pricing:** $30K-$50K for sales transition strategy

---

### 9. Messaging Doesn't Resonate
**Pain:** Website copy falls flat. Demos don't convert. Prospects say "interesting" but don't buy. Value proposition unclear to target buyers.

**Current Solution:** Copy written by technical founders, messaging focused on features not outcomes, website designed by committee

**Cost of Pain:** 1-2% website conversion rates (should be 5-10%); demo show-up rates below 50%; low email response rates; brand confusion in market

**Your Solution:** Customer research and voice-of-customer analysis, messaging hierarchy, website copy rewrite, demo script optimization

**Pricing:** $25K-$45K for messaging + copy

---

### 10. Wrong Team Structure
**Pain:** Hired marketing before sales (or vice versa). Team structure doesn't match GTM strategy. Key roles unfilled or duplicated.

**Current Solution:** Hiring based on "we need more leads" or "we need to close more deals" without strategic org design

**Cost of Pain:** 6-12 months wasted with wrong hires; $150K-$300K in severance and recruiting costs; remaining team demoralized; delayed growth

**Your Solution:** GTM org design, role definitions and hiring sequence, interview scorecards, 12-month hiring roadmap

**Pricing:** $20K-$35K for org design

---

### 11. Expansion Revenue Strategy Missing
**Pain:** Focused entirely on new logos, ignoring expansion from existing customers. Net revenue retention below 100%.

**Current Solution:** Reactive upselling when customers "ask for more," no systematic expansion motion, customer success focused on support not growth

**Cost of Pain:** Growth relies entirely on new sales (expensive); customer lifetime value 30-50% lower than potential; high churn when customers outgrow initial contracts

**Your Solution:** Expansion revenue playbook, usage-based growth triggers, customer success-led expansion, land-and-expand strategy

**Pricing:** $25K-$40K for expansion strategy

---

### 12. International Expansion Chaos
**Pain:** Opportunistic deals in new countries without strategy. Support and legal issues emerge. Resources stretched thin.

**Current Solution:** "We got an inbound from Germany, should we support it?" approach, reactive localization, no market prioritization

**Cost of Pain:** Support nightmares with time zones and languages; legal/compliance exposure; 3-6 months wasted on markets that will never be big enough

**Your Solution:** Market prioritization framework, international GTM playbook, localization strategy, legal/compliance readiness assessment

**Pricing:** $30K-$50K for international strategy

---

### 13. No Clear GTM Metrics for Product Team
**Pain:** Product builds features in vacuum. Sales promises features to close deals. Roadmap driven by loudest customers, not strategic priorities.

**Current Solution:** Product-sprint-sales in separate silos, feature requests tracked in spreadsheets, roadmap presented as fait accompli

**Cost of Pain:** Engineering time wasted on one-off features; sales team selling roadmap not current product; strategic initiatives delayed

**Your Solution:** GTM feedback loop to product, sales-validated roadmap input, product-market fit measurement, feature prioritization framework

**Pricing:** $20K-$35K for product-GTM alignment

---

### 14. Event/Field Marketing Without Strategy
**Pain:** Random trade show attendance with no follow-up. Events chosen based on "we always go" not ROI. Field marketing disconnected from sales.

**Current Solution:** Marketing picks events, sales complains about leads, no measurement of event ROI

**Cost of Pain:** $50K-$200K wasted annually on ineffective events; sales team pulled away from selling to staff booths; no pipeline attribution

**Your Solution:** Event strategy and selection criteria, field marketing playbook, lead capture and follow-up process, event ROI measurement

**Pricing:** $15K-$30K for event strategy

---

### 15. Board/Investor Communication on GTM
**Pain:** Can't articulate GTM strategy to board. Metrics presented inconsistently. Investors question whether company can scale.

**Current Solution:** Scrambling before board meetings, defensive answers to growth questions, constantly changing story

**Cost of Pain:** Down rounds or inability to raise; board losing confidence in management; strategic misalignment with investors; CEO distracted by board prep instead of execution

**Your Solution:** Board-ready GTM narrative, consistent metrics and reporting cadence, investor communication templates, growth story documentation

**Pricing:** $20K-$35K for board narrative + quarterly prep

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Pre-PMF companies still searching for product-market fit (GTM won't fix bad product)
- ❌ Companies with <6 months runway (not enough time to implement)
- ❌ Founders who won't delegate sales (need founder buy-in on transition)
- ❌ Organizations where product, sales, and marketing refuse to align
- ❌ Buyers looking for "silver bullet" without willingness to change

**Best Prospects:**
- $2M-$20M ARR with clear product-market fit signals
- CEO/founder who recognizes they're the bottleneck in sales
- Series A-C stage with board pressure for growth
- Willingness to invest in proper GTM infrastructure
- 12+ months runway to implement changes

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We're already working on this internally."**
> "That's great—you're ahead of most companies. The question is whether internal efforts are moving the needle. Most startups spend 12-18 months 'working on GTM' without building true repeatability. Our audit identifies exactly where you're losing deals and wasting spend. Would you like to see a 30-day diagnostic that benchmarks you against companies that successfully scaled?"

**"We can't afford this right now—we're conserving cash."**
> "I understand runway is precious. Here's a different frame: you're currently spending $1M+ annually on GTM activities that aren't optimized. A $30K investment to fix that has a 30:1 ROI in year one. More importantly, without systematic GTM, you're burning cash on ineffective channels. What's your current CAC, and do you believe it's as efficient as it could be?"

**"We tried hiring a sales consultant before and it didn't work."**
> "Unfortunately common—many 'sales consultants' just teach generic methodologies without understanding your specific situation. Our approach is different: we start with deep customer research to understand your ICP, then build a GTM strategy tailored to your product and market. No cookie-cutter playbooks. Would you be open to hearing about our specific methodology?"

**"Our investors/board want us to figure this out ourselves."**
> "Boards often push for self-sufficiency, which makes sense. But here's what we've seen: companies that try to 'figure it out' alone take 18-24 months to build GTM fit; companies that get expert help do it in 6-12 months. At your current burn rate, what's the cost of an extra 12 months? We can work with your board to show clear milestones and ROI."

**"We need someone full-time, not a consultant."**
> "You're absolutely right—long-term you need internal GTM leadership. We actually help with that: we build the GTM foundation, then help you hire the right VP Sales or CMO who can execute against the strategy. Most companies hire the person first, then try to figure out the strategy. That often leads to expensive mis-hires. We flip the order: strategy first, then the right hire."

## 📞 Discovery Questions

**To CEO/Founder:**
- "What's your current ARR, and what's your growth rate over the last 12 months?"
- "How much of your time is spent on sales vs. other responsibilities?"
- "What's your biggest frustration with how you're currently acquiring customers?"
- "If you could wave a wand and fix one thing about your GTM, what would it be?"

**To VP Sales (if exists):**
- "Walk me through your sales process—how standardized is it deal to deal?"
- "What's your current quota attainment across the team?"
- "What percentage of leads from marketing are actually qualified?"
- "What's your sales cycle length, and how much does it vary?"

**To Board Member/Investor:**
- "What are the top 3 GTM metrics you want to see improvement on in the next quarter?"
- "What's your confidence level that the current team can scale GTM to the next level?"
- "What would success look like 12 months from now in terms of growth and efficiency?"
