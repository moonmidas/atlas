# Template: Sales Process Optimization

**Category:** Specialty Consulting  
**Sub-Category:** Revenue Operations  
**Entry Cost:** $3K-$8K

**Revenue Model:** Fixed project fee + performance bonus tied to outcomes

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Sales teams waste 60% of their time on non-selling activities: forecasting, administrative work, deal desk negotiations, and chasing bad leads. Meanwhile, deals stall in pipeline stages for weeks with no clear next steps, forecasting is guesswork, and win rates languish below 20%. Sales process optimization consulting transforms chaotic sales operations into predictable revenue machines.

This opportunity serves B2B companies with $5M-$100M revenue who have sales teams but lack systematic processes. The ROI is immediately measurable—shorter sales cycles, higher win rates, and improved forecast accuracy.

**Market Size:** $6B+ sales optimization consulting market  
**Target:** B2B companies with 5-50 sales reps seeking operational excellence  
**Examples:** Sandler Training, Force Management, Challenger Inc., independent sales consultants

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Inaccurate Sales Forecasting ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Forecast calls are exercises in hope, not data. Deals slip constantly. Board meetings become defensive. Cash flow planning is impossible.

**Current Solution:** Sales rep self-reporting, "gut feel" adjustments by management, spreadsheets updated manually before board meetings

**Cost of Pain:** Missed board expectations; investor confidence erosion; cash crunches from surprise misses; CEO/CRO spending 10-15 hours weekly on forecast calls instead of strategy

**Your Solution:** Forecast methodology (commit vs. best case vs. pipeline), stage-weighted forecasting, objective exit criteria, deal inspection cadence

**Pricing:** $25K-$40K for forecasting transformation

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your forecast accuracy rate—how often do you hit within 10% of your commit?"
- "How much time do you spend weekly on forecast calls and deal reviews?"
- "When deals slip, is it usually a surprise or did you see it coming?"
- "What percentage of your quarter comes from deals that were in commit at the start vs. slipped from prior quarters?"

---

### 2. Deals Stalled in Pipeline
**Pain:** Opportunities sit in stages for weeks with no progression. Sales reps don't know how to advance them. Pipeline becomes bloated with zombie deals.

**Current Solution:** Weekly "pipeline reviews" that just update close dates, sales rep chasing with "just checking in" emails, hoping deals will move on their own

**Cost of Pain:** 40-50% of pipeline is stale (no activity in 30+ days); sales time wasted on unwinnable deals; forecasting inflated by fake opportunities; 6-12 month sales cycles when 3-4 months is achievable

**Your Solution:** Stage-exit criteria definitions, next-step requirement enforcement, deal advancement playbooks, pipeline hygiene processes

**Pricing:** $20K-$35K for pipeline velocity improvement

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What percentage of your pipeline hasn't had activity in the last 30 days?"
- "What's your average time in each stage, and what should it be?"
- "When deals stall, what do your reps typically do to get them moving?"

---

### 3. No Standardized Sales Methodology
**Pain:** Every rep sells differently. No common language for deals. Can't diagnose what's working or coach effectively. New hires struggle to ramp.

**Current Solution:** Ad-hoc training during onboarding, "shadowing" top performers, sporadic coaching conversations

**Cost of Pain:** 12-18 month ramp time for new hires (should be 6-9 months); inconsistent customer experience; can't scale what works; top performer reliance

**Your Solution:** Methodology selection and implementation (MEDDIC, Challenger, SPIN, etc.), sales playbook creation, manager coaching framework, certification program

**Pricing:** $30K-$50K for methodology implementation

---

### 4. Poor Discovery and Qualification
**Pain:** Reps demo too early, miss key stakeholder engagement, chase deals that will never close. "Happy ears" lead to wasted effort.

**Current Solution:** Generic discovery questions, BANT qualification (outdated), reps deciding what's "qualified" without standards

**Cost of Pain:** 40-50% demo-to-close rates (should be 60-80%); 6+ demos per rep weekly with low conversion; chasing RFPs without decision criteria knowledge

**Your Solution:** Advanced discovery framework, MEDDPICC qualification, multi-threading strategy, qualification scorecards

**Pricing:** $25K-$40K for discovery optimization

### 5. Sales Ops and Admin Burden

**Current Solution:** CRM mandates without workflow support, manual quote generation, sales asking "what's the status?" constantly

**Cost of Pain:** 20-30 hours weekly per rep on admin (should be <10); low quota attainment from lack of selling time; rep turnover from frustration

**Your Solution:** Sales process automation, proposal templates and workflow, CRM optimization, admin task reduction analysis

**Pricing:** $20K-$35K for operational efficiency

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"We already use a sales methodology."**
> "Most companies have bought into MEDDIC, Challenger, or similar—which is great. But adoption is the real challenge. We find that 70% of teams with 'implemented' methodologies still struggle with consistent execution. We focus on operationalizing methodology: embedding it in your CRM, enabling managers to coach to it, and measuring compliance. Are you confident your team is consistently executing your current methodology?"

**"This sounds like it's just about CRM discipline."**
> "CRM hygiene is one piece, but process optimization goes much deeper. It's about defining what 'qualified' actually means in your business, creating consistent deal advancement criteria, building playbooks that reps actually use, and designing manager coaching cadences. Think of CRM as the system of record—we optimize the system of action around it. What's your biggest frustration with how deals move through your pipeline?"

**"Our sales team will resist process changes."**
> "Resistance is natural—top performers especially hate change. That's why our approach starts with listening to the team, not imposing solutions. We identify what top performers already do well and codify it. We involve reps in building playbooks. And we show them how process saves them time and helps them make more money. Have you tried implementing process changes before? What resistance did you see?"

**"We need to focus on lead gen, not process optimization."**
> "Lead gen is important, but pouring more leads into a leaky bucket is expensive. Let's say you double your lead flow—if your current win rate is 15% and we can get it to 25%, that's a bigger revenue impact than 2x leads. Plus, process optimization often reveals you don't need more leads, you just need to stop wasting time on bad ones. What's your current cost per qualified opportunity?"

**"Can't our VP Sales just do this internally?"**
> "Your VP Sales absolutely should drive this—but most VPs are drowning in forecast calls, board prep, and hiring. Process optimization requires dedicated project time that most VPs don't have. We act as an extension of your sales leadership team, bringing best practices from 50+ companies and doing the heavy lifting of documentation and implementation. What's your VP's current time allocation?"
