# Template: Direct Primary Care (DPC)

**Category:** Medical Practices  
**Sub-Category:** Primary Care - Membership Model  
**Entry Cost:** $150K-$400K  
**Timeline:** 6-12 months  
**Revenue Model:** Monthly membership fees ($50-$100/patient)

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

Direct Primary Care (DPC) is a revolutionary healthcare model that eliminates insurance companies from the primary care relationship. Physicians charge patients a flat monthly fee in exchange for unlimited access to primary care services. This model dramatically improves the doctor-patient relationship while creating predictable, recurring revenue for physicians. DPC practices typically care for 600-800 patients compared to 2,500+ in traditional fee-for-service models, allowing for more personalized care and reduced physician burnout.

The DPC movement is experiencing explosive growth, with over 2,000 practices nationwide and growing 20% annually. Patients love the unlimited access, transparent pricing, and time with their doctor. Physicians escape the administrative burden of insurance billing and enjoy higher job satisfaction with better work-life balance.

**Market Size:** $2.4B growing to $7.2B by 2030  
**Target:** Self-insured employers, uninsured individuals, high-deductible plan holders  
**Examples:** Qliance (pioneer), Paladina Health, Everside Health, numerous independent practices

**Primary Buyer:** Individuals with high-deductible health plans, small business owners, freelancers  
**Secondary:** Self-insured employers seeking to reduce healthcare costs

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Surprise Medical Bills and Pricing Opacity ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Patients receive unexpected bills weeks or months after appointments, often for amounts they can't afford. Traditional practices can't provide upfront pricing because insurance reimbursement varies unpredictably.

**Current Solution:** Patients call insurance companies, wait for EOBs, and often delay or avoid care due to cost uncertainty.

**Cost of Pain:** 46% of Americans delay care due to cost concerns; average surprise bill $1,200-$3,000; financial stress and delayed treatment leading to worse outcomes.

**Your Solution:** Transparent, flat monthly membership with no copays, deductibles, or surprise bills. All primary care included at a known, predictable cost.

**Pricing:** $60-$100/month individual; includes unlimited visits, same/next-day appointments, 24/7 access to physician via phone/text/email.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many times have you received a medical bill you weren't expecting in the past year?"
- "When you call a doctor's office, can they tell you exactly what your visit will cost?"
- "How much did you spend out-of-pocket on primary care last year, and how much of that was a surprise?"

---

### 2. Impossible Appointment Availability
**Pain:** Patients wait 2-6 weeks for routine appointments and can't get same-day care when sick. Traditional practices book 15-minute slots for 2,500+ patients, creating chronic access problems.

**Current Solution:** Urgent care ($150-$300/visit), ER visits ($1,000+), or suffering through illness.

**Cost of Pain:** Lost wages from missing work ($200-$500/day), extended illness duration, anxiety about health issues, unnecessary ER visits averaging $1,400.

**Your Solution:** Same-day or next-day appointments guaranteed; unhurried 30-60 minute visits; direct physician access via secure messaging and phone.

**Pricing:** Included in monthly membership - no per-visit charges.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "The last time you were sick, how long did you wait to see your doctor?"
- "How much time do you typically spend with your doctor during appointments?"
- "If you have a health question after hours, what do you do?"

---

### 3. Insurance Administrative Burden on Patients
**Pain:** Patients navigate complex insurance networks, prior authorizations, claims denials, and explanation of benefits that require a degree to understand. Insurance becomes a barrier to care, not an enabler.

**Current Solution:** Hours on hold with insurance companies, hiring medical bill advocates, or skipping recommended care.

**Cost of Pain:** Average 12 hours/year dealing with insurance issues; $500-$2,000 in denied claims requiring appeal; treatment delays of weeks or months.

**Your Solution:** No insurance needed for primary care. Direct relationship between patient and physician. No claims, no authorizations, no networks to worry about.

**Pricing:** Monthly membership eliminates insurance complexity for primary care.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How many hours did you spend dealing with insurance paperwork or calls last year?"
- "Have you ever had a claim denied and had to fight it? How long did that take?"
- "Do you understand your Explanation of Benefits when you receive them?"

---

### 4. Rushed Appointments and Assembly Line Medicine
**Pain:** Traditional primary care appointments last 7-12 minutes on average. Patients feel unheard, doctors seem rushed, and complex issues get band-aid solutions or referrals.

**Current Solution:** Multiple appointments for single issues, specialist referrals ($200-$500/visit), or going untreated.

**Cost of Pain:** Incomplete diagnoses leading to complications; unnecessary specialist referrals (30% of primary care visits result in referrals); patient dissatisfaction and anxiety.

**Your Solution:** 30-60 minute appointments as needed. Unhurried consultations where patients feel heard. Time to address root causes, not just symptoms.

**Pricing:** Unlimited visit time included in membership; no per-visit limitations.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How long was your last doctor's appointment, and did you feel like all your concerns were addressed?"
- "Have you ever left a doctor's appointment feeling like you were rushed out the door?"
- "How many times have you had to schedule follow-up visits because everything couldn't be covered in one appointment?"

---

### 5. Lack of Direct Physician Access
**Pain:** After leaving an appointment, patients have no way to reach their doctor with follow-up questions. Phone trees, nurses, and delays create frustration and anxiety.

**Current Solution:** Calling the office, leaving messages, waiting for callbacks, or visiting urgent care for questions.

**Cost of Pain:** Average 24-48 hour callback delays; $150-$300 urgent care visits for simple questions; patient anxiety and confusion about treatment plans.

**Your Solution:** Direct physician cell phone and secure text/email access. Real-time answers to questions. Relationship-based medicine where you know your doctor.

**Pricing:** Included in membership - 24/7 access to your personal physician.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "If you have a question about your medication or treatment plan, how quickly can you reach your doctor?"
- "Have you ever gone to urgent care just because you couldn't reach your primary doctor?"
- "Do you have your doctor's direct contact information, or do you go through staff?"

---

### 6. Fragmented Care and Poor Care Coordination
**Pain:** When patients see specialists or visit ERs, their primary care doctor often doesn't know about it. Medical records don't follow patients, leading to duplicate tests and dangerous medication interactions.

**Current Solution:** Patients act as their own care coordinators, carrying records, repeating medical histories, and hoping nothing falls through cracks.

**Cost of Pain:** 30% of medical tests are duplicates due to lack of coordination; medication errors affect 1.5 million people annually; delayed or missed diagnoses.

**Your Solution:** DPC physician coordinates all care, maintains comprehensive records, communicates with specialists, and ensures continuity across the healthcare system.

**Pricing:** Care coordination included in membership; physician acts as patient's healthcare quarterback.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "When you see a specialist, does your primary doctor follow up with you about the results?"
- "How many times have you had to repeat the same medical tests because different doctors didn't share records?"
- "Do you feel like someone is overseeing all your healthcare, or are you managing it yourself?"

---

### 7. High-Deductible Health Plan Nightmare
**Pain:** Patients with HDHPs pay $3,000-$8,000+ out-of-pocket before insurance kicks in, yet still face inflated "chargemaster" prices for routine care that should cost a fraction of that amount.

**Current Solution:** Avoiding care until emergencies, paying inflated cash prices, or paying premiums for insurance they can't afford to use.

**Cost of Pain:** Delayed preventive care leading to serious conditions; paying 3-10x markup on routine services; financial stress from healthcare costs.

**Your Solution:** DPC provides unlimited primary care at predictable cost regardless of deductible status. Wholesale pricing on labs, imaging, and medications saves 50-90%.

**Pricing:** $60-$100/month + wholesale access to services typically costing 10x more through insurance.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "What's your health insurance deductible, and how much of it have you met this year?"
- "How much did you pay out-of-pocket for primary care last year despite having insurance?"
- "Have you avoided going to the doctor because you couldn't afford the deductible?"

### 8. Employer Healthcare Cost Crisis
**Pain:** Small and mid-size employers face 8-15% annual premium increases while employees still struggle with high out-of-pocket costs. Traditional insurance is unaffordable yet necessary for catastrophic coverage.

**Current Solution:** Reducing benefits, increasing employee cost-sharing, or dropping health benefits entirely (impossible for retention).

**Cost of Pain:** Average small business spends $8,000-$15,000/employee/year on health benefits; 30% cost increases over 3 years; employee dissatisfaction and turnover.

**Your Solution:** Pair DPC membership with high-deductible catastrophic plan. DPC handles 80% of employee healthcare needs at fixed cost. Employers save 20-40% on total healthcare spend.

**Pricing:** $50-$70/employee/month for DPC membership + lower premium HDHP = significant savings vs. traditional PPO.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How much have your health insurance premiums increased in the past three years?"
- "What percentage of your employees' healthcare costs come from primary care vs. catastrophic events?"
- "How many of your employees have delayed care due to high deductibles?"

### 9. Chronic Disease Management Failures
**Pain:** Patients with diabetes, hypertension, obesity, and other chronic conditions need frequent monitoring and support. Traditional model makes this financially unsustainable and administratively difficult.

**Current Solution:** Quarterly 15-minute visits, phone tag with doctors, expensive specialist referrals, and deteriorating health outcomes.

**Cost of Pain:** Uncontrolled chronic conditions cost $6,000-$20,000/year in complications; 50% of patients don't follow treatment plans due to access/cost barriers.

**Your Solution:** Unlimited visits and monitoring for chronic conditions. Nutrition counseling, lifestyle coaching, and medication management included. Real support, not rushed appointments.

**Pricing:** Chronic disease management included in flat monthly fee; no per-visit charges or copays for frequent monitoring.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "If you have a chronic condition, how often do you see your doctor to monitor it?"
- "Do you feel you get enough support managing your condition, or do you feel on your own between appointments?"
- "How much do you spend annually on copays and visits for your chronic condition management?"

### 10. Mental Health Integration Gap
**Pain:** Mental health and primary care are siloed in traditional medicine. Patients must navigate separate systems, providers don't communicate, and mental health issues get overlooked during rushed primary care visits.

**Current Solution:** Separate psychiatrist/psychologist visits ($150-$300/hour), often not covered by insurance, creating another barrier to integrated care.

**Cost of Pain:** Untreated depression/anxiety costs $4,000-$10,000/year in lost productivity and health complications; 60% of mental health issues are identified in primary care but poorly managed.

**Your Solution:** DPC physicians provide basic mental health screening, counseling, and medication management. Integration with mental health professionals at discounted rates.

**Pricing:** Mental health screening and basic counseling included; referrals to specialists at wholesale rates.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Does your current primary doctor screen for or discuss mental health during visits?"
- "If you needed mental health support, would you know where to start or how much it would cost?"
- "Do you feel your physical and mental healthcare are connected, or handled separately?"

### 11. Medication Cost Inflation
**Pain:** Prescription drug costs have skyrocketed, with many common medications costing $100-$500/month even with insurance. Patients skip doses or abandon prescriptions due to cost.

**Current Solution:** Shopping around pharmacies, using GoodRx, Canadian pharmacies, or going without needed medications.

**Cost of Pain:** Medication non-adherence costs $300 billion annually in avoidable hospitalizations; patients paying 300-500% markup on generic drugs through insurance.

**Your Solution:** DPC practices buy medications wholesale and dispense at cost to members. Savings of 50-90% on most medications. In-office dispensing convenience.

**Pricing:** Medications at wholesale cost plus small dispensing fee; example: Lipitor $10/month vs. $120 through insurance.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "How much do you spend monthly on prescription medications?"
- "Have you ever not filled a prescription or skipped doses due to cost?"
- "Do you know how much your medications actually cost, or what the pharmacy markup is?"

### 12. Preventive Care and Wellness Neglect
**Pain:** Insurance-based preventive care is limited to annual physicals. Patients who want to optimize health, not just treat sickness, find little support in the traditional model.

**Current Solution:** Paying out-of-pocket for wellness services, functional medicine, or ignoring prevention entirely.

**Cost of Pain:** Preventable chronic diseases cost $730 billion annually; patients paying $500-$2,000/year for wellness services outside insurance.

**Your Solution:** Comprehensive wellness focus including nutrition counseling, fitness planning, stress management, and proactive health optimization included in membership.

**Pricing:** Preventive and wellness services included; no additional charges for going beyond minimum insurance requirements.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Beyond the annual physical, what support do you get for staying healthy?"
- "Are you satisfied with a healthcare system that only treats you when you're sick?"
- "What would comprehensive wellness support look like for you?"

### 13. Administrative Complexity for Physicians
**Pain:** Doctors spend 2 hours on paperwork for every 1 hour of patient care. Burnout is epidemic, with 44% of physicians experiencing burnout and leaving practice at alarming rates.

**Current Solution:** Hiring more staff, reducing patient load (reducing income), or leaving medicine entirely.

**Cost of Pain:** Physician burnout costs healthcare system $4.6 billion annually; average 1,800 patients per year lose their doctor due to burnout; quality of care suffers.

**Your Solution:** DPC eliminates insurance billing entirely. Simple monthly membership model requires minimal admin staff. Physicians can practice medicine, not paperwork.

**Pricing:** DPC model allows sustainable practice with 600-800 patients instead of 2,500+, improving physician wellbeing and patient care quality.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Does your current doctor seem rushed, burned out, or able to give you their full attention?"
- "Have you had to change doctors because yours left practice or seemed overwhelmed?"
- "What would it mean to you to have a doctor who isn't burned out and actually enjoys practicing medicine?"

### 14. Small Business and Freelancer Healthcare Gap
**Pain:** Entrepreneurs, freelancers, and small business employees often can't afford quality health insurance. They fall into the coverage gap - too much income for Medicaid, can't afford marketplace plans, and don't have employer coverage.

**Current Solution:** Going uninsured, buying minimal catastrophic coverage, or joining spouse's expensive plan.

**Cost of Pain:** 30 million Americans uninsured or underinsured; medical bankruptcy affects 500,000+ annually; delayed care leading to emergencies.

**Your Solution:** DPC provides affordable, accessible primary care for $600-$1,200/year - less than one month of traditional insurance premiums. Pair with catastrophic plan for complete coverage.

**Pricing:** $50-$100/month makes quality primary care accessible to independent workers and small business employees.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "If you're self-employed or work for a small business, how do you handle healthcare?"
- "What percentage of your income goes to health insurance premiums?"
- "Have you considered going without insurance because of the cost?"

### 15. Healthcare Transparency and Trust Deficit
**Pain:** Patients don't trust the healthcare system. Hidden costs, surprise billing, rushed care, and feeling like a number have eroded the doctor-patient relationship.

**Current Solution:** Seeking alternative medicine, delaying care, or accepting poor service as "just how healthcare is."

**Cost of Pain:** Healthcare distrust leads to 20% lower treatment adherence; patients avoid preventive care; chronic conditions worsen due to system avoidance.

**Your Solution:** DPC restores the doctor-patient relationship through transparency, time, access, and trust. Patients know exactly what they're paying for and receive personalized attention.

**Pricing:** Transparent monthly membership with no hidden costs rebuilds trust through honest, upfront business relationship.

**Discovery Questions:**
- "Do you trust the current healthcare system to have your best interests at heart?"
- "When you interact with the healthcare system, do you feel like a valued patient or a billing code?"
- "What would it take to restore your trust in your primary care doctor?"

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"But I already have insurance - why would I pay extra for DPC?"**
> "Your insurance doesn't actually give you access to healthcare - it gives you access to paying for healthcare. You're paying $300-$800/month in premiums plus copays and deductibles just to have the privilege of paying more when you're sick. DPC is different - you pay $60-$100/month and actually GET healthcare. Unlimited visits, same-day appointments, direct doctor access. Plus we save you money on medications and labs that your insurance often doesn't cover well anyway. Most patients with HDHPs save $1,000-$3,000/year even after the DPC membership."

**"What if I need a specialist or hospital care?"**
> "DPC handles 80-90% of your healthcare needs right in our office. For the 10-20% that requires specialists or hospitals, that's exactly what insurance is for. We actually recommend pairing DPC with a high-deductible catastrophic plan. You get the best of both worlds - unlimited, accessible primary care plus protection against major medical expenses. And unlike traditional practices, we help coordinate your specialist care and find you the best cash prices for those services."

**"This seems too good to be true - what's the catch?"**
> "No catch - just a different business model. Traditional practices make money by billing insurance for every little thing. That requires massive staff, complex billing systems, and seeing 30+ patients a day. We eliminated all that overhead by removing insurance from the equation. Our doctors see 600-800 patients instead of 2,500, which means we can give you time and access insurance practices can't afford to provide. We've been in business for X years with X happy patients. The model works because it's simple and aligned with patient interests, not insurance company profits."

**"I've never heard of this - is it legitimate?"**
> "Direct Primary Care is the fastest-growing model in primary care, with over 2,000 practices nationwide and growing 20% annually. It's recognized by the American Academy of Family Physicians and even has specific IRS provisions allowing HSA funds to pay for memberships. The model has been around for over a decade and has proven outcomes data showing better patient satisfaction, reduced hospitalizations, and lower total healthcare costs. We can connect you with existing patients who'll share their experience, or you can read the growing body of research on DPC effectiveness."

**"What if I join and don't like it?"**
> "We offer a 30-day satisfaction guarantee - if you're not completely satisfied in your first month, we'll refund your membership fee, no questions asked. After that, you can cancel anytime with 30 days notice. We're confident you'll stay because once patients experience having a doctor who actually has time for them, they never want to go back. But we want you to feel zero risk in trying this. Most of our patients come from referrals of other happy patients - that's our business model."
