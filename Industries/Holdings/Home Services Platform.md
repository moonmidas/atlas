# Home Services Platform

**Category:** Holding Companies  
**Sub-Category:** Multi-Trade Residential and Light Commercial Services Platform  
**Entry Cost:** $750K-$2.5M  
**Timeline:** 18-36 months  
**Revenue Model:** Acquire owner-led operators, centralize overhead, improve pricing and lead conversion, and compound EBITDA through tuck-ins

---

## 🎯 The Opportunity

A home services platform acquires profitable local HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, and related field service companies, then professionalizes sales, dispatch, marketing, pricing, and back office across the group. The model works because many targets are still founder-run, operationally under-instrumented, and priced at lower middle-market multiples.

The best platforms focus on one region first, build density by trade and geography, and create a shared operating system that increases technician productivity, call booking rates, recurring maintenance revenue, and eventual exit value. This is one of the clearest paths from small-business acquisition to institutional-quality platform economics.

**Market Size:** $500B+ North American home and property services market  
**Target:** $1M-$8M revenue HVAC, plumbing, electrical, garage door, pest, roofing, and restoration operators  
**Examples:** Neighborly, Authority Brands, Heartland Home Services, regional PE-backed trade platforms

---

## 👤 Buyer Profile

**Primary Buyer:** Founder/CEO or searcher building the platform; sellers are retirement-age owner-operators  
**Secondary:** GM candidates, lenders, field managers, dispatch leaders, office managers  
**Decision Maker:** Platform founder plus debt/equity partners; target-side seller decides deal acceptance

**Budget:** $750K-$2.5M for first acquisition and working capital; larger follow-ons after proof points  
**Pain Urgency:** High because seller retirements, labor shortages, and fragmented competition create a narrow consolidation window

## 🔴 Top 15 Pain Points

### 1. Scheduling and dispatch are still manual ⭐ #1 PAIN
**Pain:** Many operators route techs from whiteboards, text messages, and tribal knowledge.  
**Current Solution:** Dispatcher experience and end-of-day phone coordination.  
**Cost of Pain:** 10-20% lost capacity, missed calls, overtime, and slower response times.  
**Your Solution:** Standardized field service software, route rules, and live capacity dashboards.  
**Pricing:** $15K-$40K implementation per company plus platform license costs.

**Discovery Questions:**
- How are same-day jobs assigned right now?
- What percentage of the day is lost to windshield time or schedule gaps?

---

### 2. Missed calls leak high-intent demand
**Pain:** After-hours and overflow calls go unanswered when office staff are stretched.  
**Current Solution:** Voicemail, owner cell phone, or limited call center coverage.  
**Cost of Pain:** 5-15 booked jobs per week lost per branch.  
**Your Solution:** Centralized call handling, QA scripts, and after-hours booking.  
**Pricing:** $3K-$8K monthly centralized call center support.

**Discovery Questions:**
- How many calls go unanswered during peak weeks?
- What is the average revenue per booked service call?

---

### 3. Pricing is based on habit, not data
**Pain:** Legacy owners often undercharge relative to local demand and competitor benchmarks.  
**Current Solution:** Technician discretion or old flat-rate books.  
**Cost of Pain:** 300-800 bps margin left on the table.  
**Your Solution:** SKU-level repricing, service menu design, and conversion tracking.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$30K pricing project plus ongoing margin management.

**Discovery Questions:**
- When was your last full price-card reset?
- Which job types create the most callback but the least margin?

---

### 4. Maintenance memberships are underbuilt
**Pain:** One-off jobs dominate revenue and create seasonality.  
**Current Solution:** Informal reminder calls and ad hoc service agreements.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower retention, lower LTV, and volatile scheduling.  
**Your Solution:** Recurring membership programs with automated renewals and upsell motion.  
**Pricing:** $5K-$20K launch plus CRM automation tools.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What percentage of customers are on recurring plans today?
- How much off-season revenue would stabilize the branch?

---

### 5. Technician performance is uneven
**Pain:** Revenue per truck varies materially by branch and technician.  
**Current Solution:** Ride-alongs and informal coaching.  
**Cost of Pain:** Low average ticket, more callbacks, and customer inconsistency.  
**Your Solution:** Scorecards, sales process training, and branch manager coaching.  
**Pricing:** $1K-$3K per tech annually in training and QA.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What is the spread between top and bottom technicians by average ticket?
- Where do callbacks and low close rates cluster?

---

### 6. Marketing spend is scattered across vendors
**Pain:** Each branch buys SEO, PPC, mailers, or local sponsorships without attribution.  
**Current Solution:** Multiple local agencies and gut-feel budgeting.  
**Cost of Pain:** High CAC and poor channel visibility.  
**Your Solution:** Central demand generation, call tracking, and branch-level CAC reporting.  
**Pricing:** $8K-$25K monthly platform marketing management.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which channels actually produce booked revenue today?
- How do you compare lead quality across branches?

---

### 7. Back office headcount is duplicated across companies
**Pain:** AP, payroll, HR, and bookkeeping are repeated in every branch.  
**Current Solution:** Separate admins and local accountants.  
**Cost of Pain:** 3-8% of revenue in avoidable overhead.  
**Your Solution:** Shared service center and standardized closing calendar.  
**Pricing:** $60K-$180K setup plus salary savings captured at platform level.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which admin roles are fully duplicated today?
- How long does it take to close monthly financials by branch?

---

### 8. Inventory and truck stock are poorly controlled
**Pain:** Trucks carry the wrong parts while warehouses overbuy slow-moving inventory.  
**Current Solution:** Technician preference and branch manager ordering.  
**Cost of Pain:** Working capital drag, return trips, and stockouts.  
**Your Solution:** Truck stock standards, purchasing controls, and vendor consolidation.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$25K process redesign plus procurement savings.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which parts create the most repeat trips?
- How much dead inventory is sitting on shelves today?

---

### 9. Recruiting is owner-dependent
**Pain:** Skilled trade hiring depends on local reputation and word of mouth.  
**Current Solution:** Craigslist, Indeed, and technician referrals.  
**Cost of Pain:** Open trucks and stalled growth.  
**Your Solution:** Central recruiting engine, apprenticeship pathways, and employment brand.  
**Pricing:** $4K-$12K monthly plus recruiter or agency support.

**Discovery Questions:**
- How many open technician seats have been vacant more than 60 days?
- What is your current cost per hire?

---

### 10. Branch reporting is too slow for management action
**Pain:** Operators often see performance weeks after the fact.  
**Current Solution:** Monthly P&Ls and spreadsheet rollups.  
**Cost of Pain:** Slow intervention on conversion, labor, and pricing issues.  
**Your Solution:** Daily dashboards on bookings, average ticket, callbacks, and membership sales.  
**Pricing:** $15K-$35K BI setup plus monthly analytics support.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What metrics do branch leaders review daily versus monthly?
- Where do you lose time waiting on financial clarity?

---

### 11. Brand architecture is inconsistent
**Pain:** Some acquired brands should stay local while others benefit from shared identity.  
**Current Solution:** Keep everything unchanged or rebrand too early.  
**Cost of Pain:** Confused marketing spend and diluted reputation.  
**Your Solution:** Holdco brand strategy by branch maturity, market density, and trade.  
**Pricing:** $20K-$60K brand architecture and migration plan.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which brands have durable local goodwill worth preserving?
- Where would a unified market-facing brand improve efficiency?

---

### 12. Commercial accounts are underdeveloped
**Pain:** Many residential businesses have no disciplined commercial sales motion.  
**Current Solution:** Take inbound property manager work opportunistically.  
**Cost of Pain:** Lower average contract size and more seasonal revenue swings.  
**Your Solution:** Build light commercial account management and preventive maintenance offers.  
**Pricing:** $10K-$30K sales buildout plus rep compensation.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What percentage of revenue comes from recurring commercial contracts?
- Which verticals fit your fleet and technician mix best?

---

### 13. Insurance and fleet spend are not leveraged
**Pain:** Each branch buys its own vehicles, fuel cards, and insurance programs.  
**Current Solution:** Local broker relationships and one-off procurement.  
**Cost of Pain:** 10-20% excess spend on insurable and fleet categories.  
**Your Solution:** Platform procurement and group policy negotiation.  
**Pricing:** Internal initiative with $50K-$150K annual savings potential per platform.

**Discovery Questions:**
- How fragmented are your fleet and insurance vendors?
- What categories have never been bid at a platform level?

---

### 14. Founder transitions are messy
**Pain:** Seller relationships, employee trust, and customer confidence can break after close.  
**Current Solution:** Minimal integration planning and short transition periods.  
**Cost of Pain:** Revenue dips, technician turnover, and lost seller goodwill.  
**Your Solution:** 100-day integration playbook with retention incentives and local communication plan.  
**Pricing:** Included in deal execution; often tied to earnout design.

**Discovery Questions:**
- Which customer or employee relationships sit entirely with the founder?
- What does success look like 90 days after close?

---

### 15. Exit value is limited without true platform discipline
**Pain:** A collection of branches is not the same as an institutional platform.  
**Current Solution:** Roll up revenue and hope buyers pay up.  
**Cost of Pain:** 2-4 turns of EBITDA left unrealized at exit.  
**Your Solution:** Build governance, repeatable integration, dense geography, and KPI credibility.  
**Pricing:** Platform-level operating investment of $150K-$500K annually.

**Discovery Questions:**
- What would an institutional buyer need to see to underwrite a higher multiple?
- Are your branches managed as assets or as a real operating system?

## 🎯 Positioning Strategy

### The Hook
"Buy great local trade businesses, professionalize the machine behind them, and turn fragmented service companies into one dense regional platform."

### Authority Builders
1. Home services roll-up scorecard by trade and market density
2. Case study on membership revenue uplift after acquisition
3. Branch dashboard sample for dispatch, average ticket, and callback rates
4. Seller transition guide for founder-led trade businesses

### Positioning Angles
- **Density Wins:** Geographic clustering improves marketing, routing, recruiting, and procurement.
- **Operational Lift:** Most value comes from better execution, not financial engineering alone.
- **Retirement Wave:** Seller urgency creates a repeatable sourcing tailwind.

### Differentiators
- Practical field-service operating playbook rather than purely financial roll-up thesis
- Balanced local-brand versus platform-brand integration strategy
- Strong recurring revenue expansion through memberships and commercial maintenance

## 🚨 Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Owner-dependent companies where the seller still books jobs, dispatches, and sells major installs
- ❌ Businesses with weak reviews or unresolved licensing and permitting issues
- ❌ Markets with no route density path or highly seasonal single-service concentration
- ❌ Companies with poor job costing and no credible financial cleanup path
- ❌ Teams with chronic technician churn or toxic branch culture

**Best Prospects:**
- Founder-ready sellers with strong local reputation and second-line field leaders
- Branches above $2M revenue with clear pricing and dispatch upside
- Markets where tuck-ins can build route density within 90 minutes of existing branches

## 💬 Objection Handling

**"These businesses are too people-dependent to scale."**
> "They are people-intensive, but that is exactly why better recruiting, training, dispatch, and scorecards create outsized lift once standardized across multiple branches."

**"We will overpay if we buy quality operators."**
> "You only overpay if you cannot underwrite operational improvement. The right platform sees value where smaller buyers only see historical EBITDA."

**"Integration will kill the local culture that made the business work."**
> "A good platform does not erase the local culture. It removes admin friction and gives the branch better tools while keeping customer trust intact."

**"Home services are too exposed to the housing cycle."**
> "Replacement, repair, and preventive maintenance are more resilient than new construction. The model gets stronger when you diversify trades, memberships, and light commercial work."

**"Technician shortages make this impossible."**
> "Labor is the constraint, but scale actually helps. Better recruiting infrastructure, training paths, and cleaner scheduling make the platform a better employer than independents."

## 🚀 Validation Path

**Month 1:** Interview 20 owners, field managers, and industry lenders in one target region.  
**Month 2:** Build trade-specific sourcing list, lender relationships, and acquisition criteria.  
**Month 3:** LOI one founder-led operator and diligence dispatch, pricing, call handling, and accounting maturity.  
**Month 4-6:** Close first deal, install KPI dashboards, launch call center coverage, and reset pricing where obvious.  
**Month 6-12:** Add memberships, improve recruiting, and pursue 1-2 tuck-ins within the same service radius.
